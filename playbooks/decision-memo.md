# Decision Memos: Make the Governing Tradeoff Decidable

Title rationale: The title states that a memo succeeds by exposing the tradeoff an accountable human must decide.

## Lead Judgment

A decision memo should lead with the recommended judgment and the mechanism that makes one option preferable, then preserve the strongest alternative and unresolved evidence.

## Controlling Object

Whether the expected benefit of a choice is sufficient to accept its controlling cost, risk, or loss of reversibility.

## Use a Memo When Reconstruction Would Hide the Tradeoff

Use a decision memo when a choice has meaningful tradeoffs, dependencies, or consequences.

## The Memo Must Expose the Causal Wager

- Name the decision.
- Gather source context.
- Identify options.
- Compare tradeoffs.
- Surface risks and missing information.
- Recommend a next action.
- Record the decision and review date.

Decision question: Does the recommended option reduce the controlling risk enough to justify its cost and loss of reversibility?

## Accountable Leadership Retains the Decision

Every decision memo should be reviewed by the accountable leader before it becomes an operating commitment.

## The Memo Is Complete When the Tradeoff Is Decidable

A decision memo is complete when the accountable human can approve, reject, defer, or request more information without reconstructing the whole context from scratch.
