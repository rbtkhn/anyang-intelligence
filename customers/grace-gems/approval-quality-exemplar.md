# Grace Gems Approval Exemplar

This worked example shows what meaningful owner approval looks like when a listing recommendation includes trust-sensitive changes.

## Context

- a listing-review turn recommended revise-before-promotion
- the draft change touches packaging language and first-paragraph clarity
- the owner must decide whether the listing promise is still accurate

## Approval Receipt

```text
Approval Receipt

Lane: Grace Gems
Workflow surface: owner listing approval
Date: 2026-07-08

Approval decision under review:
Whether revised listing language may replace the current storefront language and whether promotion should proceed.

Approver role:
Owner

Authority actually held:
- listing publication authority
- product-promise authority
- pricing and promotion authority

What the approver reviewed:
- the buyer-confusion summary
- the trust-risk note
- the proposed revised copy
- the recommendation to delay promotion until the promise language is fixed

Evidence shown to the approver:
- repeated buyer-question pattern
- current listing copy
- proposed revised copy
- note about inconsistent packaging detail

Open uncertainties still visible at approval time:
- whether future batches can support the same packaging description consistently

Decision outcome:
- approved with changes

Changes required before use:
- remove the packaging phrase entirely rather than soften it
- keep the listing unpublished for promotion until the next owner review

What remains not approved:
- any promotion push tied to the old promise language

Meaningful-approval assessment:
- strong

Why the approval was meaningful or symbolic:
The owner reviewed the actual trust-sensitive risk, changed the customer-facing language directly, and limited the scope of approval.

Next required follow-up:
- update the listing draft
- preserve the decision in the owner review record or decision log
- revisit promotion after the revised listing is live
```

## Why This Approval Matters

The approval is meaningful because it governs a real customer-promise boundary, not just the style of the copy.
