# Singularity Science Executive OS Install

Singularity Science is an internal Anyang Intelligence research lane for singularity-relevant source analysis.

It is not a public content channel, customer deliverable, or autonomous research authority. The Executive OS role is to preserve source discipline, route implications into the right operating lanes, and prevent transcript material from becoming public claims without human review.

## Context Map

| Field | Current state |
| --- | --- |
| Operating context | Internal singularity research project |
| Primary source lanes | Innermost Loop and Moonshots |
| Core archive | [archive/](archive/) |
| Primary cadence | Event-driven when transcripts are captured; weekly or ad hoc review when research signals accumulate |
| Main output | Source notes, transcript analyses, research ledger rows, product implications, governance warnings, primitive candidates |
| Human authority | Anyang Intelligence operator retains final authority over source use, public claims, cross-customer routing, and preservation decisions |

## Executive Memory Objects

Singularity Science should preserve:

- Source metadata.
- Transcript locations.
- Rights and attribution notes.
- Analysis files.
- Singularity-relevant research signals.
- Hype versus durable-signal judgments.
- Customer lane relevance.
- Governance concerns.
- Reusable primitive candidates.
- Watch items.

Do not treat raw transcript text as reusable memory outside the archive.

## Decision System

The Executive OS prepares these decisions:

- Should a transcript be captured, summarized, or ignored?
- Does the source create a singularity-relevant signal worth tracking?
- Which customer lanes might be affected?
- Is the signal hype, durable, or uncertain?
- Does the signal imply a skill, checklist, loop, template, validator, or governance update?
- Does any implication require membrane review before transfer?

The system may recommend. Human authority decides whether to preserve, publish, route, or generalize.

## Operating Review Cadence

Review cadence:

- Event-driven when a new transcript or source arrives.
- Weekly when multiple singularity-relevant signals accumulate.
- Before routing any source-derived implication into customer work.
- Before quoting or externally using any transcript material.

The review should ask:

- What changed in AI, agency, cognition, media, science, markets, governance, or human formation?
- What is hype versus durable signal?
- What customer lane might this affect?
- What governance boundary is stressed?
- What primitive candidate, if any, should be tested?

## Risk and Governance Boundary

Human authority remains required for:

- Public claims.
- Long quotes or transcript reuse.
- Rights and attribution decisions.
- Customer-facing use.
- Cross-customer transfer.
- Any implication involving children, money, legal/tax/compliance, publication, identity, contractor work, or private customer context.

Governance risks:

- Transcript material is treated as public content.
- Source claims become Anyang Intelligence claims.
- A research trend is overgeneralized into product doctrine.
- A source-specific insight is moved into a customer lane without membrane review.
- Rights or attribution uncertainty is hidden.

The strictest applicable membrane wins.

## 30-Day Installation Plan

First 30 days:

1. Keep [archive/](archive/) as the canonical storage surface for transcripts, source notes, analyses, and ledgers.
2. Run one Innermost Loop source through the source-note and analysis templates.
3. Add one row to the Innermost Loop research ledger.
4. Test whether the analysis produces a useful product implication, governance warning, or primitive candidate.
5. Route only synthesized implications into other repo surfaces.
6. Do not publish or customer-route transcript text without human review.

## Success Criteria

Singularity Science is working when:

- Transcripts and source notes are findable.
- Analysis separates source metadata from Anyang Intelligence synthesis.
- Research signals are tracked over time.
- Customer relevance is explicit but membrane-bounded.
- Governance concerns are visible before use.
- Primitive candidates are tested before becoming doctrine.
- Human authority remains intact for public use, routing, and preservation decisions.
