# Singularity Science Customer Impact Map

This map is the first 30-day operating artifact for the Singularity Science lane.

Its purpose is to show how singularity-relevant research signals may inform each Anyang Intelligence lane without creating customer obligations, leaking transcript context, or bypassing human authority.

## Operating Rule

Singularity Science is advisory.

It may surface research signals, governance warnings, and primitive candidates. It may not directly change customer commitments, publish source-derived claims, or move transcript bulk across membranes.

## Cross-Lane Patterns

The first repeated implications across lanes are:

- AI-assisted output needs stronger quality gates, not weaker ones.
- Faster generation increases authority-leak risk unless approval checkpoints stay explicit.
- Evidence discipline becomes more important as research and product claims move faster.
- Shared primitives should usually be checklists, validators, review questions, and draft queues.
- Child, money, property, donor, rights, and public-claim contexts need stricter routing than general productivity contexts.

## What Stays Inside Singularity Science

The following should remain inside the research lane unless a human explicitly reviews and translates them:

- Raw transcript text and long quotes.
- Source-specific hype cycles, personalities, and rhetorical framing.
- Unverified performance claims about models, tools, or companies.
- Speculative timelines presented as operating certainty.
- Source-specific passages about children, money, legal/compliance matters, safety, or rights.
- Any interpretation that has not yet been rewritten as a generic primitive, checklist, question, or guarded memo.
