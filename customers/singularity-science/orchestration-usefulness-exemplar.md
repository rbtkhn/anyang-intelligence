# Singularity Science Turn Exemplar

This worked example shows what a good research-lane turn looks like when one interaction behaves like a small workflow rather than only a synthesis draft.

## Input Context

Signal:

- An Innermost Loop analysis memo identifies a repeated pattern: orchestration quality is becoming a stronger product differentiator than raw model rank.

Constraints:

- keep transcript bulk inside Singularity Science
- preserve source discipline
- route only a lane-safe implication
- create no customer obligation directly

## Orchestration Steps Performed Inside The Turn

1. Review the source memo and isolate the transferable research judgment from source-specific rhetoric.
2. Compare the signal against existing primitives and identify that the gap is not a new market claim but a workflow-evaluation need.
3. Prepare a lane-safe primitive candidate: a turn-receipt structure that can be reused across customer lanes.
4. Record what evidence supports preservation and what still requires membrane judgment before any lane-specific adoption.
5. Stop before creating any lane obligation or rewriting customer-lane doctrine.

## Produced Artifacts Or Decisions

- primitive candidate: reusable turn receipt
- routing judgment: safe to preserve centrally inside Singularity Science
- next-step recommendation: test the primitive against one real lane workflow before broader adoption

## Approval Stop Point

Human approval is still required to:

- treat the primitive as normal operating doctrine in a customer lane
- route any source-derived claim into lane policy
- decide which lane should test the primitive first
