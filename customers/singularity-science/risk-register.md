# Singularity Science Risk Register

This register tracks the main operating and governance risks for the Singularity Science research lane.

Review cadence: weekly when active source flow is high, otherwise event-driven when a new source, primitive, or routing decision creates pressure.

| Risk | Consequence | Owner | Current posture | Next review |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Transcript material is routed outside the archive without membrane review | Source-specific text could leak into customer or public artifacts and create rights or attribution failures | Anyang Intelligence operator | Hold transcript bulk inside `archive/`; route only synthesized implications | Next source-routing review |
| Source claims are mistaken for Anyang Intelligence claims | Research hype could become product doctrine or customer guidance too early | Anyang Intelligence operator | Require analysis and primitive translation before transfer | Weekly research review |
| Primitive candidates accumulate without receiving-lane testing | Research becomes concept-heavy and workflow-light | Anyang Intelligence operator | Preserve candidates in primitives and customer-impact surfaces | Next monthly research review |
| Cross-lane relevance is named without enough authority clarity | Advisory insights could be mistaken for obligations | Anyang Intelligence operator | Keep customer-impact routing advisory and membrane-bounded | Before any lane transfer |
| Rights or attribution uncertainty is buried during fast intake | Future reuse becomes unsafe or unclear | Anyang Intelligence operator | Keep source metadata, rights notes, and capture method explicit in source notes and transcripts | Each new source intake |
