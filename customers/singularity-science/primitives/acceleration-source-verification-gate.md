# Acceleration-Source Verification Gate

Use this gate when a Moonshots-style source compresses many bold claims, fast timelines, benchmark shorthand, policy shifts, or infrastructure theses into a persuasive acceleration narrative.

This primitive exists because Moonshots often produces real product intelligence and real rhetoric risk at the same time.

## Use When

Use this gate before routing a Moonshots seam into a primitive, lane-safe question, or customer-impact implication when:

- several claims depend on unstable company, benchmark, policy, pricing, or timeline facts
- the source sounds unusually authoritative because it bundles many signals into one worldview
- the receiving lane is high-governance, high-trust, or claim-sensitive
- the operator feels the seam is strategically useful but not yet safe to treat as operating fact

## Core Questions

1. What is the exact seam being extracted?
2. Which claim inside that seam is the real reason it feels important?
3. Is that claim primary-source verified, conversational shorthand, or still unknown?
4. Would the receiving lane need the factual claim, or only the review question it suggests?
5. Can the seam be translated into a lane-safe checklist or question without importing the claim itself?
6. What stays worldview-only even if the seam is useful?

## Decision Rule

Use one outcome only:

- `preserve-now`
  - the seam yields a reusable primitive or routing question even if the strongest factual claim later softens
- `verify-first`
  - the seam's value depends on unstable claims and should not be routed yet
- `route-as-question`
  - the seam can safely become a lane review question without asserting the underlying claim
- `keep-worldview-only`
  - the seam is stimulating but does not yet justify an operating artifact

## Standard Routing Packet

When the outcome is `route-as-question`, capture:

```text
Source episode:
Seam:
Transferable question or checklist:
Receiving lane:
Membrane classification:
Human authority required:
Evidence still needed:
What stays inside Singularity Science:
```

## Recommended First Uses

- Moonshots episodes mixing frontier-model policy, robotics cost curves, orbital-compute theses, or fusion/infrastructure claims
- consciousness-adjacent interpretability claims
- legal or institutional adaptation claims that sound directionally right but are still evidence-thin

## Why This Primitive Won First Pass

This primitive won the first Moonshots preservation pass because it:

- already recurs across at least 2 Moonshots episodes
- clearly applies across multiple receiving lanes
- is blocked mainly by review discipline rather than missing technical context

## Boundary

Do not use this gate to block everything. The point is to separate reusable operating value from acceleration rhetoric, not to turn Moonshots into a no-output archive.
