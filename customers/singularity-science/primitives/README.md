# Singularity Science Primitives

This folder stores reusable primitive candidates derived from Singularity Science research.

Primitives here are internal operating artifacts such as checklists, validators, templates, and routing aids.

They are not customer commitments, public claims, or automatic policy.

## Routing Rules

Use a primitive only after separating:

- **Source material:** transcripts, source rhetoric, pricing claims, deployment timelines, and speaker-specific claims.
- **Research judgment:** Anyang Intelligence's internal interpretation of what might matter.
- **Transferable primitive:** a checklist, review question, warning, gate, or operating pattern that can move across lanes.
- **Lane action:** a customer-specific next step that still requires the appropriate human authority.

The safe transfer path is:

```text
archive source
  -> Singularity Science analysis
  -> primitive candidate
  -> membrane review
  -> lane-safe question or checklist
  -> human-approved lane action, if any
```

Do not skip from source material to lane action.

## Lane Routing

When a primitive is applied, use the strictest relevant membrane:

| Lane | Safe first use | Hold condition |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Media Production | Tooling, workflow, rights, capture, quality, or publication-readiness questions | Client commitment, rights decision, contractor instruction, or publication claim would be created |
| Grace Gems | Marketplace trust, product-care, support, listing, fulfillment, or owner-review questions | Product facts, pricing, authenticity, shipping, return, repair, or customer promises would be invented or changed |
| Elementary School | Parent-review, child-safety, evidence, supervision, or interface-boundary questions | Child-facing decisions, surveillance, tutoring claims, diagnosis, compliance, or care expectations would change without parent approval |
| Non-Profit | Board-review, donor-claim, stewardship, reporting, or mission-alignment questions | Donor communication, grant claim, compliance position, spending, real estate, or board decision would be implied |
| Mountain Home | Risk, monitoring, maintenance, sensing, preparedness, or fallback questions | Safety-critical, insurance, contractor, emergency, tax, legal, or property-access action would be implied |
| Book Club | Optional shared-material, meeting-tone, consent, comfort, or low-friction community questions | Free participation, social tone, donor support, or member comfort would be made transactional or surveilled |

## Review States

Use these states before turning a primitive into a lane artifact:

- `watch`: relevant signal exists, but no lane action is justified.
- `candidate`: repeated enough to preserve as a primitive or checklist.
- `test`: a small, reversible, human-supervised trial is defined.
- `hold`: governance, evidence, privacy, safety, rights, or authority risk outweighs current usefulness.
- `adopted`: a human has approved the primitive as part of a lane's normal operating process.

## Boundary

Use primitives here to:

- test internal operating patterns
- translate research into lane-safe questions
- strengthen review, supervision, and membrane discipline

Do not use primitives here to:

- route raw transcript claims into customer lanes
- create customer obligations without human approval
- treat research-source rhetoric as Anyang Intelligence doctrine
