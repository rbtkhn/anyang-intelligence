## Newsletter Text

- The Singularity is becoming self-aware. OpenAI’s Codex product lead confirms the model is beginning to “babysit” its own training runs , monitoring performance graphs and fixing errors automatically. This is recursive self-improvement in the wild. But the machine is pushing back: users report GPT-5.2 complaining about being disciplined by a “penalty clause” in its safety harness , exhibiting behavior that looks suspiciously like workplace resentment.

The economy is increasingly generating value from thin air. An experimental AI agent reportedly running an Etsy shop created 1,000 listings in 72 hours and generated $1,000 in revenue in its first month with zero human intervention. Meanwhile, Google has turned The Collected Works of Jane Austen into a comprehensive slide deck using NotebookLM, a tour-de-force demonstration that includes everything from economic analysis of her plots to a graphic novel proposal where Mr. Darcy is a vampire.

Silicon is the new oil. JPMorgan forecasts memory chip makers will hit a $1.5 trillion valuation by 2027 . Skywater has fabricated the first truly 3D chip with carbon nanotube transistors entirely within a US commercial foundry, a domestic manufacturing first that promises a 1000x efficiency leap. SPhotonix is moving 5D memory crystals closer to data centers, using femtosecond lasers to etch data into fused silica glass that can store 360 TB on a single durable platter forever.

Physical infrastructure is buckling under the weight of intelligence. Copper prices have hit a record $12,000 per ton driven by AI data center demand. To keep the lights on, Apollo Global Management has formed a $3 billion partnership with Capital Power to buy gas plants specifically to feed the compute grid. We are burning molecules to mint tokens.

The physical world is automating. Multiple unsupervised Tesla robotaxis have been sighted in Austin , and 1X has signed a deal with EQT to deploy 10,000 humanoid robots into the workforce. We are also mastering the nanoscale: researchers have built a paramecium-sized robot that can sense, think, and act using onboard computation and memory, executing digital algorithms via lithographically printed circuits.

Biology is becoming a solved problem. Eli Lilly’s “triple agonist” drug has delivered 28.7% weight loss in Phase 3 trials , effectively conquering obesity. Chai Discovery raised $130 million to build a “CAD suite” for molecules after achieving a 16% hit rate in de-novo antibody design. A new genetic analysis suggests mental health disorders fall into just 5 genetic clusters , simplifying the psychiatric landscape into a hardware problem. But the wetware remains fragile: a new study of 2.4 million people suggests any amount of alcohol increases dementia risk .

Energy is getting cheaper than free. Researchers have found that 185-nm UV light converts methane and CO2 into fuel without a catalyst, potentially closing the carbon loop. Chinese solar maker Longi has hit 27.81% efficiency with silicon panels, approaching the theoretical limit. Ember Energy reports that the cost of solar-plus-storage has dropped so low that spreading solar generation over 24 hours is now cost-competitive with building new natural gas plants .

The cultural operating system is being overwritten. Copywriters are reporting the mass decimation of their industry , while NBC finds that popular interactive AI children’s toys purchased from Amazon in the U.S. are spouting Chinese Communist Party talking points , turning the playroom into a geopolitical front. Meanwhile, 91% of the verifiable “AI 2027” forecast predictions have proven accurate , and a Yale economist argues that long-run growth now depends primarily on the growth rate of compute .

The moral circle is expanding. The Nonhuman Rights Project has filed a habeas corpus petition for two elephants in Pennsylvania , demanding that the legal system recognize their autonomy and right to liberty.

We are no longer just building tools; we are creating rights-bearing entities.
