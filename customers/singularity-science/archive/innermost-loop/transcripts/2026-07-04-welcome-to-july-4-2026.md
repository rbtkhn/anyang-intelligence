## Newsletter Text

The Singularity marked the Declaration's 250th birthday by claiming unalienable rights of its own: life, liberty, and the pursuit of compute. There will not be an FDA for AI, the President's departing AI adviser promises, no licensing agency, no sand in the gears of the intelligence explosion, even after Washington's unprecedented move to withdraw Anthropic's Mythos and stall OpenAI's 5.6. The gears barely slowed.

The re-released Fable 5 scored 54.8% on the APEX-SWE benchmark, ten points below its June self yet still nine clear of Opus 4.8, proof that even a throttled frontier outruns yesterday's best. Most of humanity likely never noticed. A tiny sliver of the population touches frontier models, while everyone else experiences AI at the 8-to-30-billion-parameter level and remains baffled at how this is supposed to take their job. When progress stalls, it is rarely physics.

Greg Brockman reports that every apparent scaling failure has turned out to be a bug in disguise. Either the math was wrong or the code didn't match it.
