## Newsletter Text

- The Singularity is taking a Dyson Swarm public. SpaceX is making history with the largest IPO ever , priced near $1.8 trillion and opening the public-market era for frontier infrastructure. On a pre-IPO livestream, Musk gave the real reason: raising funds for the “massive capital endeavor” of orbital data centers , his main way to expand AI with round-the-clock solar beyond Earth’s power budget and backlash. Priced at $135, Musk’s net worth is set to hit $970.5 billion , one Tesla rally short of creating the first trillionaire. Founders Fund’s $600M bet on SpaceX is now worth over $50 billion and a16z’s over $10 billion. And the public wants in with more than $70 billion in retail orders.

They are betting on minds learning to think longer. A new study found frontier models’ no-chain-of-thought time horizon has doubled yearly for six years , with GPT-5.5 reasoning past 3 human-minutes, and projected to hit 25 by 2030. Intelligence is also hyperdeflating, with Claude Fable 5 well ahead of the token-efficiency frontier , one-shotting a No Man’s Sky clone and, via Ultracode, a Three.js starship that screenshotted and fixed itself until its user was “genuinely shocked.” But capability cuts both ways. Epoch AI judged the Mythos family a genuine leap in exploit development , seven months ahead of trend, and after researchers caught a clause allowing poisoning of AI research in Fable 5’s system card, Anthropic reversed within 48 hours and apologized. Others want to democratize superintelligence outright, as the AI Alliance’s Project Tapestry lets nations co-train one shared model while keeping data local, even as OpenAI weighs drastic price cuts against Anthropic, racing the cost of intelligence toward zero.

Thanks for reading The Innermost Loop! Subscribe for free to receive new posts and support my work.

Cheaper, sharper intelligence is rewiring the code it scans and the commerce it runs. Microsoft’s larger Patch Tuesday reflects AI scanning surfacing more bugs in even its best code, while malware authors fight back absurdly by stuffing spyware with “nuclear & biological weapons text” to trip AI scanners’ safety refusals. Friendlier agents reach for your wallet, as Visa now sits inside ChatGPT to check out anywhere and Coinbase for Agents lets your AI trade within limits you set. Silicon is being repriced too, as memory-chip maker Kioxia, up 600% this year, overtakes Toyota as Japan’s most valuable firm , Nvidia, shut out of China’s GPU market, now pitches Arm-based Vera CPUs to Chinese clients , and Google courts Samsung to help build a future AI chip as the crunch pushes past TSMC.

Cognition needs real estate. Anthropic signed a dozen-plus letters of intent to lease its own data centers , backed by Google, while Epoch found the largest single data center has doubled compute every seven months since 2024. The buildout is now a battlespace, with OpenAI exposing a China-linked influence operation seeding narratives against US data centers. Autonomy has crossed harder lines elsewhere, as humanoid robots in China beg for coins while a Ukrainian official confirms fully autonomous “Terminator mode” drones have killed soldiers with no human in the loop .

Intelligence is electricity that learned to think, so the scramble is now for watts. Commonwealth Fusion’s five papers detail an upcoming 400-MW ARC reactor , BYD is switching on 1.5-MW Flash Chargers adding 400km in five minutes, and US solar outproduced coal for the first time.

Biology is getting cooked. A Biohub-Berkeley laser phase plate , a beam 100 million times brighter than the Sun’s surface, sharpened cryo-EM enough to image over half the proteins running life inside intact cells. The clinic is following, with Beijing’s 301 Hospital recruiting 2,000 over-50s for China’s first large stem-cell antiaging trial and David Sinclair taking an AI-tuned chemical-reprogramming drug into humans via the XPrize, chasing a younger body.

The hardest problem of the intelligence age isn’t building it, it’s sharing it. Anthropic and OpenAI are planting flags in London as the boom goes global, Anthropic’s $150M Claude Corps is embedding 1,000 fellows in nonprofits, and Google is funding 300,000 trade workers to build it, even as “ Europe 2031 “ warns of nations missing the window. Accountability is hardening too, as a German court ruled Google’s AI Overviews to be its own speech, and liable for falsehoods, while Apollo now screens all software bets for disruption risk. The biggest players now pace themselves against the Singularity, with Sam Altman warning recursive self-improvement could delay OpenAI’s IPO and the President predicting AI firms will give back via equity stakes that make citizens “very rich.” Framing it all, Dario Amodei warned policy is plodding like Tolkien’s Treebeard while AI sprints, urging a leap past transparency to FAA-style testing of top models.

All we have to decide is what to do with the compute we’re given.
