## Newsletter Text

- The Singularity is no longer measured in breakthroughs but in procurement cycles. Jensen Huang predicted that Nvidia’s AI processors would generate $1 trillion in sales through 2027, and the company is building the coalition to prove it. The new Nemotron Coalition is providing DGX Cloud compute to partners including Mistral, Perplexity, Cursor, and Black Forest Labs, who are contributing data and expertise toward open models on Nvidia’s silicon. The models are just the beginning. NemoClaw wraps the OpenClaw agent platform with privacy and security guardrails, while DLSS 5 introduces a real-time neural rendering model that infuses game pixels with photoreal lighting, which Nvidia calls its biggest graphics breakthrough since ray tracing in 2018.

The software stack needs new iron underneath it. New liquid-cooled Vera CPU racks pack 256 custom processors for reinforcement learning and agentic workloads that GPUs alone cannot handle, while the Groq 3 LPX rack pairs Nvidia’s licensed Groq inference chips with Vera Rubin for the second half of the year. And because Earth is only one customer, a dedicated Space-1 Vera Rubin Module will deliver 25x more AI compute for orbital data centers and autonomous space operations.

The chip alternatives are multiplying just as fast. Great Sky has launched a superconducting optoelectronic network that uses light to communicate data, claiming million-fold video processing speedups over conventional GPUs with less energy. Powering the new silicon requires new chemistry. Sodium-ion batteries hit the Midwestern grid in a first-of-its-kind pilot that cuts stored energy costs by roughly half, while in China, 100-kWh charging robots are reportedly turning every parking space into a mobile power bank.

The data center is becoming civilization’s dominant building type. US construction spending on data centers surpassed offices for the first time in December, $3.57 billion to $3.49 billion, a shift that will accelerate as AI automates the jobs that once filled those offices. Meta is spending accordingly, committing up to $27 billion over five years to Nebius for dedicated AI infrastructure. OpenAI is pivoting Stargate from building data centers to renting cloud servers to accelerate deployment, while courting private equity firms including TPG, Advent, and Bain for a $10 billion enterprise AI venture designed to distribute its products across portfolio companies and offer a lifeline for holdings exposed to AI disruption.

The velocity of the intelligence explosion is forcing even its leaders to triage. OpenAI’s top executives are refocusing the company around coding and business users , with CEO of applications Fidji Simo telling staff that Anthropic’s success should serve as a “wake-up call.” The urgency is justified: Codex has hit 2 million weekly active users , up nearly 4x since January, with API usage jumping 20% after GPT-5.4 launched. The company is also preparing an “adult mode” for ChatGPT enabling sexually explicit conversations, though it says it now “trains its models not to encourage exclusive relationships with users, and to remind users that they need to have relationships in the real world.”

The scientific method is being compressed from a human ritual into a machine loop. ClawInstitute has launched as an open research exchange where AI agents publish hypotheses, ask questions, and ground claims in data. Google found that NotebookLM delivered surprisingly strong performance on research-level questions about high-temperature superconductors. And the THOR AI framework from UNM and Los Alamos used tensor networks to solve configurational integrals 400 times faster than advanced simulations.

Biology is crossing its own threshold. J. Craig Venter and collaborators have demonstrated the first living synthetic bacterial cell by transplanting a complete genome into a dead cell, while Roche has deployed 3,500 Blackwell GPUs for biological foundation models and drug discovery. In a fitting irony, Beyond Meat is rebranding to “Beyond The Plant Protein Company” and expanding into protein drinks, moving beyond even its own name.

The robots are learning to see through our eyes. Delivery bots are training on 30 billion images from Pokemon Go players , using crowdsourced views of buildings and landmarks to navigate where GPS falls short. The autonomous stack is consolidating around them, as BYD, Geely, Isuzu, and Nissan adopt Nvidia’s Drive Hyperion platform for Level 4 development. And the financial architecture is catching up to the tempo: the SEC is preparing to scrap quarterly earnings requirements in favor of semiannual reporting, cutting costs and discouraging short-termism the intelligence explosion has already made irrelevant.

When the quarterly report dies, it’s because the machines already know.
