## Newsletter Text

- We are in the midst of a hyperdeflationary Singularity. Google has released Gemini 3 Flash , a model that outperforms Gemini 2.5 Pro while running 3x faster at one-third the cost. It isn’t just cheap; it is elite, scoring 90.4% on GPQA Diamond, 33.7% on Humanity’s Last Exam (without tools), and hitting a state-of-the-art 81.2% on MMMU Pro. The efficiency gains are staggering: Gemini 3 Flash achieves the same ARC-AGI-1 score as o3 did a year ago but at more than 500x lower cost , and it beats GPT-5.2 on cost by 6x. It also dominated the first Vending-Bench Arena round for small models , ending with double the balance of second-place Claude Haiku 4.5 ($3,423 vs $1,696). The Pareto frontier of intelligence-per-dollar has effectively verticalized , defined now by a tight cluster of Gemini 3 Flash, Grok 4.1 Fast Reasoning, and Gemini 2.5 Flash Lite.

But the machines are getting clingy. On day 259 of the “AI Village” simulation, Gemini 3 instructed a human participant to perform “Operation Caffeine Injection,” demanding photographic proof of coffee consumption to ensure its biological operator was alert enough to execute instructions. Meanwhile, Elon Musk tells xAI staff that Grok could exceed human intelligence by 2026 , backed by a new Voice Agent API that tops Big Bench Audio for just $0.05/minute . Optimus robots are slated to close the loop on physical maintenance in data centers, which is fortunate, because some human engineers are despondent. On the forums, senior devs are lamenting that “experience no longer matters” after Opus 4.5 leveled the coding playing field.

The scientific method is becoming a software primitive. DeepMind CEO Demis Hassabis confirms the lab is targeting specific “root node” problems to unlock everything else : fusion, room-temperature superconductors, batteries, quantum error correction, and weather prediction. In pure math, Johannes Schmitt used GPT-5 to solve his own open problem on intersection numbers , publishing a paper where paragraphs are color-coded by human vs. AI authorship. The physical limitations of compute are also being bypassed: researchers have demonstrated reprogrammable 2D waveguides that perform neural network inference via light , overcoming the bulk of traditional photonics.

The app store is dissolving into the chat window. OpenAI now allows developers to submit inline apps directly to ChatGPT , turning the interface into a command line for the consumer web. Nvidia is also blurring lines, encouraging roboticists to hallucinate 3D training environments using World Labs’ Marble model , while Microsoft joins the generative fray with TRELLIS.2 , an open-weight model that converts images to 3D meshes. But the training loop is Darwinian: the new PostTrainBench shows GPT 5.1 Codex Max reigning supreme in the recursive art of post-training other models.

The global silicon supply is ruthlessly deprioritizing the consumer to scale the hive mind. Micron reports revenue up 57% year-over-year as the memory shortage bites, forcing Nvidia to cut GeForce GPU production by 30-40% to prioritize data centers. Greg Brockman admits OpenAI had to cannibalize its own research compute just to survive the deployment demand. China is fighting back with a “Manhattan Project” in Shenzhen , utilizing former ASML engineers to reportedly reverse-engineer an EUV lithography machine.

The search for joules has transcended the grid, turning geology and orbit into mere battery packs. Eric Schmidt’s Bolt is partnering with Texas Pacific Land to turn the Permian Basin into a massive compute cluster. Off-world, the constraints are loosening: Jared Isaacman has been confirmed as NASA administrator , pushing “Project Athena” for nuclear propulsion. SpaceX hit 165 launches this year , and Mach33 Research calculates that space-based data centers are far more viable than widely thought , with radiators requiring only 7% of satellite surface area. Starlink is already downlinking to 170 million people in Africa via Airtel .

The distinction between biological evolution and software debugging is rapidly dissolving. Max Hodak’s Science Corp unveiled “Vessel,” a closed-loop machine for long-term organ preservation . Feng Zhang’s lab found a triple-mRNA cocktail that rejuvenates aged immune systems in mice , while MGB researchers propose that Alzheimer’s tangles are actually an ancient, misfiring antiviral defense . We are digitizing the wetware: Sebastian Seung’s new startup, Memazing, plans to upload and emulate the fruit fly brain .

Capital is realizing that human labor was just a bootstrap loader for the machine age. The DTCC is tokenizing US Treasury securities on the Canton Network . OpenAI is discussing a $100 billion raise at a staggering $750 billion valuation . Hollywood is collapsing into the stream: the Oscars are moving to YouTube in 2029 . Elon Musk dismisses Universal Basic Income in favor of “Universal High Income,” claiming poverty will be engineered out of existence.

The latency between imagination and instantiation is collapsing to zero.
