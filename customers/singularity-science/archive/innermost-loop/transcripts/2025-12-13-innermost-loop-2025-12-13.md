## Newsletter Text

- The clock speed of autonomy is accelerating. Epoch AI extrapolates that Gemini 3 Pro has achieved a SOTA autonomous time horizon of 4.9 hours , with GPT-5.2 following at 3.5 hours. Based on this trajectory, some analysts now estimate the doubling time for autonomous capability is down to just one month , a pace that renders annual roadmaps obsolete. To keep up, Zoom has deployed “Federated AI,” a swarm of small, open, and closed models that scored 48.1% on Humanity’s Last Exam, beating even the monolithic Gemini 3 Pro. The network is officially smarter than the node. Meanwhile, Google has updated Gemini 2.5 Flash with native audio , allowing agents to navigate complex voice workflows with human-like nuance.

Science is being containerized. The National Science Foundation has launched “Tech Labs,” a new class of independent research organizations designed to break technical bottlenecks that universities can’t touch. The DOE is backing this industrial approach with $320 million to build the “American Science and Security Platform,” an integrated discovery engine comprising an “American Science Cloud” and a “Transformational AI Models Consortium” explicitly designed to double the nation’s scientific productivity within a decade. The industrialization of scientific discovery with AI is already bearing fruit: Terry Tao confirms his collaborators and he have now solved Erdős problem #1026 using a hybrid of AlphaEvolve and deep literature search, turning the “lone genius” into a “human-in-the-loop.”

A silicon curtain is falling. The U.S. has launched a “Pax Silica Initiative” to secure the entire semiconductor stack, from minerals to logic, with allies like Japan and the UAE. China is countering with a $70 billion subsidy package to decouple its chip industry from the West.

Robotics is getting a world model. DeepMind demonstrates that Veo can now be used to evaluate robotic policies , effectively allowing robots to “dream” scenarios before executing them. In the physical world, Unitree has launched the first humanoid app store , commoditizing physical skills, while Tesla’s holiday update lets users ask Grok to handle FSD navigation .

Protein design has graduated from biology to nanotechnology. Researchers are using AI to create proteins that retain their structure above 150°C and withstand extreme physical forces, effectively inventing hardware-grade biochemistry that functions outside the narrow constraints of life.

The Singularity is backwards compatible. Hungarian researchers have enabled rats to play DOOM in VR using a complete motion-tracking setup. This is a primitive form of uplift: plugging a biological neural net directly into a digital game loop to expand its cognitive horizon.

The invisible hand is getting a firmware update. OpenAI’s Sam Altman confirms that enterprise sales are the priority for 2026 , while Mira Murati’s Thinking Machines releases Tinker to democratize fine-tuning. Even the payout mechanism is changing: YouTube now allows creators to cash out in stablecoins .

Education is being patched to match the new industrial reality. xAI has partnered with El Salvador to bring Grok-powered tutoring to every public school student , while UATX is building an Applied Engineering Lab next door to SpaceX to pipeline talent directly into the hard-tech industrial base.

The energy grid is prepping for ignition. Commonwealth Fusion Systems’ commercial demo site near Boston is 75% complete , signaling that net-energy fusion is moving from physics to construction.

Space is becoming a server rack. SpaceX is on track to have 10,000 Starlink satellites in orbit by February . Even Mars is getting wired: Blue Origin plans to deploy an AI data center to the Red Planet in 2028 .

The Solar System finally has a business model.
