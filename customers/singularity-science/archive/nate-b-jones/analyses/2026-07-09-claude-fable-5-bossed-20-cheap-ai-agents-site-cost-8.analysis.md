## Seam Analysis

### Seam: orchestration / verification topology

- Core claim: the real breakthrough is not that one model got trustworthy enough, but that verification topology now allows worker, checker, and boss roles to correct one another.
- Likely durable:
  - orchestration structure matters as much as raw model quality
  - reciprocal checking can reduce reliance on one agent's self-report
  - role specialization is a practical product surface
- Likely speculative:
  - the degree to which this exact topology generalizes cleanly to many other tasks
  - the implied ease with which nontechnical users can replicate it
- Verification burden: medium
- Primitive opportunity: candidate for a verification-topology review gate or stronger turn-usefulness instrumentation around checker design
- Lane-safe routing opportunity: not yet; preserve first as an internal primitive candidate
- Membrane risk: medium; the source could be over-read as proof that orchestration is solved
- ROI disposition: primitive-candidate

### Seam: standards / constitution enforcement

- Core claim: writing one mission-specific constitution and checking against it repeatedly can create better results than issuing scattered instructions round by round.
- Likely durable:
  - standard-setting once, then checking repeatedly, is a strong orchestration pattern
  - user-protective standards can outrank aesthetics or convenience
- Likely speculative:
  - the exact degree of quality gain in this case
  - how fully the constitution captured accessibility quality in practice
- Verification burden: medium
- Primitive opportunity: route into lane-specific review prompts about constitutions, non-negotiables, and recurring checks
- Lane-safe routing opportunity: yes
- Membrane risk: low-to-medium; the pattern is transferable if framed as a question rather than a claim
- ROI disposition: lane-test-ready
