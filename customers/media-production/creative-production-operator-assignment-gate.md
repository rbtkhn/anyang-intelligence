# Creative Production Operator Assignment Gate

Use this gate before assigning work to the outsourced Creative Production Operator.

The goal is to protect capacity, source truth, rights, client approval, and budget before a production task leaves the operator's hands.

This gate does not approve publication, delivery, spending, product claims, rights decisions, or client commitments. It only decides whether a task is ready to assign.

## Assignment Decision

Choose one:

- `Assign`: the task is clear, source-bound, budget-safe, and reviewable.
- `Revise assignment`: the task is useful but the brief, source notes, scope, or deliverable definition needs tightening.
- `Hold`: do not assign until source, approval, rights, capacity, budget, or governance inputs are clarified.
- `Reject`: the task is not worth assigning in its current form.

When in doubt between `Assign` and `Revise assignment`, choose `Revise assignment`.

When in doubt because authority, source, rights, or budget is missing, choose `Hold`.

## Required Before Assignment

Every CPO task must have:

- Client or lane: `Grace Gems` or `Predictive History`.
- Source signal: product, listing, customer question, trust asset need, episode, topic, transcript, outline, or operator-provided source.
- Approved or review-ready production brief.
- Asset job: what the asset should help the buyer, customer, viewer, or operator do.
- Deliverables: exact output types and quantity.
- Source notes: facts, claims, references, or episode/topic framing the contractor may use.
- Do-not-invent list.
- Rights or attribution notes.
- Budget/spend rule.
- Due date or review window.
- Reviewer.
- Quality gate expected before handoff.

If any item is missing, do not assign until it is filled or explicitly marked not applicable.

## Grace Gems Assignment Checks

Before assigning Grace Gems work, confirm:

- Product, listing, customer-support, trust, or promotion source is available.
- Product facts are source-bound.
- Owner approval needs are named.
- No product details, prices, dimensions, materials, gemstone identity, authenticity claims, shipping promises, repair/return promises, certificate/appraisal claims, rush-order commitments, discounts, or customer commitments are invented.
- Customer messages or support-derived inputs are translated into generic buyer-confusion patterns unless explicit reuse is approved.
- Output is useful for listing support, product clarity, customer support, social/WeChat selling, promotion, or trust architecture.

Default decision if claims are uncertain: `Hold`.

## Predictive History Assignment Checks

Before assigning Predictive History work, confirm:

- Episode, topic, transcript, outline, source note, or prediction/outcome frame is available.
- Asset supports judgment, evidence, prediction/expectation, outcome, causal forces, decision problem, or lesson learned.
- Historical claims, quotes, maps, images, likenesses, music, and source rights are marked for review when uncertain.
- Task does not ask for generic decorative history imagery.
- Output helps the viewer understand the episode or topic faster.

Default decision if source/rights are uncertain: `Hold`.

## Capacity And Budget Checks

Before assigning, confirm:

- The work fits the current CPO capacity assumption.
- The task is sized for the $500/month contractor allocation.
- Any paid tool, stock asset, subscription, additional contractor, distribution spend, or rush work is flagged.
- Human approval exists before any spend.
- The assignment does not assume a filled role, unlimited revisions, or same-day production unless explicitly approved.

If the task would consume too much capacity, reduce scope before assigning.

## Assignment Scope Sizing

Use these default sizes:

- `Small`: one brief, one concept set, one graphic draft, one caption set, or one review packet.
- `Medium`: one small asset package with 2-4 related outputs.
- `Large`: multi-format package, video draft, campaign set, or work requiring multiple review cycles.

Default to `Small` until the contractor's quality and speed are proven.

## Review Packet Requirement

The CPO must return work with:

- Files or links.
- Source notes used.
- Claims or rights uncertainties.
- What changed from the brief.
- What decision is needed.
- Recommended next action.
- Any reuse ideas.

Work without a review packet is not ready for quality review.

## Gate Output

Use this format:

```text

Client/lane:
Task:
Source checked:

Capacity/budget fit:

Human authority boundary:
```

## Human Authority Boundary

The CPO may produce drafts, concepts, source notes, edits, and review packets.

The CPO may not approve:

- publication
- delivery to client
- product, policy, authenticity, shipping, repair, return, certificate, pricing, discount, or customer-promise claims
- rights or licensing decisions
- paid tools or spend
- hiring or subcontracting
- final creative direction
- customer commitments

Humans retain authority over client commitments, publication, spending, rights, and external claims.
