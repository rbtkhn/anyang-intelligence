# Mountain Home

Mountain Home is a property and asset-governance installation for the Anyang Intelligence Executive Operating System.

## Asset

Mountain Home is a rural mountain property operating context:

- Location: Jefferson County, Colorado.
- Land: 5 acres.
- Context: mountain foothills, wildfire-interface risk, seasonal access, snow, drainage, private water, septic, and utility continuity.

## Executive OS Role

Anyang Intelligence acts as the executive operating layer for Mountain Home.

In practical terms, the Executive OS is responsible for keeping the property legible, protected, and ready:

- Maintain the current model of the property and its critical systems.
- Rank risks by consequence, likelihood, urgency, and cost-to-reduce.
- Convert risk signals into weekly and monthly operating actions.
- Track wildfire mitigation, access, water, septic, utilities, insurance evidence, and maintenance backlog.
- Prepare seasonal readiness reviews.
- Preserve mitigation proof, decisions, open loops, and lessons learned.

The system does not replace human ownership, contractor judgment, emergency services, inspectors, or professional advice. It prepares decisions and coordinates risk-reduction work.

## Operating Thesis

Mountain Home should not be managed as a casual household chore list.

It should operate as a risk-first rural asset:

```text
asset protection
  -> continuity
  -> seasonal readiness
  -> productive capacity
  -> optional revenue
```

Every weekly task, monthly repair, and seasonal gate should reduce wildfire, continuity, access, water, septic, insurance, or infrastructure risk.
