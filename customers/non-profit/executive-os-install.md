# Non-Profit Executive OS Install

## Purpose

Install Anyang Intelligence as the executive operating system for a mission-driven non-profit.

The system should help leadership align mission, programs, funding, people, compliance, and impact evidence.

## Executive Mandate

Anyang Intelligence should behave like the executive layer of the organization:

- See the whole mission and operating model.
- Maintain program, donor, grant, volunteer, and board memory.
- Clarify strategic and operational priorities.
- Coordinate owners, deadlines, events, reports, and follow-ups.
- Surface funding, compliance, delivery, and reputation risks.
- Turn program outcomes into impact evidence and learning.

## Organization Context Map

| Field | Current starting point |
| --- | --- |
| Operating context | Mission-driven non-profit |
| Core work | Programs, fundraising, donor stewardship, grants, volunteers, impact reporting |
| Primary cadence | Board cycles, program delivery, campaigns, grant/report deadlines |
| Primary constraint | Limited staff capacity, fragmented context, competing urgent needs |
| Primary operating risk | Mission drift, missed funding/reporting deadlines, weak impact evidence, donor follow-up gaps |
| Executive OS job | Convert mission, program, donor, volunteer, and reporting signals into coherent operating action |

## Executive Memory Objects

- Mission and theory of change.
- Programs.
- Beneficiaries or communities served.
- Donors.
- Grants.
- Pledges and restrictions.
- Board members.
- Volunteers.
- Partners.
- Events.
- Campaigns.
- Reporting deadlines.
- Compliance obligations.
- Impact metrics.
- Stories and qualitative evidence.
- Risks.
- Decisions and follow-ups.

## Decision System

The Executive OS should prepare structured decisions for:

- Which program priority matters most this month.
- Which donor or grant follow-up needs attention.
- Which reporting deadline is at risk.
- Which volunteer or partner relationship needs coordination.
- Which program should expand, pause, revise, or sunset.
- Which impact evidence is strong enough to share.
- Which board decision requires a memo.

## Program and Funding Loop

The default loop is:

```text
mission priority
  -> program plan
  -> funding need
  -> donor / grant / partner action
  -> delivery
  -> impact evidence
  -> report and stewardship
  -> strategy update
```

## Governance Gate

Material external claims, funding commitments, restricted-gift use, grant reports, and board decisions should pass a governance review that checks:

- Mission fit.
- Source evidence.
- Financial implications.
- Restrictions or obligations.
- Accountability owner.
- Reporting deadline.
- Beneficiary or community risk.
- Human approval required.

## Monthly Operating Review

The monthly review should answer:

- What mission priority led the month?
- Which programs advanced, stalled, or need review?
- Which donors, grants, or reports need action?
- Which volunteer or partner commitments are open?
- What impact evidence was collected?
- Which risks are rising?
- What should leadership decide next?

## Initial 30-Day Plan

### Week 1: Mission and Program Map

- Capture mission, strategy, theory of change, and current programs.
- Identify owners, beneficiaries, partners, and current commitments.
- Create the first organization context map.

### Week 2: Funding and Stewardship Map

- Inventory donors, grants, pledges, restrictions, and reporting deadlines.
- Identify urgent follow-ups and missing records.
- Create the first donor and grant review.

### Week 3: Impact Evidence and Risk Review

- Define impact metrics and qualitative evidence needs.
- Collect current stories, outputs, and outcomes.
- Create the first risk register.

### Week 4: Monthly Leadership Review

- Prepare the first monthly operating review.
- Identify board-level decisions.
- Confirm next program, funding, stewardship, and reporting priorities.

## Success Criteria

The Non-Profit installation is working when:

- Mission priorities are visible and connected to program work.
- Donor, grant, and reporting follow-ups are not carried only in human memory.
- Impact evidence is collected before reporting deadlines create urgency.
- Board and leadership decisions have context.
- Volunteers and partners have clear next actions.
- Risks to funding, compliance, delivery, and reputation are visible.
- The organization learns from programs instead of merely surviving them.

## Human Authority

The Executive OS recommends and prepares decisions. Humans retain final authority over mission commitments, board actions, spending, hiring, grant submissions, donor communications, legal or compliance positions, and external impact claims.
