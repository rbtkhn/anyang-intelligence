# Non-Profit Turn Exemplar

This worked example shows a stewardship review turn designed for leadership and board-safe preparation rather than autonomous action.

## Input Context

Stewardship signal:

- a grant report is due soon
- one impact claim in the draft relies on thin anecdotal evidence
- a related restricted-fund question is still unresolved

## Orchestration Steps Performed Inside The Turn

1. Review the draft reporting packet against evidence quality and restriction sensitivity.
2. Separate what is ready for normal stewardship from what requires stronger evidence or leadership review.
3. Reclassify the thin impact claim as `hold pending evidence`.
4. Prepare a revised next-step packet with one leadership question about the restricted-fund issue.
5. Stop before any donor-facing submission or compliance implication is made.

## Produced Artifacts Or Decisions

- revised reporting recommendation
- hold note on the weak impact claim
- leadership question on the restricted-fund issue
- board/professional escalation flag if the restriction remains unclear

## Approval Stop Point

Human approval remains required for:

- donor or grant submission
- restricted-fund interpretation
- board action
- compliance or external impact claims
