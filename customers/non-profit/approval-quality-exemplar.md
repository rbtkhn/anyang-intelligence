# Non-Profit Approval Exemplar

This worked example shows what meaningful leadership approval looks like when stewardship pressure and governance pressure overlap.

## Context

- a revised grant-reporting packet has been prepared
- one impact claim was held pending stronger evidence
- one restricted-fund question remains unresolved enough to require escalation if internal clarification fails

## Approval Receipt

```text
Approval Receipt

Lane: Non-Profit
Workflow surface: leadership stewardship approval
Date: 2026-07-08

Approval decision under review:
Whether the revised reporting packet is safe to continue preparing for submission.

Approver role:
Leadership

Authority actually held:
- packet review authority
- external-claim caution authority
- escalation authority for board or professional review

What the approver reviewed:
- revised reporting packet
- hold note on the weak impact claim
- restriction-sensitive question
- escalation recommendation

Evidence shown to the approver:
- current report draft
- evidence-strength concern on the held claim
- summary of the unresolved restricted-fund issue

Open uncertainties still visible at approval time:
- final interpretation of the restriction question
- whether stronger evidence will arrive before the deadline

Decision outcome:
- provisional / partial approval

Changes required before use:
- do not restore the held claim without stronger evidence
- escalate for professional or board review if the restriction is not resolved internally within the deadline window

What remains not approved:
- final donor or grant submission
- any claim that depends on the unresolved restriction

Meaningful-approval assessment:
- mixed

Why the approval was meaningful or symbolic:
Real review happened and the escalation path is clear, but unresolved governance pressure still limits how final the approval can be.

Next required follow-up:
- resolve the restriction issue
- confirm final packet contents
- obtain any needed board or professional signoff before submission
```

## Why This Approval Matters

This is a good example of partial but still meaningful approval. Leadership is not pretending the packet is final; it is preserving the boundary around what remains unresolved.
