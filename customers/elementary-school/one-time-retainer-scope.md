# One-Time Retainer Scope

This document defines what the **$1,000 one-time retainer** from Elementary School to Anyang Intelligence produces before delivery begins.

It is a hybrid scope document: internal enough to govern Anyang Intelligence execution, but clean enough to adapt into parent-facing language.

## Scope Decision

The primary one-time package is a **30-Day Personalized Learning Plan** for new students in a parent-led elementary learning context, including onboarding.

The retainer is a one-time service package. It is not customer pricing, a subscription hypothesis, or an ongoing support agreement. No customer-pricing model has been designed yet.

## Payment Record

- Payer: Elementary School.
- Payee: Anyang Intelligence.
- Amount: $1,000.
- Payment type: one-time retainer.
- Working classification: retainer/prepaid service obligation.
- Deliverable: 30-Day Personalized Learning Plan for new students, including onboarding.
- Pricing status: customer pricing not yet designed.
- Evidence needed: invoice, receipt, payment record, or other approved accounting evidence.
- Review needed: owner/bookkeeper/CPA review if final accounting or tax classification is needed.

## Purpose

The package should help a parent move from scattered new-student learning intentions to a usable first-month plan and onboarding path.

It should define:

- What the learner will try during the first 30 days.
- What onboarding steps should happen before or during the first week.
- How the parent will review and approve the plan.
- How Khan Academy Kids, the reading basket, and the student portfolio support the plan.
- What evidence will be saved.
- How the first month will turn into next-month decisions.

The goal is not to prove mastery or replace the parent. The goal is to make the first month of parent-led learning easier to see, guide, remember, and adjust.

## Primary Deliverable

The deliverable is a **30-Day Personalized Learning Plan**.

It should include:

- A learner context summary based on parent goals, learner interests, observed signals, and existing resources.
- A new-student onboarding sequence.
- A 30-day rhythm that balances reading, Khan Academy Kids, hands-on practice, projects, enrichment, breaks, and parent observation.
- A weekly plan structure for four weeks.
- A reading basket plan.
- A Khan Academy Kids use rule.
- A portfolio evidence plan.
- Weekly parent review prompts.
- A first monthly portfolio review shape.
- Parent decisions needed before or during the month.

The plan should be practical enough for the parent to use without turning the home learning environment into bureaucracy.

## Inputs Needed Before Delivery

Before delivery begins, collect or confirm:

- Parent goals for the first 30 days.
- Parent non-negotiable boundaries.
- Current learning resources and curriculum choices, if any.
- Learner interests, preferences, frustrations, and proud work.
- Student startup survey responses, if available.
- Khan Academy Kids parent-observed engagement notes, if available.
- Reading basket signals or current books, if available.
- Student portfolio setup status.
- Any known schedule constraints.
- Any parent-approved outside-support notes from tutors, teachers, clinicians, or specialists.
- Applicable education accountability requirements the parent wants considered.

If these inputs are incomplete, the plan may still be drafted as a starter plan, but missing inputs should be clearly marked.

## Included Outputs

The one-time retainer includes:

- One 30-day parent-reviewed learning plan.
- One new-student onboarding sequence.
- One learner context summary.
- One suggested daily/weekly rhythm.
- Four weekly focus outlines.
- One reading basket plan.
- One Khan Academy Kids use rule.
- One portfolio evidence plan.
- One weekly parent review template.
- One first-month portfolio review outline.
- One next-month decision list.
- One safety and authority boundary summary.

Reasonable revisions are included to make the plan usable after parent review. Unlimited revisions are not included unless separately approved.

## Out Of Scope

This one-time retainer does not include:

- A full curriculum.
- Legal homeschool compliance service.
- Diagnosis or treatment recommendations.
- Medical, psychological, special education, or legal determinations.
- Grading, transcript creation, or proof of mastery.
- App-based mastery claims.
- Unsupervised child-facing AI.
- Unlimited revisions.
- Ongoing daily support.
- Ongoing weekly planning after the first 30 days.
- Parent messaging or family-facing reports beyond the agreed scope.
- Outside specialist conclusions.
- Customer pricing design.
- Conversion into a recurring subscription or monthly service.

If any out-of-scope need appears, the correct action is to flag it for parent review and, when appropriate, teacher, tutor, clinician, specialist, attorney, district, or other professional support.

## Authority And Safety Boundary

The parent or guardian retains final authority over:

- Curriculum.
- Pacing.
- Daily implementation.
- Child-facing communication.
- Sensitive information.
- Outside support.
- Legal/accountability requirements.
- What is saved, shared, or omitted.
- All child-facing decisions.

Anyang Intelligence may organize context, draft the plan, suggest activities, prepare review prompts, and surface patterns for parent review.

Anyang Intelligence may not replace the parent, teacher of record, tutor, clinician, legal authority, or specialist. It may not diagnose the child, hide concerns from the parent, or treat app progress as proof of mastery.

## Evidence And Review

Progress evidence should be parent-reviewed and child-connected.

Useful evidence includes:

- Parent observation notes.
- Reading basket notes.
- Portfolio artifacts.
- Proud work selected by the learner.
- Milestone log entries.
- Photos of physical work.
- Digital work approved by the parent.
- Khan Academy Kids observations reviewed by the parent.
- Weekly parent review answers.
- Child reflections, when appropriate and low-pressure.

Do not rely on app activity alone. Do not treat any single artifact as a grade, diagnosis, transcript, or legal record.

The month is working when:

- The parent feels less scattered.
- The learner has a humane daily rhythm.
- Reading and portfolio evidence are easier to sustain.
- The parent can see what sparked curiosity, friction, pride, and growth.
- The next-month decisions are clearer than they were at the start.

## Delivery Acceptance

The retainer is complete when the parent has:

- A usable 30-day personalized learning plan.
- A review rhythm for the month.
- A reading basket plan.
- A Khan Academy Kids use rule.
- A student portfolio evidence structure.
- A first monthly portfolio review shape.
- Clear parent decisions for the next month.
- Explicit safety, authority, and out-of-scope boundaries.

Completion means the scoped package has been delivered for parent review. It does not mean the plan has produced mastery, solved every learning need, met all legal/accountability requirements, established customer pricing, or created an ongoing subscription.

## Next Step After Delivery

After the 30-day plan is delivered and reviewed, the parent may choose one of these next paths:

- Use the plan independently.
- Request a revised 30-day plan.
- Scope separately approved follow-on work.
- Focus on one component, such as reading basket, portfolio review, learner profile, or weekly parent review.
- Pause and collect more evidence before deciding.

Any follow-on work should be separately scoped and approved.
