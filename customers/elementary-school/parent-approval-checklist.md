# Parent Approval Checklist

This is the parent-facing checklist used after a **30-Day Personalized Learning Plan** draft is shared for review.

Its job is to make approval easy, specific, and low-friction.

The parent does not need to approve every idea in the abstract. The parent only needs a clear way to say:

- yes
- change this
- not yet

## Purpose

Use this checklist to help the parent review:

- what feels right
- what needs to change
- what is not approved yet
- what information may be saved
- what information may be shared

This checklist is the parent-facing companion to the internal [parent-approval-record.md](parent-approval-record.md).

## Parent Review Prompt

Use or adapt this message when sending a draft:

Hi,

Here is the draft 30-day plan for your review.

Please mark what feels good, what should change, and what is not approved yet. Short notes are enough.

The goal is not to pressure you into a full yes. The goal is to make sure the plan reflects your real boundaries, your child, and your household before anything is used.

## Short Version

Use this when the parent needs a lighter review surface:

```text
Please reply with:

- what you approve
- what you want changed
- what is not approved yet
- whether the plan is safe to use now, safe only after changes, or not safe to use yet
```

## Why This Matters

The checklist helps:

- keep approval concrete
- keep changes visible
- prevent vague “looks good” feedback from being mistaken for full approval
- protect parent authority without making review feel bureaucratic

## Boundary

This checklist is not:

- a legal form
- a waiver
- a diagnosis tool
- a substitute for parent judgment

It is a simple approval surface for the Student Operating System.
