# Khan Transition Point

This document defines the transition point between **Khan Academy Kids** and the main **Khan Academy** experience inside Elementary School.

The goal is not to treat the transition as a badge, graduation, or automatic promotion.

The goal is to define when the learner is showing enough **visible capability**, **fit**, and **readiness** that the main Khan Academy environment can become useful.

See [khan-transition-readiness-target.md](khan-transition-readiness-target.md) for the companion doctrine that treats this threshold as a deliberate capability target Elementary School can build toward.

## Core Principle

The transition should be based more on:

- observed capabilities
- sustained fit
- parent-approved readiness

and less on:

- raw completion counts
- streaks
- app progress screens
- abstract "finished the app" language

Course completion may be a signal.

It should not be the authority.

## What Makes The Two Products Different

### Khan Academy Kids

Khan Academy Kids is better for:

- low-friction early use
- playful exploration
- parent-observed startup signals
- shorter supervised practice blocks
- early literacy, numeracy, and interest discovery

### Main Khan Academy

The main Khan Academy environment asks for more:

- sustained attention
- navigation independence
- tolerance for a more explicit course structure
- more comfort with less toy-like presentation
- more ability to follow a learning path over time

This means the transition point is partly about **capability** and partly about **interface readiness**.

## Specific Capabilities To Look For

The move from Khan Academy Kids toward main Khan Academy should usually wait until several of these are visible:

### 1. Navigation Readiness

The learner can:

- move through simple digital flows without constant adult rescue
- tolerate selecting among lessons or activities
- recover from small navigation errors without melting down

Why it matters:

- the main Khan environment is less protected and more course-like

### 2. Attention Readiness

The learner can:

- stay with a learning activity longer than the very short novelty window
- tolerate a more explicit task structure
- finish a meaningful chunk without constant switching

Why it matters:

- the main Khan environment benefits from steadier attention

### 3. Reading Readiness

The learner can:

- read enough interface and lesson language to participate without everything being read for them
- tolerate more text than the earliest Khan Kids flow usually requires
- use reading as part of learning rather than only as a barrier

Why it matters:

- main Khan often depends more heavily on reading the interface, prompts, and explanations

### 4. Follow-Through Readiness

The learner can:

- continue after a mistake
- try the next item without collapsing interest immediately
- accept a course-like sequence better than before

Why it matters:

- main Khan becomes more useful when the learner can stay in a sequence long enough for the system to help

### 5. Frustration Regulation Readiness

The learner can:

- hit moderate difficulty without immediate shutdown
- accept correction better than before
- resume after a wrong answer with support that is light rather than constant

Why it matters:

- the main Khan environment can create more visible correction pressure

### 6. Parent-Fit Readiness

The household can:

- support the supervision level the learner still needs
- fit the longer or more structured block into the real rhythm
- absorb the transition without turning it into a daily conflict

Why it matters:

- a learner may be cognitively ready before the household is operationally ready

## What Course Completion Should Mean

Course completion inside Khan Academy Kids should be treated as:

- a possible confidence signal
- a possible persistence signal
- a possible sign that the current environment is becoming familiar

Course completion should **not** be treated as:

- proof that the learner is ready for the main Khan Academy
- proof of grade placement
- proof of mastery
- proof that the learner has outgrown Khan Academy Kids

Completion matters only when it aligns with broader visible capability.

## Better Transition Signals Than Completion Alone

The strongest signals are combinations like:

- repeated willing use + increasing attention span + lower prompt dependence
- successful off-app transfer + stronger reading tolerance + calmer response to correction
- visible boredom with overly easy or overly playful flows + parent-confirmed readiness for more structure

These are more trustworthy than a completion badge by itself.

## Suggested Transition Rule

Elementary School should treat the transition as appropriate when:

- Khan Academy Kids use is no longer mainly a startup support tool
- the learner is showing several readiness capabilities at once
- the parent approves a trial move
- the main Khan Academy experience can be introduced as a limited extension rather than a full replacement

## Recommended Transition Shape

Do not switch all at once.

Prefer:

```text
Khan Academy Kids remains available
  ->
main Khan Academy is introduced in a narrow approved lane
  ->
parent observes fit, frustration, independence, and follow-through
  ->
Student Operating System decides whether to expand, keep limited, or roll back
```

This keeps the transition reversible.

## Practical First Main-Khan Use Cases

The first main Khan Academy use should usually be:

- a narrow extension around a visible interest
- a small structured practice lane
- a limited experiment with parent observation

It should usually not begin as:

- a full migration
- a claim that the learner has graduated
- a replacement for all other supports

## Governance Boundary

Elementary School may use:

- capability signals
- parent observation
- household fit
- limited course-completion context

Elementary School may not use:

- completion badges as transition authority
- app progress as mastery proof
- digital completion as grade placement

## Success Criteria

The transition point is working when:

- the main Khan environment feels like a useful next step, not a forced jump
- the learner can participate without disproportionate frustration
- the parent can explain why the transition is happening
- the system can roll back without drama if fit is poor
- course completion is treated as one clue among several, not the verdict
