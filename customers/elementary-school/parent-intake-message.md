# Parent Intake Message

This message can be adapted and sent before drafting a **30-Day Personalized Learning Plan** for a new student.

The goal is to collect enough parent-approved context to make the first month practical, safe, and personalized without turning intake into paperwork.

## Message

Hi,

Before I draft the 30-day learning plan, I want to collect a clear starting picture of your child, your goals, and the boundaries you want respected.

This plan is meant to support you as the parent. You keep final authority over curriculum, pacing, child-facing communication, what information is saved, what is shared, and whether outside support is needed. The plan will not diagnose, grade, prove mastery, replace legal/accountability requirements, or treat an app score as the full learning picture.

Please reply with whatever you know now. Short bullet points are perfect. If something is unknown, you can write `not sure yet`.

## 1. First-Month Goal

- What would make the first 30 days feel successful?
- What do you most want help with right now: planning, rhythm, reading, math, portfolio evidence, confidence, curiosity, app use, or something else?
- What should the plan avoid?

## 2. Parent Authority And Privacy

- Who should approve the plan before it is used?
- Are there any topics, activities, apps, teaching styles, or approaches you do not want included?
- Is there any information you do not want saved?
- Is there any information you do not want shared?
- Are there homeschool, district, reporting, legal, or accountability requirements you want considered?

If any of these boundaries are unclear, I will pause and clarify before drafting.

- Current daily rhythm:
- Current learning resources or curriculum, if any:
- Reading comfort as you see it:
- Math comfort as you see it:
- Writing or drawing comfort as you see it:
- Favorite topics, books, games, animals, places, shows, activities, or projects:
- Something your child is proud of right now:
- Something that feels hard, frustrating, or avoided:
- Preferred learning modes: reading, watching, building, drawing, talking, moving, playing, listening, experimenting, or other:
- Any known support needs you want considered:

These notes are starting observations, not fixed labels.

## 4. Student Voice

If it feels easy and low-pressure, ask your child a few of these and send short notes:

- What do you like learning about right now?
- What is something you are proud you can do?
- What is something that feels hard or frustrating?
- What books, shows, games, animals, places, or activities do you like?
- If you could make a project about anything, what would you make?
- Do you like learning best by reading, watching, building, drawing, talking, moving, or playing?
- What is one thing you want grown-ups to know about how you learn?

Please skip any question that creates stress. This is a conversation starter, not a test.

## 5. Schedule And Parent Time

- Which days are learning days?
- What time of day usually works best?
- How much parent time is realistic on a light day?
- How much parent time is realistic on a fuller day?
- Are there naps, work schedules, siblings, travel, appointments, activities, or energy limits to plan around?
- Do you prefer a fixed daily rhythm or a flexible menu?

If you are not sure, I will draft three options: minimum viable day, standard day, and fuller day.

- Books currently available:
- Apps or websites already used:
- Physical materials available, such as paper, pencils, art supplies, dice, cards, coins, counting objects, ruler, measuring tape, blocks, or outdoor space:
- Projects or topics already started:
- Tools or materials you want avoided:

## 7. Khan Academy Kids

- Is Khan Academy Kids installed?
- Do you approve using it as a small daily support block?
- What time limit feels right?
- What should I help you observe: choices, frustration, ease, favorite activities, avoided activities, questions, or something else?
- May any app screenshots, notes, or evidence be saved?

Default if approved: 15-20 minutes on learning days, parent-supervised, used for practice and observation rather than as the full curriculum or proof of mastery.

## 8. Reading Basket

- What books does your child currently like?
- What books does your child avoid?
- What books do you want included?
- Does your child prefer fiction, nonfiction, graphic novels, joke books, magazines, field guides, poetry, recipes, read-alouds, or something else?
- Have you noticed reading frustration, avoidance, confidence, rereading, or strong interests?

The first basket can include easy confidence books, current-level books, one stretch book, interest-led nonfiction, and a parent read-aloud.

## 9. Portfolio Evidence

- Where should physical work be saved?
- Is there a digital folder, or should we keep this physical-only for now?
- Who decides what gets saved?
- How often do you want to save evidence?
- Should your child choose proud work each week?
- What is easiest to preserve: photos, worksheets, drawings, recordings, screenshots, parent notes, projects, questions, or milestones?

The default is to save only what helps you see the learning arc. The portfolio should not become paperwork.

## 10. Watch Items And Support

- Is there anything you want me to watch gently for during the first-month plan?
- Are there reading, writing, math, attention, fatigue, behavior, speech/language, health, developmental, emotional, or frustration patterns you want handled carefully?
- Are there teachers, tutors, clinicians, specialists, or other qualified supports whose notes you want considered?

I will name patterns carefully without alarm. Anything high-stakes or outside the plan stays with you and the appropriate qualified human support.

## 11. Approval Before Use

Before the plan is used, I will ask you to approve:

- Daily rhythm.
- Khan Academy Kids rule.
- Reading basket plan.
- Portfolio evidence plan.
- Week 1 focus.
- Child-facing prompts or survey language.
- Watch items and support signals.
- What information may be saved.
- What information may be shared.
- First monthly portfolio review shape.

Once I have your answers, I can draft either:

- A ready plan, if the core information is clear.
- A provisional plan, if some details are missing but authority and safety boundaries are clear.
- A hold note, if parent authority, privacy, safety, schedule, or basic learner context needs clarification first.

Thank you. The best plan is the one that helps you see and support the actual child in front of you.
