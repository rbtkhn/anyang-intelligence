# Monthly Portfolio Review

The monthly portfolio review turns student portfolio artifacts into a parent-readable learning report.

Its job is not to grade the child. Its job is to help the parent see growth, pride, interests, friction, milestones, and next steps.

## Purpose

Use the monthly portfolio review to:

- See the learning arc across the month.
- Notice what the child is proud of.
- Identify milestones worth celebrating.
- Spot recurring interests and patterns.
- Choose better books, projects, practice, and support for next month.
- Make the `$100/month` value visible through concrete evidence and clearer parent decisions.

## Source Materials

Use:

- Physical artifacts from the student portfolio.
- Digital portfolio artifacts.
- Milestone log.
- Reading basket notes.
- Parent observation notebook.
- Khan Academy Kids parent-observed engagement notes.
- Weekly parent reviews.
- Child reflections.

Do not rely on app activity alone. The best evidence is parent-reviewed, child-connected, and visible in actual work.

## Monthly Report Shape

```text
This month we noticed:

Proud work:

Milestones:

Growth evidence:

Recurring interests:

Friction or support needs:

Recommended next steps:

Portfolio pieces to keep:

Parent decision needed:
```

## Analysis Types

### Learning Arc

Ask:

- What changed from the beginning of the month to the end?
- What became easier?
- What became more detailed, confident, independent, or expressive?
- What did the child return to repeatedly?

Output:

- A short narrative of growth.
- 2-3 artifacts that show the arc.

### Pride Pattern

Ask:

- What did the child choose as proud work?
- What kind of work does the child want remembered?
- Does pride appear around effort, creativity, correctness, independence, beauty, usefulness, or mastery?

Output:

- A parent note about motivation.
- One way to create more pride-producing work next month.

### Milestone Timeline

Ask:

- What happened for the first time?
- What hard thing did the child return to?
- What should be celebrated?

Output:

- A dated milestone entry.
- One celebration or acknowledgment.

### Skill Snapshot

Ask:

- What does the portfolio show about reading, writing, math, explanation, creativity, persistence, or organization?
- Is there enough evidence to see a trend?

Output:

- A conservative skill snapshot.
- A note when evidence is too thin to conclude.

Do not present this as a grade, diagnosis, or proof of mastery.

### Interest Map

Ask:

- What topics, characters, materials, questions, or activities keep appearing?
- What did the child choose voluntarily?
- What could become next month's project, book basket, field trip, or writing prompt?

Output:

- 3-5 recurring interests.
- 1-3 next resources or activities.

### Friction And Support Signal

Ask:

- What caused frustration, avoidance, fatigue, or repeated confusion?
- Did the child recover with support?
- Is the pattern occasional, recurring, or persistent?

Output:

- A parent-safe support note.
- A recommended next support action.

If the pattern suggests reading, writing, attention, speech/language, math, behavior, health, or developmental concerns, the parent should consider teacher, tutor, clinician, or specialist support.

### Portfolio Gaps

Ask:

- What is missing?
- Are there few writing samples?
- Is there little math evidence?
- Are there no child reflections?
- Are projects, questions, or reading notes missing?

Output:

- A gap list.
- One low-pressure way to collect better evidence next month.

### Parent Decision Brief

End every review with:

- Repeat this.
- Add this.
- Pause this.
- Watch this.
- Ask for support here, if needed.

The review should make the next parent action obvious.

## Child Reflection Page

Once per month, ask the child:

- What are you proud of?
- What was hard?
- What did you get better at?
- What do you want to learn next?
- What should we keep in the portfolio?

The child reflection page should be short, kind, and optional if the child is tired or resistant.

## Report Archive

Save the completed monthly portfolio review in the student portfolio.

The report should sit beside the evidence, not replace it:

- Physical work remains available for the child to touch and revisit.
- Digital work remains available for review, sharing, or printing when appropriate.
- Reports summarize patterns, decisions, and next steps.

## Family Share Packet

Optional: create a parent-approved family share packet.

It may include:

- 2-5 proud-work highlights.
- 1 milestone.
- 1 child quote.
- 1 photo or artifact.

Do not include sensitive learning concerns, private parent notes, diagnosis-like language, or family details without explicit parent approval.

## `$100/Month` Value Report

At the end of the review, name the parent-visible value created:

- Planning time saved.
- Better next books or activities chosen.
- Learning evidence preserved.
- Child pride made visible.
- Milestones captured.
- Parent uncertainty reduced.
- Support needs noticed earlier.

The report should answer:

```text
What did this system help the parent see or decide that would otherwise have been scattered, forgotten, or harder to notice?
```

## Boundary

The monthly portfolio review is not:

- A gradebook.
- A diagnosis.
- A legal homeschool compliance report.
- A transcript.
- A substitute for parent judgment.
- A substitute for teacher, tutor, clinician, or specialist support.

It is a parent-reviewed learning-memory report.
