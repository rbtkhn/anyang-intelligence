# Abigail Phase 2 Onboarding Survey

This is the personalized **Phase 2 onboarding survey** for **Abigail**.

Phase 1 gave enough signal to classify the case as `Provisional`.

Phase 2 now asks for the next useful details needed to move this case toward a safe first-month draft.

## Parent Framing

Hi,

Thank you for completing Phase 1 of the onboarding survey for Abigail.

That first step gave a strong starting picture. This short Phase 2 follow-up does two things:

- it completes the few practical details needed to unlock a thoughtful first-month draft
- it adds a little more learner detail so the month can fit Abigail more naturally from the start

You do not need perfect answers. Short answers are enough.

The first section is the only part needed to move from `Provisional` toward drafting. The second section helps tailor the month more intelligently.

## Phase 2 Questions

## Part 1: Needed To Draft Safely

### 1. Parent Contact And Drafting Approval

- What is the main parent or guardian name for this plan?
- What is the best email address?
- What is the best phone number?
- Do you approve drafting a first-month plan from the information you have already shared?

### 2. Save / Share Boundary

- Is there anything you do not want saved?
- Is there anything you do not want shared?

### 3. Outside-Support Context

- Are there any teacher, tutor, clinician, specialist, or school notes you want considered?
- If yes, do you want that outside-support context included in planning now?

## Part 2: Helps Tailor The First Month Better

### 4. Abigail's Interests

- What topics, activities, books, games, or projects does Abigail return to most often?
- What usually catches her interest fastest?

### 5. Proud Work

- What is something Abigail is proud of right now?
- What kind of work or activity makes her feel successful?

### 6. Current Friction

- What currently feels hard, frustrating, or often avoided?
- When follow-through drops, what does that usually look like?

### 7. Best Learning Modes

- What kinds of learning seem to work best for Abigail right now?
  - reading
  - talking
  - building
  - drawing
  - moving
  - watching
  - playing
  - other

### 8. Current Rhythm And Schedule

- Which days are active learning days?
- What time of day usually works best?
- Do you want a fixed rhythm or a flexible menu?

### 9. Current Resources

- What books are already available and easy to use?
- Is there a curriculum or program already in use?
- What apps or websites are already being used?
- What materials are easy to use right now?

### 10. Khan Academy Kids Follow-Up

- Is Khan Academy Kids installed already?
- What would you want help deciding about it?
- If you try it, what time limit would feel right?
- Should it stay inside a broader screen-time budget?

### 11. Reading Detail

- What kinds of books does Abigail choose voluntarily?
- What kinds of books are resisted or abandoned?
- Does she seem to prefer fiction, nonfiction, graphic formats, joke books, field guides, poetry, or read-alouds?
- What does reading frustration look like when it shows up?

### 12. Portfolio And Evidence

- Would you prefer a physical portfolio, digital folder, or both?
- Who should decide what gets saved?
- Should Abigail choose proud work each week?

## Close

Once Part 1 is complete, Elementary School should have the key authority and boundary details needed to move this case toward a first-month draft.

Part 2 helps make that month more personalized, usable, and observant from the beginning.
