# Customers

Customer installations are concrete operating contexts where Anyang Intelligence acts as the Executive Operating System.

The goal is to prove the pattern across different kinds of complexity before turning repeated workflows into software.

## Current Installations

| Installation | Domain | Executive OS proof |
| --- | --- | --- |
| [Book Club](book-club/README.md) | Recreational community | Monthly cadence, book selection, discussion memory, host coordination, member experience |
| [Grace Gems](grace-gems/README.md) | Marketplace business | Listing gates, margin discipline, customer-promise clarity, weekly marketplace execution |
| [Media Production](media-production/README.md) | Digital media production | Editorial calendar, production pipeline, quality gates, distribution, audience learning |
| [Mountain Home](mountain-home/README.md) | Property and asset governance | Risk register, continuity planning, seasonal readiness, maintenance prioritization |
| [Non-Profit](non-profit/README.md) | Mission-driven organization | Program priorities, donor stewardship, grant tracking, volunteer coordination, impact reporting |

## Portfolio Logic

The early portfolio should stay small and sharp.

Grace Gems shows that an Executive OS can run commercial operating loops. Mountain Home shows that the same pattern can govern a real-world asset where risk, readiness, and continuity matter more than revenue. Book Club shows the lightest version of the pattern: keeping a small community enjoyable, coordinated, and continuous without making it feel over-managed. Media Production shows creative operations: moving ideas through editorial judgment, production, quality review, distribution, and audience learning. Non-Profit shows mission governance: aligning programs, donors, volunteers, compliance, and impact evidence.

Together they test the core Anyang claim: an Executive OS is not a chatbot or a dashboard. It is a living executive layer that keeps a complex operating context coherent.
