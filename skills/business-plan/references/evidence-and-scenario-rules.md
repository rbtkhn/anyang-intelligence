# Business Plan Evidence And Scenario Rules

Apply these rules to every plan and change proposal.

## Evidence Classes

Label material statements as:

- `Confirmed fact`: directly supported by an authorized source.
- `Estimate`: a bounded approximation with method, source, date, and uncertainty.
- `Assumption`: a temporary planning input requiring owner acceptance.
- `Hypothesis`: a testable explanation or opportunity.
- `Scenario`: a conditional outcome built from explicit inputs.
- `Interpretation`: analysis derived from evidence, not a source statement.
- `Missing`: required evidence is unavailable or unauthorized.

Never promote a public storefront observation, discussion, owner interest, prior plan, or model output into a confirmed private fact.

## Decision-Bound Evidence

For each evidence request, state:

```text
Decision supported:
Evidence requested:
Why it may change the decision:
Minimum sufficient scope:
Permitted source:
Privacy and retention boundary:
Owner or custodian:
```

Never request a broad data dump. Stop when the evidence is sufficient for the named decision or when the membrane prohibits further review.

## Economics And Capacity

For every material economic input, preserve:

- metric and unit;
- source and period;
- calculation and exclusions;
- whether gross, net, cash, or accrual;
- confidence and uncertainty;
- tax or accounting review dependency.

Do not invent margins, sustainable production capacity, conversion, demand, return rates, labor availability, cash runway, or channel economics. Route money classification and tax-sensitive conclusions through `skills/tax-financial-governance/SKILL.md`.

If economics or capacity is required for the decision but unavailable, use `Hold`. If the owner explicitly authorizes scenario planning despite non-critical gaps, label the output provisional and do not present it for final approval.

## Scenario Construction

Use scenarios only when they clarify a real decision:

1. Name the decision and uncertain drivers.
2. Use the smallest useful set, normally low/base/high or two competing cases.
3. State every changed assumption and hold other inputs constant where possible.
4. Show calculations, units, time horizon, sensitivity, and missing evidence.
5. Define what evidence or threshold would select, revise, or reject each case.
6. Never average scenarios into false certainty or convert the base case into a forecast.

## Measures And Learning

Distinguish:

- `Outcome metric`: result the plan seeks.
- `Leading indicator`: earlier signal of progress.
- `Guardrail`: harm, risk, quality, privacy, cash, or customer-trust limit.
- `Burden measure`: owner or staff time and operational load.

Each measure requires a definition, source, baseline or `Missing`, target or decision threshold, owner, cadence, and review date.

For every unresolved material assumption, define a bounded learning item:

```text
Assumption or hypothesis:
Decision affected:
Smallest permitted evidence:
Success or decision threshold:
Guardrail and stop condition:
Owner:
Review date:
Resulting route: [plan change / intake change / operating-loop design / no change]
```

Learning agendas authorize evidence preparation only when explicitly stated. They do not authorize experiments, customer impact, or external action.
