## Core Naming Rule

Keep these layers distinct:

- `Learning Core` = the project lane
- `Learning Core` = the family's operating artifact
- `Student Operating System` = the broader method layer

Do not use these terms as synonyms.

Preferred sentence pattern:

- `Learning Core` delivers a collaborative build of the family's `Learning Core`.
- The `Learning Core` is built using the Student Operating System method.
- Continuity support helps the family keep the `Learning Core` alive over time.

## Standard Lane Flow

Default Learning Core flow:

```text
registration and Phase 1 survey
  -> readiness review
  -> personalized Phase 2 if needed
  -> Ready / Provisional / Hold
  -> plan drafting gate
  -> 30-day collaborative build
  -> observation and evidence loop
  -> continuity invitation if month-one evidence justifies it
```

Do not skip directly from partial intake to polished plan copy without checking readiness and the drafting gate.

## Readiness Rule

Classify the case before drafting:

- `Ready` when parent authority, learner picture, rhythm reality, starter-tool stance, and caution posture are clear enough to draft without invention
- `Provisional` when the core picture is usable but some learner-fit or boundary details are still emerging
- `Hold` when the learner picture is too thin, the boundary picture is too unclear, or outside-support context needs clarification before safe drafting

When in doubt, prefer `Provisional` or `Hold` over overconfident drafting.

## Parent-Facing Survey Rule

Use the lane's survey logic like this:

- Phase 1 = fast signal capture
- Phase 2 = personalized follow-up based on what Phase 1 left unclear

Do not ask the parent to design the 30-day plan for us.

Prefer:

- observable behavior
- household reality
- support level
- reading situation
- tool approval posture
- caution and boundary signals

Avoid:

- diagnostic wording
- abstract theory
- parent-as-planner questions
- false precision about mastery or grade placement

## Drafting Rule

The free 30-day build must be complete enough to use with dignity.

Do not weaken the free month in order to create subscription pressure.

Before drafting:

- confirm parent authority and boundaries
- identify missing inputs honestly
- keep Khan Academy Kids optional unless approved
- keep app activity subordinate to parent observation and portfolio evidence
- avoid claims of mastery

If the case is `Provisional`, label assumptions and missing inputs clearly.

If the case is `Hold`, do not draft around the gap. Name the missing clarification instead.

## Continuity Rule

The monthly subscription is justified only when month-one evidence reveals a real next need.

Good continuity logic:

- the parent wants help interpreting patterns
- the parent wants continuity without rebuilding context
- school, reading, and home signals need interpretation together
- the `Learning Core` is useful and worth keeping alive

Bad continuity logic:

- the free month is intentionally thin
- the invitation appears before evidence exists
- the paid layer sounds like access to basic care
- the invitation is generic feature language with no child or household evidence behind it

## Khan And Reading Rule

Treat Khan Academy Kids and the reading basket as observation-and-recommendation surfaces.

They can inform:

- learner profile updates
- next-step recommendations
- parent review questions
- portfolio evidence choices

They do not prove:

- mastery
- grade placement
- diagnosis
- a complete learner picture by themselves

The Khan transition doctrine is about capability readiness and fit, not automatic promotion based on course completion alone.

## Guardrails

Do not:

- blur `Learning Core`, `Learning Core`, and `Student Operating System`
- diagnose or grade the child
- treat app activity as proof of mastery
- imply the subscription is the hidden real product
- replace parent judgment
- hide missing evidence behind confident prose
- imply OB1 is required for the `Learning Core`

## Done When

The Learning Core artifact or decision:

- uses the right naming layer
- fits the lane's onboarding and readiness flow
- preserves parent authority and child-safety boundaries
- keeps the free-month and continuity boundary honest
- treats observation as evidence and recommendation as governed interpretation
