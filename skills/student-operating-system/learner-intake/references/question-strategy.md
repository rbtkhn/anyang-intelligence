# Adaptive Question Strategy

Use questions in small conversational batches. Ask no more than three at once, reflect what was heard, and pursue only information that could change readiness, profile wording, household fit, evidence handling, or the next action.

## Question Quality

Prefer:

- recent events over generalized traits;
- observable sequences over explanations imposed by the operator;
- what already works as well as what is difficult;
- questions that distinguish learner choice from adult structure;
- minimum viable household rhythm over an ideal schedule;
- one concrete cross-context connection over exhaustive history.

Avoid diagnosis, grade placement, fixed learning-style claims, exhaustive inventories, private history, and repeated questions already answered by authoritative inputs.

## Create Sequence

### Authority And Privacy

Confirm the parent-approved label, opaque operator reference, guardian authority, intake permission, discussion boundary, preservation and deletion rules, sharing boundary, exclusions, and minimum relevant caution implication.

Stop at `Hold` if these remain unclear.

### Direction

Ask what the parent wants to understand this month, what would make it useful without an academic-level change, what should be avoided, and what already works.

### Concrete Evidence

Ask for:

- one recent learning moment that went well;
- what the learner chose, noticed, asked, built, repeated, or connected;
- one recent friction moment and what happened immediately before it;
- what helped, if anything;
- a current interest, proud moment, or recurring question.

Record the event separately from the parent's interpretation.

### Context And Agency

Ask where learning already happens, whether the learner carried an idea between contexts, what choices the learner can make now, and what still needs close adult structure.

### Household Fit

Ask what is sustainable on a difficult day and a normal day, whether a fixed rhythm or flexible menu fits, what signals shortening or pausing, what preparation burden is sustainable, and what screen boundary applies.

### Tools

Ask only about resources that could change the first week. Keep Khan Academy Kids, reading baskets, and detailed daily plans optional unless parent approval and learner fit justify them.

### Evidence And Independence

Ask what evidence is useful, what feels excessive, who controls preservation and deletion, what triggers an immediate pause, and what the learner might increasingly notice, choose, or carry forward.

Use the light default only if approved:

1. One learner-selected item.
2. One concise parent observation.
3. One open question or next direction.

### Optional Learner Voice

After explicit parent approval, ask at most:

1. What are you interested in learning about right now?
2. What is something you feel proud of?
3. What might you like to make, explore, or try next?

Allow every question to be skipped. Stop when comfort, attention, or interest falls. The guardian decides what may be preserved.

## Change Triage

Begin with four questions:

1. What prompted this review?
2. Which part of the current profile may no longer be accurate or useful?
3. What recent evidence supports that concern?
4. Have authority, privacy, safety, household, or tool boundaries changed?

Then inspect only affected sections. Ask what evidence would weaken the proposed interpretation and whether the earlier wording remains useful in any context.

Do not force a change. `No Change` and `Open Question` are successful outcomes when evidence does not support revision.

## Timing

- Authority and privacy gate: 8–10 minutes; stop at 12.
- Create evidence conversation: 15–20 minutes; stop at 25.
- Optional learner voice: 5–7 minutes.
- Operator classification: 10–15 minutes offline.
- Guardian verification: 5–8 minutes.
- Change triage: target 5 minutes before deciding whether deeper questions are justified.

Time limits reduce pressure; they never justify skipping an authority boundary.
