# Business Intake Question Strategy

Ask no more than three questions at once. Reflect what was heard, label uncertainty, and pursue only information that could change readiness, context wording, the intended operating review, or an approval boundary.

## Sequence

### Authority And Data

Confirm the business reference, named owner, intake purpose, permission, temporary handling, preservation, deletion, sharing, exclusions, reuse restrictions, and pause conditions. Stop at `Hold` if a required boundary remains unclear.

### Goals And Scope

Ask for the 30-90 day outcome, the decision the owner wants the first operating review to support, and what is explicitly outside scope.

### Offer And Channels

Ask only for the products or services, customer groups, sales or delivery channels, and priority items needed for the intended review.

### Economics And Evidence

Ask for available revenue, price, direct cost, labor, fulfillment, advertising, refund, or margin evidence only when it changes the intended review. Accept estimates when labeled. Separate source evidence, owner-reported facts, estimates, hypotheses, and `Missing` values.

Route tax, accounting, payroll, legal, or financial classification questions through `skills/tax-financial-governance/SKILL.md`; do not turn intake answers into professional conclusions.

### Customer And Operating Signals

Prefer redacted recurring patterns, counts, and opaque source references over raw messages. Ask what repeatedly creates demand, confusion, support burden, delay, rework, or owner intervention.

### Capacity And Constraints

Ask about fulfillment lead times, staffing, inventory, custom-work limits, tools, budget, realistic weekly capacity, and conditions that require a pause. Do not infer production capacity from revenue, demand, or a service package.

### Authority Boundaries

Ask which decisions require owner approval, including publication, pricing, promotions, spending, hiring, customer messages, policies, refunds, product claims, fulfillment promises, and external commitments.

## Mode Guidance

For `create`, establish the minimum useful context for the first operating review. Do not collect a complete business archive.

For `resume`, begin with the verified checkpoint, current phase and status,
unresolved decision, and evidence added since the checkpoint. Ask again only
for missing, expired, contradicted, or changed authority and data fields.
Separate confirmed owner statements from preparation hypotheses and public
observations. For a meeting continuation, capture corrections, priority,
constraint, costly friction, risky promise, approved review scope, evidence
permission, privacy limit, approval authority, success metric, review date, and
status before recommending work.

For `change`, begin with the trigger, affected section, new evidence, counterevidence, and any changed authority or data boundary. Do not repeat unaffected intake sections.

For Grace Gems, use `projects/grace-gems/business-intake-survey.md` as the canonical project-specific question set. Its six evidence groups satisfy the project route but do not replace the shared authority, approval, readiness, or persistence gates.

For Mountain Villa, use `projects/mountain-villa/business-intake-survey.md` as the canonical project-specific question set. Preserve the stricter property, safety, professional-review, access, security, and financial/legal membranes; do not infer a rental or hospitality business model.

## Question Quality

Prefer recent evidence, observable operating sequences, bounded estimates, and
explicit missing values. Tie every evidence request to an approved question.
Avoid exhaustive histories, broad data dumps, raw private records, leading
questions, duplicate questions, and requests for information that cannot
change the next action.
