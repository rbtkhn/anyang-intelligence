## Review definition

- Business priority: support owned-channel growth, distinct fine-jewelry and
  affordable-jewelry strategies, and a consistent customer-trust experience.
- Decision this review should support: determine which portfolio-wide trust or
  positioning inconsistencies deserve a deeper CEO-approved operating review,
  and which storefront or segment should be prioritized first.
- Selected scope: the public Etsy surfaces of the 13 CEO-provided shops,
  reviewed from a new-customer perspective.
- Comparison dimensions:
  - segment and apparent value proposition;
  - storefront identity and positioning;
  - product and material claim clarity;
  - custom-order expectation clarity;
  - trust signals and documentation language;
  - packaging, fulfillment, return, and repair promises visible publicly;
  - consistency between the public promise and the Phase 2 meeting guardrails.
- Why this scope now: the CEO supplied the full portfolio roster and assigned a
  new-customer review of all 13 shops as a Phase 2 follow-up.
- What remains outside scope: margins, production capacity, private analytics,
  customer records, supplier records, ownership arrangements, internal shop
  administration, listing edits, pricing, advertising, customer communication,
  and any claim about actual operating performance.

## Recommendation boundary

- Proposed analysis or deliverable: a concise 13-shop new-customer observation
  matrix, segment-level findings, a short list of portfolio-wide trust risks,
  and a recommended next review question.
- Actions permitted:
  - read public Etsy pages;
  - record sanitized observations;
  - compare public positioning and promise clarity;
  - recommend a bounded next review.
- Actions not permitted:
  - follow shops from an Etsy account as part of this review;
  - edit, publish, deactivate, or promote listings;
  - change prices, policies, claims, packaging, fulfillment, returns, or
    customer communications;
  - access or store private seller, buyer, order, supplier, or financial data;
  - treat public observations as proof of internal performance.
- Approval required before use: CEO approval is required before any observation
  becomes a customer-facing change, operating commitment, or implementation
  instruction.

## Measurement

- Primary value metric: portfolio coverage - reviewed public evidence for all
  13 CEO-provided storefronts, with unavailable surfaces explicitly marked.
- Baseline: 9 exact shop pages initially verified, 3 additional shops
  corroborated through indexed Etsy evidence, and ReliquarieUS pending
  independent verification.
- Target or comparison: 13 storefront observation rows, separated into 5
  solid-gold and natural-gem shops and 8 affordable-jewelry shops.
- Quality, trust, rights, or burden guardrail: every finding must cite visible
  public evidence or be labeled `Not observed`; no private or authenticated
  seller data enters the review.
- New burden introduced: one CEO review of the findings and one decision on the
  next bounded operating-review question.
- Net operating judgment: proceed as a public-evidence Phase 2 review; hold all
  implementation.
