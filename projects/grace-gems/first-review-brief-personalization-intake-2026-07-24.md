## Review definition

- Business priority: reduce specification risk and decision burden while
  preserving flexible custom service in the five solid-gold and natural-gem
  shops.
- Decision this review should support: determine the minimum intake and approval
  record needed before accepting configuration, made-to-order, or limited
  bespoke work.
- Selected workflow: a maximum of five recent custom inquiries or accepted
  orders, no more than one owner-selected example per eligible shop when
  available.
- Working service categories:
  - configuration: permitted adjustments to an existing design;
  - made to order: an existing approved design produced after purchase, with
    permitted configuration choices when applicable;
  - bespoke commission: a substantially original or redesigned one-off under a
    separate scope, quote, and timeline;
  - standard ready-to-order: an unchanged design without personalization.
- Why this scope now: the CEO confirmed that customization is limited to the
  five fine-jewelry shops, bespoke work is uncommon, and specification mistakes
  may create material customer and financial harm.
- What remains outside scope: approving the category wording for publication,
  evaluating design quality, generating customer designs, training AI on
  customer material, pricing changes, production execution, or reviewing
  affordable-shop orders.

## Evidence

- Evidence to review:
  - redacted request summary and assigned service category;
  - base design reference and requested changes;
  - gemstone, material, size, design-element, and quantity specifications when
    applicable;
  - feasibility decision, price and lead-time acknowledgment, and evidence of
    customer approval before production;
  - whether an AI concept was used and whether a human-approved production
    specification replaced it.
- Source locations: owner-authorized private custom-order location outside Git.
- Privacy or membrane limits: exclude customer identity, contact information,
  full messages, payment data, personal images, proprietary customer artwork,
  and production-ready design files. Use redacted field presence and sanitized
  patterns only.
- Known evidence gaps: evidence location, reviewer access, and representative
  examples are not yet confirmed; proposed category definitions are not
  approved customer-facing language.
- Evidence interface: the Executive Assistant retrieves or reviews only the
  owner-authorized
  minimized evidence and returns a redacted evidence receipt. The Chief
  Executive analyzes only that returned evidence.

## Recommendation boundary

- Proposed analysis or deliverable: a gap analysis against a proposed minimum
  personalization-intake record and a recommendation for one bounded workflow
  design question.
- Actions permitted: the Executive Assistant may retrieve the approved redacted
  examples and
  return a minimized evidence receipt; the Chief Executive may classify them,
  identify missing decision fields, compare approval sequencing, and recommend
  a minimum intake control.
- Actions not permitted: respond to customers; accept, reject, price, design, or
  modify an order; create production specifications; publish category
  definitions; upload customer material to an AI system; change listings or
  policies.
- Approval required before use: CEO approval of customer-facing definitions and
  any intake, design, pricing, communication, or production change.

## Measurement

- Primary value metric: percentage of sampled custom requests with category,
  exact specification, feasibility, price, lead time, and customer approval
  captured before production commitment.
- Baseline: not yet reviewed.
- Target or comparison: establish the bounded baseline; the proposed control
  target is 100% completion of required fields before commitment.
- Quality, trust, rights, or burden guardrail: an AI concept may support
  inspiration or visualization but must not substitute for a human-approved
  production specification or imply rights, originality, feasibility, or final
  appearance.
- New burden introduced: redaction of no more than five examples and one CEO
  decision on the minimum intake record.
- Net operating judgment: approved review question; evidence review and any
  operational use remain held.

## Review and decision

- Planned completion date: not assigned.
- Review authority: CEO-approved bounded personalization-intake review.
- Continue if: representative redacted examples can be reviewed without
  customer-identifying or rights-sensitive material.
- Revise if: one example per eligible shop is unavailable or the three custom
  categories do not describe the actual requests.
- Stop or hold if: production proceeded without an agreed specification,
  feasibility is unclear, rights-sensitive material cannot be excluded, or
  evidence access is not explicitly authorized.
- Next learning cycle: CEO decision on the category definitions and whether to
  design a minimum custom-request decision loop.
