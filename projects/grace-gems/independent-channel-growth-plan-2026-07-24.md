# Grace Gems Proposed Independent-Channel Growth Plan

**Plan ID:** GG-P2-PLAN-OWNED-CHANNEL-2026-07-24-01  
**Date:** 2026-07-24  
**Related capture:** [CEO Meeting Capture](ceo-meeting-capture-2026-07-23.md)  
**Decision owner:** Grace Gems CEO; individual name not recorded  
**State:** `Proposed - not approved or activated`

## Decision summary

Build one bounded owned-channel proof around the Grace Gems fine-jewelry brand
before considering portfolio-wide expansion. Keep Etsy operating as a
marketplace channel while acquiring website demand independently through
owner-approved search, Pinterest, content, referral, and voluntary email
paths.

The objective is not to leave Etsy quickly. It is to make Etsy one productive
channel rather than the sole commercial dependency.

## Evidence status

### Confirmed

- Grace Gems operates 13 Etsy shops across fine-jewelry and affordable-jewelry
  segments.
- The CEO identified expansion of independent Grace Gems website sales and
  reduced Etsy reliance as a priority.
- Grace Gems is the only portfolio shop recorded as connected to Pinterest.
- The five solid-gold and natural-gem shops accept configuration and
  made-to-order requests; fully bespoke work is limited.
- The eight affordable shops sell unchanged supplier designs without
  personalization or made-to-order service.

### Operator-reported

- Approximately 100% of the current business depends on Etsy.

This requires verification against a bounded trailing-period channel baseline
before it becomes a financial planning fact.

### Hypotheses

- Grace Gems fine jewelry is the best initial owned-channel proof because it
  has greater differentiation, custom-service depth, and brand-building
  potential than unchanged supplier products.
- Pinterest, search-led educational content, referrals, and voluntary email
  acquisition can generate demand that did not originate on Etsy.
- An owned site may improve customer relationship control and channel learning,
  but no margin or conversion advantage is assumed without evidence.

### Missing

- Revenue, order, gross-margin, fee, refund, and contribution-margin baselines
  by channel and segment.
- Current website readiness, analytics, conversion, accessibility, privacy,
  security, and checkout evidence.
- Owner-approved launch collection, brand architecture, acquisition budget,
  capacity, service levels, and 90-day proof threshold.

## Strategic choices

1. **Prove one owned brand first.** Do not reproduce all 13 Etsy shops on
   independent sites before one bounded model works.
2. **Keep Etsy productive.** Do not close shops or reduce viable marketplace
   demand merely to improve a concentration percentage.
3. **Acquire customers independently.** Never redirect an Etsy-initiated
   transaction to another checkout.
4. **Lead with differentiated fine jewelry.** Use an owner-selected,
   evidence-supported collection rather than the full catalog.
5. **Build trust before traffic.** Verify product, material, shipping,
   personalization, lead-time, certificate, return, repair, privacy, and
   payment claims before publication.
6. **Scale from contribution evidence.** Do not infer profitability from
   revenue, traffic, conversion, or lower marketplace fees alone.

## Explicit non-choices

- No immediate Etsy exit.
- No simultaneous independent launch for all 13 storefronts.
- No transfer of Etsy-originated transactions off-platform.
- No paid advertising, discounting, inventory purchase, or agency commitment
  without separate CEO approval.
- No customer-data export into Git or use outside its authorized purpose.
- No website publication, claim, policy, or customer communication authorized
  by this plan.

## 90-day learning sequence

### Gate 0 — Risk and baseline readiness

Prepare decisions on:

- Etsy product-family eligibility, prioritizing unchanged supplier jewelry;
- truthful seller, production-partner, product-origin, and shipping-origin
  representations;
- related-account and business-continuity exposure;
- owner-controlled exports of shop settings, listings, and reviews, with order
  and customer data retained privately;
- trailing-period Etsy revenue, orders, fees, advertising attribution, refunds,
  and contribution economics;
- the exact owned brand, collection, and decision owner for the proof.

**Gate:** do not publish or buy traffic until material compliance questions and
the owned-channel baseline are either resolved or explicitly held by the CEO.

### Days 1–30 — Owned-channel trust foundation

Prepare an owner-reviewable website release candidate containing:

- one owner-selected fine-jewelry collection;
- approved brand, product, material, shipping, personalization, lead-time,
  documentation, return, repair, and privacy language;
- configuration, made-to-order, bespoke, and standard-product boundaries;
- functioning checkout, payment, tax, privacy, accessibility, security,
  analytics, and fulfillment handoffs;
- source tagging that distinguishes independent traffic from Etsy-originated
  activity;
- a voluntary email or inquiry path with approved consent and retention rules.

**Decision:** approve, revise, or hold a bounded owned-site release.

### Days 31–60 — Independent demand tests

Run only separately approved, low-burden acquisition tests:

- Pinterest content linked directly to the owned site;
- search-led educational and product-intent pages;
- direct referrals or partnerships with approved attribution;
- voluntary email follow-up to independently acquired subscribers;
- paid acquisition only under a separate budget, margin, claim, and stop-rule
  approval.

Each test must define its audience, source, content, spend if any, measurement,
capacity limit, stop condition, and owner.

**Decision:** continue only sources that produce qualified owned-site demand
without claim, capacity, privacy, or contribution-margin failure.

### Days 61–90 — Conversion and channel proof

Compare independently acquired website activity with the verified Etsy
baseline:

- qualified visits and product engagement;
- inquiry, configuration, and checkout progression;
- completed orders and cancellations;
- customer-service and specification burden;
- contribution after product cost, fulfillment, payment, advertising, returns,
  and human review;
- fulfillment quality and customer-promise exceptions.

**Decision:** `Scale`, `Continue learning`, `Revise`, or `Hold`. Expansion to
another brand requires a new owner-approved plan decision.

## Measurement framework

| Measure | Definition | Current baseline | Initial decision use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Etsy revenue concentration | Etsy revenue divided by total revenue for the same period | Operator reports approximately 100%; unverified | Establish exposure, not a forced reduction quota |
| Independently acquired website revenue | Revenue from demand that did not originate on Etsy | Missing | Verify owned-channel proof |
| Qualified owned-site traffic | Visits matching approved product or inquiry intent | Missing | Compare acquisition sources |
| Owned-site conversion | Completed owned-site orders divided by eligible sessions | Missing | Diagnose funnel readiness |
| Contribution per order | Revenue less product, platform/payment, fulfillment, advertising, return, and attributable handling costs | Missing | Prevent revenue-only scaling |
| Custom-intake completeness | Eligible requests with category, specification, feasibility, price, lead time, and approval recorded | Missing | Protect trust and owner time |
| Customer-promise exceptions | Orders with claim, specification, availability, origin, dispatch, or policy mismatch | Missing | Guardrail; investigate every material exception |
| Owner review burden | Decision-owner minutes per qualified inquiry or completed order | Missing | Prevent channel growth from increasing the primary constraint |

## Proposed 90-day proof scenarios

These are learning thresholds, not forecasts or approved targets:

| Scenario | Independent completed orders | Acquisition-source breadth | Decision |
| --- | ---: | ---: | --- |
| Minimum signal | 1 | 1 verified independent source | Diagnose; insufficient for scaling |
| Base proof | 5 | At least 2 independent sources | Evaluate repeatability and contribution |
| Strong early proof | 10 | At least 2 independent sources | Consider a bounded scale proposal |

No scenario supports scaling when contribution economics, capacity, trust,
privacy, or fulfillment guardrails fail.

## Risks and guardrails

- **Etsy eligibility:** unchanged supplier products require immediate
  product-family classification under current Etsy rules.
- **Related-account exposure:** multiple shops do not eliminate shared-platform
  enforcement risk.
- **Off-platform transactions:** transactions initiated on Etsy remain on Etsy.
- **Brand dilution:** do not reproduce unrelated storefront identities on the
  owned site without a deliberate brand decision.
- **Capacity:** custom-demand growth must not exceed design, production, or
  owner-decision capacity.
- **Claims:** AI concepts are inspiration or visualization only unless an
  approved human process converts them into production specifications.
- **Privacy:** analytics, customer, order, financial, and supplier evidence
  remains in owner-authorized private systems outside Git.
- **Economics:** no advertising or scaling decision without contribution
  evidence and CEO-approved spending authority.

## Approval decisions required

3. which evidence sources and private locations reviewers may access;

5. the acquisition channels, budgets, owners, and stop conditions;
6. the 90-day proof threshold and review date.
