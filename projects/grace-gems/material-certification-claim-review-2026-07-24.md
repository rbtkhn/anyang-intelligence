# Grace Gems Phase 2 Material and Certification Claim Review

**Review date:** 2026-07-24  
**Related review:** [Phase 2 Storefront First-Review](storefront-first-review-observation-matrix-2026-07-24.md)  
**Evidence boundary:** Public storefront evidence and official FTC guidance  
**Status:** `evidence review complete - owner substantiation required; no changes authorized`

## Executive finding

GraceGemsUS, GlamGemsBoutique, and AmorFineJewelryArt use substantially similar
portfolio-level promises that all gemstones are `100% natural and untreated`
and that a certificate of appraisal can be supplied for an added fee and about
one additional week.

Those promises may be accurate. The public evidence does not establish:

- who issues the certificate;
- whether it is an identification report, treatment report, origin report,
  grading report, insurance appraisal, or valuation;
- whether it covers the center stone, every stone, or the finished piece;
- whether the report verifies treatment status and geographic origin;
- whether the same promise is supportable for every active listing.

The immediate need is a controlled claim vocabulary and substantiation path,
not new marketing copy.

`High substantiation burden` is not a finding that the claim is false. It means
the claim is specific enough that Grace Gems should be able to identify the
supporting evidence and accountable owner.

## Claim types that must not be treated as synonyms

| Claim | What it should answer |
| --- | --- |
| Natural or mined | Whether the material formed naturally rather than being laboratory-created or simulated |
| Laboratory-grown or laboratory-created | Whether a gemstone has essentially the same relevant properties as the named mined stone but was created in a laboratory |
| Simulated or imitation | Whether the material resembles another stone without being that stone |
| Treated | Whether a process changed appearance, durability, or other characteristics |
| Untreated | Which treatments were tested for and by whom |
| Unheated | Whether heat treatment was tested for; this does not address every other possible treatment |
| Geographic origin | The country or region attributed to the stone and the basis for that attribution |
| Conflict-free or ethically sourced | The precise sourcing standard, covered material, evidence chain, and exclusions |
| Certified | The issuer, report number, subject, tests, results, and report date |
| Appraised | The appraiser, purpose, valuation date, assumptions, and value type |
| Grade | The grading system, grader, tolerances, and whether the grade applies to the actual stone |

## Official guidance context

The [FTC Jewelry Guides](https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/topics/tools-consumers/jewelry-guides)
state that jewelry marketers should accurately represent characteristics
including type, grade, quality, metallic content, weight, treatment, origin,
production, manufacture, and distribution.

FTC business guidance also explains that laboratory-created gemstone
descriptions should clearly disclose that the stone is not mined and that
certain gemstone treatments should be disclosed when they are nonpermanent,
create special-care requirements, or significantly affect value. This review
does not make a legal determination; it identifies the need for qualified
review before publishing or revising claims.

## Minimum owner substantiation request

Do not request the full supplier archive. For each **claim pattern** Grace Gems
wants to retain, request:

1. approved exact claim term;
2. product or stone types covered;
3. evidence source and accountable internal owner;
4. laboratory or appraiser name, when applicable;
5. report type and what it actually verifies;
6. whether the report is item-specific or representative;
7. treatment tests included and excluded;
8. origin basis, if geographic origin is claimed;
9. certificate price and turnaround rule;
10. customer disclosure and care instruction required.

Supporting reports, supplier records, appraisals, and item-level documents must
remain in an approved private location outside Git. The sanitized review should
record only the claim pattern, evidence status, responsible role, and last
verification date.

## Recommended internal claim register

| Field | Required value |
| --- | --- |
| Claim ID | Stable internal identifier |
| Approved term | Exact internal vocabulary |
| Plain-language meaning | What the claim tells the customer |
| Covered products | Storefront, category, or SKU pattern |
| Exclusions | Products or conditions not covered |
| Evidence type | Lab report, appraisal, supplier declaration, assay, or other |
| Evidence owner | Accountable internal role |
| Customer documentation | Included, optional, paid, or unavailable |
| Care disclosure | Required statement or `none` |
| Last verified | Date |
| Status | Approved, revise, hold, or retire |

## Decision boundary

Owner substantiation should precede any recommendation to retain, narrow,
standardize, or remove a material claim. Qualified legal or gemological review
may be required for final customer-facing language.

No listing, website, certificate promise, price, policy, customer
communication, purchasing, or supplier action is authorized by this review.
