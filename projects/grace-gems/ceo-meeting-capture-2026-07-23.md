## Operator-reported platform-concentration risk — 2026-07-24

- The operator reported that 100% of the current business is based on Etsy.
- This is a material strategic risk signal, not yet a verified revenue
  calculation. Confirm it against a bounded trailing-period channel baseline
  before using it as a financial fact.
- Thirteen Etsy storefronts do not create platform diversification. Etsy
  remains the common marketplace, discovery, payments, policy, and account
  dependency.
- Etsy's current Seller Policy permits account or related-account restrictions,
  suspension, or termination in specified circumstances. Its Creativity
  Standards require products to fit allowed seller-role categories.
- The CEO's description of affordable-shop products as unchanged supplier
  designs sourced after customer orders creates an urgent eligibility question.
  It does not establish a policy violation; a separate product-family
  compliance review is required.
- Independent-channel development must acquire demand independently. It must
  not redirect transactions initiated on Etsy, which Etsy's current policy
  prohibits.
- Current official references reviewed on 2026-07-24:
  - [Etsy Seller Policy](https://www.etsy.com/legal/sellers/)
  - [Etsy Creativity Standards](https://www.etsy.com/legal/creativity/)
  - [Etsy Fees and Payments Policy](https://www.etsy.com/legal/fees/)

### Proposed strategic response

1. Review Etsy eligibility and related-account continuity risk.
2. Preserve owner-controlled private exports of shop and operating records.
3. Verify the actual Etsy concentration baseline and channel economics.
4. Build independently acquired demand to an owned Grace Gems website.
5. Reduce concentration gradually while retaining Etsy as a productive channel.

This response is proposed planning work only. It does not authorize account,
listing, website, advertising, pricing, customer, or fulfillment changes.
