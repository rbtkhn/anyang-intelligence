## Review definition

- Business priority: make reliable availability and dispatch promises for
  affordable-shop products that are sourced from suppliers after a customer
  order.
- Decision this review should support: determine which evidence and timing
  checkpoints are needed to make a trustworthy dispatch commitment without
  describing standard supplier products as personalized or made to order.
- Selected workflow: a maximum of eight recent completed affordable-shop
  orders, selected by the owner to represent materially different suppliers or
  fulfillment paths without requiring one record from every shop.
- Why this scope now: the CEO confirmed that affordable shops sell unchanged
  supplier designs, do not accept personalization or made-to-order requests,
  and purchase products from suppliers after customer orders are received.
- What remains outside scope: supplier selection or negotiation, supplier
  identity, purchasing changes, margins, inventory investment, listing edits,
  customer contact, shipping-origin revalidation, and automation.

## Evidence

- Evidence to review:
  - redacted storefront and product-reference category;
  - customer-order timestamp;
  - supplier availability confirmation and purchase timestamp;
  - receipt or quality-check milestone in the United States when applicable;
  - promised and actual customer dispatch dates;
  - exception category without customer or supplier identity.
- Source locations: owner-authorized private order and procurement locations
  outside Git.
- Privacy or membrane limits: exclude customer and supplier identities,
  addresses, full order numbers, messages, invoices, unit costs, margins,
  payment data, and complete tracking numbers. Store only aggregate cycle times
  and sanitized exception categories in Git.
- Known evidence gaps: evidence locations, access, normal lead-time baseline,
  and owner-approved service threshold are not yet confirmed.
- Evidence interface: the Executive Assistant retrieves or reviews only the
  owner-authorized
  minimized evidence and returns a redacted evidence receipt. The Chief
  Executive analyzes only that returned evidence.

## Recommendation boundary

- Proposed analysis or deliverable: a bounded order-to-availability and
  order-to-dispatch baseline, exception pattern summary, and recommendation for
  one minimum promise-control checkpoint.
- Actions permitted: the Executive Assistant may retrieve the approved redacted
  sample and
  return a minimized evidence receipt; the Chief Executive may calculate
  bounded elapsed times, distinguish unchanged supplier products from custom
  work, and recommend a control question.
- Actions not permitted: contact or replace suppliers; purchase inventory;
  change prices, dispatch estimates, listings, policies, or customer messages;
  expose supplier economics; infer sustainable capacity from the sample.
- Approval required before use: CEO approval before any supplier, procurement,
  inventory, dispatch-promise, listing, or customer-communication change.

## Measurement

- Primary value metric: percentage of sampled orders with supplier availability
  confirmed and a traceable dispatch commitment, plus median and maximum
  order-to-United-States-carrier-acceptance time.
- Baseline: not yet reviewed.
- Target or comparison: establish the bounded baseline first; flag every
  supplier unavailability event and every dispatch outside the customer promise
  for owner decision rather than inventing a target.
- Quality, trust, rights, or burden guardrail: do not label unchanged supplier
  goods as personalized or made to order; do not expose supplier identity,
  price, or margin; do not use the sample as proof of sustainable capacity.
- New burden introduced: selection and redaction of no more than eight records,
  followed by one CEO decision.
- Net operating judgment: approved review question; evidence review held until
  sources and access are authorized.

## Review and decision

- Planned completion date: not assigned.
- Review authority: CEO-approved bounded supplier-dependent fulfillment review.
- Continue if: the required timestamps and milestones can be reviewed in
  redacted form.
- Revise if: eight records cannot represent the materially different
  fulfillment paths or milestone definitions are inconsistent.
- Stop or hold if: availability or promise evidence is missing, the review
  requires supplier-identifying economics, or access has not been explicitly
  authorized.
- Next learning cycle: CEO decision on whether to design a minimum
  availability-and-dispatch decision loop.
