## Decision in one sentence

Should Grace Gems adopt four distinct custom-order categories as the
foundation for a controlled portfolio-wide workflow?

## Why this decision is needed

Public storefronts currently use `custom` for materially different work:
engraving, product-option changes, production of existing designs, and fully
bespoke commissions. These transactions carry different cost, approval,
payment, timing, cancellation, return, documentation, and customer-expectation
risks.

Without a shared classification, the business cannot reliably standardize
custom-order intake or determine which controls are necessary for each order.
This becomes more important as Grace Gems expands custom work and prepares an
independent website.

## Proposed categories

Approve the four categories as an **internal operating vocabulary**:

| Category | Definition | Typical examples |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **1. Personalization** | We add a personal detail to an existing piece without changing its main design. | Engraving or gift message |
| **2. Configuration** | You choose from available options to make an existing design right for you. | Ring size, metal color, metal purity, or approved stone option |
| **3. Made to order** | We make an existing design for you after your order is placed. | Existing ring design produced in the selected size and metal |
| **4. Bespoke commission** | We work with you to create a new or substantially redesigned piece. | New ring based on a reference image, custom CAD, or customer-selected center stone |

Approval of these categories would not approve customer-facing terminology,
prices, deposits, cancellation rules, policies, software, or storefront
changes.
