## Current Read

Q3 is open. The Q2 posture remains `candidate`: China is becoming more important to monitor for production scale, components, deployment evidence, and potential future relationships, but it has not earned a purchase, market-entry, or dependency decision. This ledger records dated evidence as it appears; it is not a completed quarterly conclusion.

## Evidence Standard

For a signal to move beyond `watch`, preserve a named commercial site, task, operator or buyer, deployment date, operating conditions, output or reliability evidence, intervention rate, maintenance and support arrangement, safety controls, and data implications. Production forecasts, policy direction, demonstrations, and vendor claims can inform attention but do not establish useful deployment or an economic case.

## Material Signals

### National production forecast

The July 7 [Xinhua report](https://english.news.cn/20260707/064b35b3a22c477ebac191da796c0eb0/c.html) attributes the 100,000-unit 2026 expectation to Gan Xiaobin, a deputy director in MIIT's science-and-technology department. Treat it as a policy-adjacent forecast, not a verified market outcome. The next threshold is evidence of what is shipped, to whom, for which task, with what operational and service results.

### Shanghai ecosystem activity

The July 9 [Xinhua report](https://english.news.cn/20260709/7629495dfb71431e82247c63565bc6a0/c.html) is useful as an ecosystem signal, but it combines demonstrations, company claims, and broad market assertions. It should prompt diligence on named operating sites, buyers, maintenance coverage, data paths, and repeat orders rather than a vendor conclusion.

## Active Q3 Questions

1. Does the reported 2026 output forecast become independently observable shipments and repeat orders, rather than inventory or announced capacity?
2. Which China-based deployments identify a constrained task and publish enough reliability, intervention, maintenance, safety, and support evidence to compare fairly with U.S. and allied alternatives?
3. How do current export controls, tariffs, import obligations, data-governance rules, intellectual-property exposure, and support geography affect any future trial?
4. Are there credible relationships that improve learning without exposing Anyang Intelligence IP, sensitive data, or decision authority?
