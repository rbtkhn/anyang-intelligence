# A Physical Agent Is Not Adoptable Until Its Failure Can Be Governed

Title rationale: The gate's controlling judgment is that capability alone cannot justify adoption when authority, failure response, safety, privacy, maintenance, or accountability remains unresolved.

Use this internal candidate primitive when Anyang Intelligence is considering a specific robot, embodied agent, sensing system, or physical-automation proposal.

It evaluates whether a system is ready for a bounded, human-governed use. It is not a purchase authorization, vendor recommendation, budget approval, safety certification, insurance determination, or authority delegation.

## Lead Judgment

An embodied system should remain on hold until a defined task, accountable human, evidence threshold, stop path, data boundary, maintenance owner, and failure response make a small use both reversible and reviewable.

## Controlling Object

Whether the proposed system's useful task can remain bounded when sensing, physical action, software failure, maintenance burden, or environmental conditions diverge from the expected case.

## Required Review

| Review area | Question | Hold when |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Defined usefulness | What concrete task will the system perform, for whom, in which environment, and what outcome would prove it useful? | The proposal relies on novelty, generalized capability, or undefined future value. |
| Human authority | Who owns the task, approves operation, receives exceptions, and can stop or override the system? | Authority, supervision, or an immediate stop path is unclear. |
| Evidence and reliability | What demonstrations, logs, inspections, or trials show the system can perform the bounded task reliably enough? | Vendor claims or research rhetoric are the only evidence. |
| Safety and environment | What physical hazards, bystanders, access limits, emergency conditions, and fail-safe behavior apply? | A safety-critical action, unreviewed environment, or unacceptable hazard is involved. |
| Privacy and security | What data, sensors, recordings, accounts, networks, and access permissions are required? | The data boundary, consent, security posture, or access control is unknown. |
| Maintenance and support | Who maintains, repairs, updates, stores, and supports the system, and what happens when it fails? | Ownership of upkeep, failure response, or support is absent. |
| Economics and insurance | What are the full costs, expected benefit, liability implications, and insurance/professional-review needs? | Cost, funding authority, liability, or insurance implications are unresolved. |
| Override and accountability | How are actions logged, outcomes checked, incidents reported, and delegated scope narrowed or stopped? | The system cannot be meaningfully audited, overridden, or held within its approved scope. |

## Routing Outcome

- `hold`: Any required area is unknown, unsafe, unauthorized, or unsupported by evidence.
- `watch`: A relevant capability exists, but no defined lane task justifies a proposal.
- `test`: A small, reversible, owner-approved trial has a defined task, environment, stop condition, and review receipt.
- `adopted`: The accountable human has approved a bounded operating use after the required evidence and professional review, where needed.

## Membrane Rule

Translate research into this review shape; do not import vendor claims, pricing promises, deployment timelines, or source rhetoric as Anyang Intelligence doctrine. Apply the receiving lane's strictest privacy, authority, safety, financial/legal, and domain membrane. In particular, do not assume robots or physical automation are appropriate for Learning Core, Book Club, or any client work without a separate, explicit approval.

## Uncertainty

| Status and cause | Consequence | Evidence that would reduce it |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Unresolved—task performance and failure behavior have not yet been observed in the proposed environment | Default to `hold`; do not convert general capability into purchase or operating authority | A bounded owner-approved trial with logs, stop-condition evidence, incident review, maintenance evidence, and any required safety, insurance, legal, or domain-professional review |
