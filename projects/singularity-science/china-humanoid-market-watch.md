# Whether China Humanoid Deployment Evidence Can Justify a Governed Test

Title rationale: The watch exists to identify the evidentiary threshold at which market attention may become a reversible test without silently becoming procurement or market-entry authority.

This is the vendor-neutral intelligence and relationship cadence for Anyang Intelligence's China humanoid-market attention through July 2028.

## Lead Judgment

China warrants sustained attention because its platform, standards, supply, and deployment infrastructure may produce useful options, but observation must remain separate from commitment until task evidence, supportability, data controls, and full economics justify a bounded test.

## Controlling Object

Whether independently verifiable deployment evidence can reduce reliability, support, security, and dependency risk enough to justify an owner-approved reversible test.

## Purpose

Treat China as a strategic market for learning, relationship-building, and optional future sourcing. Track what is actually becoming useful in hardware, components, deployment, pricing, service, and integration without turning market excitement into a purchase, market-entry, data-sharing, or dependency commitment.

## Quarterly Review

- Start each review from the [quarterly watch report template](templates/china-humanoid-quarterly-watch-template.md).
- Record credible China-based vendor deployments, not only demonstrations, with the task, environment, operator, evidence, and stated limitations.
- Track component and whole-robot pricing only when the source, configuration, warranty, service terms, and geography are clear.
- Identify candidate suppliers, integrators, researchers, and market-entry advisors; conduct appropriate diligence before any material relationship.
- Compare China-based options with U.S. and allied-country alternatives on task performance, support, parts, data controls, cybersecurity, total cost, and availability.
- Recheck export controls, tariffs, import requirements, data-governance rules, intellectual-property exposure, and contractual restrictions before outreach, trials, or procurement.

## Boundaries

- No China market entry, purchase, vendor recommendation, intellectual-property transfer, or data-sharing commitment is created by this watch.
- Keep Anyang Intelligence core IP, decision authority, and sensitive child/family, property, financial, or project information outside a China-dependent operating path.
- Do not send sensitive data, camera or microphone feeds, credentials, or customer/project records to a vendor or research contact without explicit owner approval and the applicable privacy, security, and legal review.
- Avoid sole-source dependence; keep alternative vendors, components, and support paths visible.
- A future pilot or purchase remains governed by the [Embodied Agency Adoption Gate](primitives/embodied-agency-adoption-gate.md) and an owner-approved economic decision.

## Evidence to Preserve

For each material signal or relationship, record the date, source, what is verified versus claimed, commercial relevance, data implications, support geography, risks, and the next human decision. Translate insights into reusable procurement questions; do not copy vendor rhetoric or project-sensitive facts across lanes.

## Uncertainty

| Status and cause | Consequence | Evidence that would reduce it |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Unresolved—vendor performance, policy targets, support geography, data handling, and total cost change faster than the quarterly evidence base | Keep the watch vendor-neutral and non-authorizing; stale evidence cannot support outreach, testing, or procurement | Dated independent deployment records, current legal and trade review, support terms, security assessment, and owner-approved task economics |
