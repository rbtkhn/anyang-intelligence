## Purpose

Test whether the episode's recursive-self-improvement discussion yields a useful strategic primitive for Singularity Science:

> **Learning-loop ownership review:** identify who owns the evidence, corrections, improvement decisions, and authority boundaries created by an AI-assisted workflow.

This review is intentionally narrower than the episode's claims about autonomous RSI. It concerns governed organizational learning, not autonomous self-modification.

## Four-level classification

### Level 1 — Source observation

The episode argues that models may improve chips, kernels, training systems, and future models, and that the important object is the ecosystem around the model rather than model weights alone.

### Level 2 — Strategic hypothesis

Organizations that own and govern their learning loop may compound faster than organizations that merely rent intelligence while losing control of context, corrections, evaluation, and deployment decisions.

### Level 3 — Testable primitive

Before promoting an AI-assisted workflow lesson into a reusable method, run the following review:

1. Who owns the learning generated by this workflow?
2. What evidence enters the learning loop?
3. Which corrections are local, lane-specific, or cross-lane?
4. Who may classify a correction as reusable?
5. Can the workflow explain why it changed?
6. What evaluator or acceptance test determines whether the change helped?
7. What approval is required before the lesson becomes doctrine?

### Level 4 — Preserved operating doctrine

Not yet approved. Preservation requires a bounded test and explicit human approval.

### Proposed strategic question

Does explicit ownership of evidence, correction, evaluation, and preservation improve Singularity Science's ability to convert fast-moving source material into reliable strategic insight?

### Proposed mechanism

Add a receipt at the transition between source analysis and primitive preservation. The receipt forces a separation between what the source said, what the project inferred, what was tested, and what a human approved.

### Expected benefit

- Reduce rhetoric-to-doctrine jumps.
- Make recurrence detection more auditable.
- Make local corrections less likely to become global rules.
- Reduce rediscovery across future source sessions.
- Improve the quality of primitive candidates reaching receiving lanes.

## Review result

### What it clarified

- The episode's RSI claims are useful as a pressure signal, but insufficient as evidence of strong autonomous RSI.
- The project already has a governed recursive-self-enhancement doctrine; the missing bridge is a receipt specifically for research-to-improvement promotion.
- Existing source notes and analyses preserve evidence lineage, but they do not consistently record the post-analysis test, observed result, or preservation decision.
- Learning-loop ownership is a cross-source strategic question, not merely a Moonshots-specific theme. It recurs in model sovereignty, harness design, workflow learning, and organizational acceleration discussions.

### What it failed to clarify

- Whether ownership of a learning loop produces measurable strategic advantage in practice.
- Which future corrections should become shared primitives rather than remain lane-local.
- Whether the proposed receipt reduces operator effort or creates useful additional overhead.
- Whether a model can reliably identify its own evidence and authority limits without human review.

### What remains uncertain

- The episode's model-specific technical claims.
- The causal relationship between open-weight access and organizational learning-loop ownership.
- The minimum receipt fields needed for useful governance without over-instrumentation.
- The recurrence threshold required before preserving a candidate as doctrine.

## Initial preservation decision

Decision: `promote-to-testable-primitive`

Reason: The ownership review is reusable as a source-to-improvement checkpoint, but it has not yet been tested across multiple source analyses or explicitly approved as operating doctrine.

Current status: candidate only.

## Human approval boundary

Only a human may:

- classify a source insight as safe to generalize;
- promote a candidate primitive into shared operating doctrine;
- approve cross-lane routing;
- change verification standards;
- authorize customer or public implications.

The model may assist with recurrence detection, seam comparison, hypothesis generation, counterexamples, test design, and evidence-gap identification. It may not make the preservation decision.
