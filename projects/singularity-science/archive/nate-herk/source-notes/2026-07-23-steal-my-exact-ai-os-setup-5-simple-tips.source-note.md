Why it matters:

The episode offers a practical context-governance model for AI operating systems: make routing explicit, distinguish durable expertise from just-in-time situational context, audit the system read-only, automate freshness, segment growing knowledge, and investigate retrieval failures. The useful object is the control loop around context quality, not the demonstrated setup or promotional offer.

Dominant seams:
- Read-only AIOS audit: inspect routing, indexes, freshness, memory, organization, and context failure modes before proposing changes.
- Expertise versus situational context: keep durable rules and identity in stable context; retrieve customer, project, and other volatile facts just in time.
- Routing, segmentation, and freshness: use a top-level routing table, split distinct knowledge streams, and schedule recurring ingestion where freshness is expected.
- Backtracking after retrieval failure: require the agent to explain where it looked, identify the routing or indexing mistake, and only then update the system.
