## Seam Analysis

### Seam: cost-performance frontier

- Core claim: Kimi K3 is presented as near-frontier and dramatically cheaper.
- Likely durable: Cost and capability should be evaluated together rather than separately.
- Likely speculative: rankings, parameter count, 1% cost, and transfer from GPT-2 optimization to frontier training.
- Verification burden: high; requires primary technical documentation and matched independent evaluation.
- Primitive opportunity: Extend the existing acceleration-source verification gate with cost-per-task and provenance checks.
- Lane-safe routing opportunity: none until the core claims are verified.
- Membrane risk: benchmark rhetoric could become procurement or strategy advice.
- ROI disposition: needs-verification

### Seam: enterprise sovereignty

- Core claim: Open weights may let organizations retain more control over data, tuning, and deployment.
- Likely durable: Deployment location and data ownership are distinct from model quality.
- Likely speculative: universal viability, regulatory permissibility, and comparative advantage.
- Verification burden: high.
- Primitive opportunity: A self-hosting decision checklist covering data sensitivity, licensing, security, operating cost, and fallback paths.
- Lane-safe routing opportunity: ask where a workflow needs provider independence without asserting that a specific model is suitable.
- Membrane risk: could create unreviewed legal, security, or procurement implications.
- ROI disposition: lane-test-ready
