It argues that AI-native execution depends less on isolated tool skill and more on recoding repeatable coordination into durable, agent-readable operating substrate.

Usable claims:

- Roadmaps can become stale coordination containers when build cycles shrink dramatically.
- Product judgment may need to move closer to the artifact rather than staying upstream in scheduled planning.
- Documentation quality increasingly matters because agents act on written operating rules.
- Partial adoption of “go faster” norms without matching substrate changes can create chaos.
