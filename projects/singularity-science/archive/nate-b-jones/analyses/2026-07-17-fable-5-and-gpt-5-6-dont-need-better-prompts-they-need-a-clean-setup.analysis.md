## Seam Analysis

### Seam: harness inventory / map-before-clean

- Core claim: before cleaning or extending a setup, operators need a visible inventory of controls, load points, ownership, and failure modes.
- Likely durable:
  - invisible harness sprawl is a real operating problem
  - “map before change” is a strong governance principle
  - the harness should be inspectable as a system, not only file by file
- Likely speculative:
  - the exact structure and completeness of one commentator’s cleaner workflow
  - whether every operator can map a harness this cleanly in practice
- Verification burden: medium
- Primitive opportunity: candidate for a harness hygiene review gate or stack-inventory primitive
- Lane-safe routing opportunity: not yet; preserve first as a research primitive candidate
- Membrane risk: low-to-medium; this is mostly an internal operating discipline
- ROI disposition: primitive-candidate

### Seam: one-rule-one-home governance

- Core claim: when the same governance or authorship rule is repeated across many skills, the setup drifts and the model receives conflicting truth.
- Likely durable:
  - duplication creates hidden maintenance risk
  - ownership matters for instruction governance
  - many “model failures” are really configuration inconsistencies
- Likely speculative:
  - the exact count and severity of duplicates in the cited setup
  - how often this is the dominant failure mode in cleaner setups
- Verification burden: medium
- Primitive opportunity: candidate for a one-rule-one-home review layer inside harness governance
- Lane-safe routing opportunity: not yet; better preserved as a primitive candidate first
- Membrane risk: low
- ROI disposition: primitive-candidate

### Seam: selective loading / phase-timed context

- Core claim: context depth should arrive when the work needs it rather than front-loading every specialist guide at the start.
- Likely durable:
  - timing of context load affects work quality
  - preserving specialist knowledge does not require loading it all immediately
  - “load later” can improve clarity without deleting useful doctrine
- Likely speculative:
  - the degree of improvement across all models or tasks
  - the best triggering logic for phase-specific loading
- Verification burden: medium
- Primitive opportunity: route into lane reviews about what guidance should load at discovery time versus drafting time versus approval time
- Lane-safe routing opportunity: yes
- Membrane risk: low
- ROI disposition: lane-test-ready

## Boundary

Do not generalize this source into a claim that thinner harnesses are always better or that prompt quality no longer matters. The durable takeaway is narrower: visible harness inventory, load timing, duplicate-rule cleanup, and hard-check design are real operating surfaces that deserve governed review.
