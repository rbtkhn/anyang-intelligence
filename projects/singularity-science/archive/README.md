# Singularity Science Archive

This archive stores internal analysis source material and source notes for the Singularity Science research project.

The archive is not a public publishing surface. It exists so source material can inform product judgment, customer implications, governance warnings, and recursive self-enhancement without being confused with customer deliverables or public claims.

## Boundary

Archive material may be used for:

- internal analysis
- source notes
- synthesis
- research signal tracking
- product implication review
- governance warning review
- customer relevance routing

Archive material may not be used to:

- republish transcripts without rights review
- quote at length in public or customer-facing artifacts
- imply source claims are Anyang Intelligence claims
- bypass attribution, rights, or source review
- move private or sensitive context across membranes

## Storage Rule

Use this structure:

```text
projects/singularity-science/archive/
  innermost-loop/
  moonshots/
  nate-b-jones/
  external-interviews/
```

Raw transcripts and source material stay in this archive. Synthesis and product implications may move into `docs/`, `projects/`, or `skills/` only after membrane review and source discipline.

Contact details in source bodies should be redacted during import. Preserve source references and attribution metadata, but do not preserve raw email addresses or similar direct contact strings inside archived transcript text unless the operator explicitly approves a narrower source-preservation exception.

## Intake Workflow

Use these archive-level intake surfaces for batch transcript landing:

- `transcript-intake-manifest.json`
- `transcript-import-ledger.md`

The manifest is the operator-facing batch intake file. The ledger is the repo-visible completeness surface showing what was imported, blocked, skipped, or still needs a source note.

Transcript import is archive intake only. It does not automatically create source notes, analysis memos, customer implications, or public claims.

## Public Use Rule

When archive material informs public, customer-facing, or reusable product work:

- cite source metadata
- use original synthesis
- avoid long transcript reuse
- preserve rights uncertainty
- route sensitive implications through the relevant membrane
