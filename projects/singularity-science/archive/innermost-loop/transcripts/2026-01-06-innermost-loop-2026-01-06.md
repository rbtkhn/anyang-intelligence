- The hardware substrate of the Singularity is scaling asymptotically. Nvidia has announced its next-generation Vera Rubin GPU platform is now in full production , delivering 50 petaflops of NVFP4 compute and a 10x reduction in inference costs, with Elon Musk confirming he will have the GPUs operating “at scale” in just 9 months . The simulation layer is also deepening. Nvidia’s DLSS 4.5 now uses a 2nd-gen transformer model to generate five frames for every rendered one, enabling 240+ FPS path tracing. The competition is responding. AMD previewed its MI500 GPUs (2-nm process) for 2027, projecting a 1,000x increase in AI performance , while simultaneously launching the Ryzen AI 400 Series for the edge, which boasts 1.3x faster multitasking . Meanwhile, Intel has officially launched Core Ultra Series 3, the first chips built on the Intel 18A process manufactured in the United States.

Matter is becoming a programmable API. Lego has unveiled the “Smart Brick,” a standard 2x4 block containing a 4.1-mm ASIC and local “BrickNet” mesh networking , effectively releasing programmable matter to children. At the industrial scale, the humanoid workforce is coming online: Hyundai plans to deploy 30,000 next-gen Atlas robots annually by 2028, now powered by Google DeepMind’s Gemini Robotics models , while Qualcomm has partnered with Figure to put its new Dragonwing IQ10 processors inside the next wave of androids. Domestic life is also being automated. LG introduced CLOiD, a home robot built on visual-language-action (VLA) models aimed at the “Zero Labor Home.”

The streets are being indexed by silicon. Nvidia has dedicated 20% of its workforce (7,000 people) to autonomous vehicles and released the Alpamayo open-source reasoning models to solve long-tail driving edge cases. Mercedes-Benz is already testing Nvidia’s point-to-point Level 2 system as a direct answer to Tesla FSD. Simultaneously, Lucid, Nuro, and Uber have unveiled their joint robotaxi fleet for the Bay Area.

While hardware scales, the marginal cost of software is collapsing to zero. Elon Musk agrees that “all software is about to be free,” a shift driving potentially imminent hyperdeflation in the sector. Engineers warn that “vibe coding” is now “quietly dying” because Claude Code can one-shot two-month projects in minutes. The application layer, in turn, is dissolving. Microsoft is redesigning its Edge web browser to function as a Copilot app , and Jeff Dean-backed BenchFlow has introduced SkillsBench to more strictly audit agent performance. Media is also becoming generative. Google’s Veo video model is coming to TVs, allowing users to generate media on demand . AMD CEO Lisa Su predicts 5 billion people will use AI daily within five years.

The energy grid is being privatized by compute. xAI has purchased five 380-MW gas turbines to power a new cluster, while Crusoe is now hosting Nvidia GB200s in Iceland powered by geothermal energy . As NIMBYism forces AI data centers out of neighborhoods, developers are increasingly looking to space-based deployment. On the supply side, the DOE awarded $2.7 billion to jumpstart domestic uranium enrichment , and Foxconn reports revenue is up 26.4% driven by AI demand . Storage is catching up: Donut Lab introduced a solid-state battery that charges in 5 minutes , and CATL is deploying sodium-ion batteries at commercial scale this year.

Reality is being patched. Australian researchers are developing a CRISPR Cas13 nasal spray to edit influenza RNA in the lungs, while Portalgraph has demonstrated a touchable 3D projector that injects VR into the real world. Congress has approved a $24.4 billion NASA budget with a new $250 million appropriation for nuclear fission on the moon. The economy is adapting to this volatility: Parcl and Polymarket are launching real estate prediction markets , and Bosch is investing $2.9 billion in AI while cutting 13,000 jobs .
