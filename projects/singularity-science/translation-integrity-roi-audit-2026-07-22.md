# Translation Integrity ROI Audit — 2026-07-22

## Executive judgment

The repository already prevents premature primitive promotion, customer-lane routing without authority, rights-uncertain publication, and live proof work without owner inputs. The audit found credible governance value, but no historical time ledger sufficient to claim realized financial ROI.

Using bounded retrospective estimates, the five-workflow sample suggests a possible net saving of **6.25 operator hours per comparable cycle** after translation-integrity review. This is a hypothesis for live measurement, not realized ROI.

Decision: retain as testable operating gate; do not promote to doctrine yet.

## Audit method

- Scope: five consequential workflows across Singularity Science and operating lanes.
- Evidence mode: repository evidence plus bounded estimates where direct time data is absent.
- Estimate basis: conservative reconstruction from documented hold, review, cleanup, authority, and evidence steps.
- Hour value used for scenario conversion: $100 per operator hour; this is an accounting assumption, not a market rate.
- Avoided rework means work the documented gate would reasonably prevent or defer; it is not proof that the work would otherwise have occurred.

## Observed evidence

- Singularity intake produced a complete, cross-linked packet and preserved rights and routing boundaries.
- Moonshots #272 states that cleanup or rediscovery reduction was not yet measured.
- Media Production identifies hidden cleanup, cost illusion, rights drift, authority drift, and correction cost as evaluation dimensions, but its run receipt is not yet populated.
- Learning Core requires parent authority, drafting authorization, approved evidence, and explicit Hold conditions before drafting.
- Grace Gems remains blocked until owner inputs, delivery records, economics, and usefulness evidence exist.

## What the gate prevented or deferred

- Building from an unverified source claim and later defending it as doctrine.
- Routing research into a customer lane before naming evidence and authority.
- Treating lower generation cost as lower accepted-output cost.
- Drafting child-facing work without parent authority or approved evidence.
- Claim-bearing commercial work without live owner inputs and exact-version approval.

## ROI interpretation

The strongest current ROI is loss avoidance and decision-quality protection, not measured labor savings. The repository does not yet capture actual proposal-to-approval time, cleanup hours, correction hours, defense time, gate completion time, false passes, or unnecessary blocks.

At the bounded estimate of 6.25 net hours per five-workflow cycle, the scenario value is $625 per cycle at $100/hour. Do not report this as realized value until five live applications have observed time and correction data.

## Recommendations

1. Keep the gate at candidate / testable, not adopted doctrine.
2. Require a completed audit receipt for the next five consequential workflows.
3. Record review, cleanup, correction, avoided-work category, and authority/evidence failures.
4. Compare estimates with observed results after the fifth application.
5. Remove fields that do not change a decision or reveal a recurring failure.

## Boundary

This audit is internal research and operating-substrate evidence. It does not establish customer ROI, legal clearance, rights clearance, safety approval, or permission to change any live workflow.
