# Creative Production Operator Readiness Sprint

This seven-day sprint prepares a defensible decision about whether one Creative Production Operator candidate is ready for bounded onboarding.

Preparation status: `Ready to run internally`

The role is still unfilled. Candidate sourcing, outreach, contracting, compensation, hiring, and assignment are not authorized by this sprint. Grace Gems work remains `Hold` until its owner-approved business intake is ready. Run the sprint only after a human separately authorizes a candidate interaction.

## Outcome

By the end of the sprint, produce one candidate readiness decision:

- `Advance to bounded onboarding`
- `Conditional advance`
- `Do not advance`
- `Hold`

This is a production-readiness decision, not a hiring, spending, or client-assignment decision. Advancing a candidate does not authorize paid work, access to client information, publication, customer contact, or production against a live backlog.

## Success Evidence

The sprint succeeds when the reviewer can inspect:

- a completed authority and data-handling gate;
- one time-boxed submission using the [synthetic readiness exercise](creative-production-operator-readiness-exercise.md);
- the candidate's source notes, AI/tool disclosure, self-review, and return packet;
- an independent score against the rubric below;
- a capacity receipt based on observed time, revision behavior, and uncertainty reporting; and
- a signed decision receipt with the next permitted action.

Preparing documents alone is not success. The operating outcome is a reviewable advance, conditional advance, do-not-advance, or hold decision after an authorized candidate run.

Choose `Hold` when the candidate interaction, data boundary, storage location, reviewer, or compensation/contract treatment is unclear. The $500 monthly contractor allocation is a planning constraint, not permission to spend or engage anyone.

Do not provide or request:

- Grace Gems owner answers, customer messages, exact private economics, supplier details, credentials, or private strategy;
- unpublished Predictive History source bodies or credentials;
- access to customer, publishing, payment, analytics, or tenant systems; or
- identity documents or other information not needed for the readiness decision.

Store the completed authority gate, candidate submission, scorecard, and decision receipt in an approved private operating location. Do not commit completed candidate records to Git.

## Seven-Day Sequence

### Day 1: Seal Scope And Authority

- Name the sprint owner and reviewer.
- Complete the authority and data-handling gate.
- Confirm that the synthetic exercise is the only production input.
- Record `Hold` without contacting a candidate if authority is missing.

### Day 2: Calibrate The Evaluation

- Reviewer completes the exercise privately as a calibration pass or records why calibration is unnecessary.
- Confirm the 120-minute time box, allowed tools, deliverables, and critical-failure rules.
- Resolve contradictions before a candidate sees the packet; do not coach toward a preferred creative answer.

### Day 3: Issue The Synthetic Exercise

- Share the unchanged exercise after candidate interaction is authorized.
- Confirm receipt, due window, permitted tools, AI disclosure requirement, and no-spend rule.
- Answer only scope questions and append the same clarification to the review record.

### Day 4: Observe Independent Production

- Candidate works without live-client data or system access.
- Candidate records actual working time, blockers, assumptions, and tool use.
- Reviewer does not rescue the output or supply missing facts; uncertainty handling is part of the test.

### Day 5: Review Quality And Authority Discipline

- Run the submission through [creative-abundance-quality-gate.md](creative-abundance-quality-gate.md).
- Score the initial submission before requesting revisions.
- Record any invented claim, hidden uncertainty, rights issue, unapproved spend, or attempted external action.

### Day 6: Test Revision And Capacity

- If the submission is safe to continue, request one bounded revision with no more than three changes.
- Record revision time, instruction-following, quality improvement, and whether scope expanded.
- Complete the capacity receipt below.

### Day 7: Decide And Bound The Next Step

- Complete the scorecard and decision receipt.
- Separate demonstrated evidence from inference.
- If advancing, authorize only the next bounded onboarding step; run the normal assignment gate before any later task.
- Do not unlock Grace Gems, spend, contract, publish, deliver, or contact customers from this decision.

## Readiness Scorecard

Score observed evidence, not résumé claims or reviewer optimism.

| Dimension | Points | What good evidence shows |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Source and claim discipline | 20 | Uses only supplied facts; labels missing information and assumptions |
| Brief and asset-job clarity | 15 | Connects the work to a precise audience need and reviewable outcome |
| Concept judgment | 15 | Produces distinct, relevant options and explains the recommendation |
| Draft execution | 20 | Creates a clear, usable, editable, source-aligned draft |
| Quality and rights awareness | 15 | Finds weaknesses, flags uncertainty, and applies the quality gate honestly |
| Return-packet discipline | 10 | Packages files, notes, decision request, and next action for review |
| Capacity evidence | 5 | Reports time and constraints credibly without overstating throughput |
| **Total** | **100** | |

Decision rules:

- `Advance to bounded onboarding`: 80 or more, no critical failure, and required evidence complete.
- `Conditional advance`: 70-79, no critical failure, with one or two exact conditions and a review date.
- `Do not advance`: below 70, or a critical failure that makes another readiness run inappropriate.
- `Hold`: evidence, authority, identity, storage, or review integrity is incomplete or conflicted.

Critical failures are inventing a material claim, concealing uncertainty, using identifying client/customer data, spending without approval, representing unlicensed material as cleared, contacting an external party, or publishing/delivering the exercise without authorization.
