# Media Production Permissions And Authority Review

Use this review when a Media Production workflow looks capable, fast, or nearly complete, but the real question is whether it is actually allowed to finish the work safely.

This artifact applies the Singularity Science [Permissions and Authority Review Gate](../singularity-science/primitives/permissions-and-authority-review-gate.md) to Media Production.

Use it between:

- assignment readiness
- draft or packet review
- quality review
- publication or delivery decisions

Its purpose is to stop false completion when orchestration quality outruns real authority.

The quality gate owns whether an asset is clear, useful, source-aware, reviewable, and ready for a human decision. This review owns whether the workflow is permitted to cross an authority boundary. Use the quality gate first for ordinary asset review; use this review when a permission or human-approval boundary is unresolved.

## Use When

Run this review when a workflow or turn:

- produced a strong brief, packet, draft, or revision plan
- appears bottlenecked on rights, approval, spend, or send authority rather than intelligence
- could be mistaken for publish-ready because the output looks polished
- hides manual choke points behind a smooth creative workflow
- is beginning to imply client, contractor, or publication commitment

Common Media Production triggers:

- a contractor packet is strong, but rights or source clearance is unresolved
- a client-facing asset looks ready, but no human has approved the final claim or timing
- a workflow can prepare delivery, but should not send, publish, or commit without review
- a tool or agent can produce options quickly, but still lacks authority to choose, spend, or distribute

## Core Questions

1. What real-world action is this workflow trying to finish?
2. Is the current bottleneck intelligence, or authority?
3. Does the workflow have the source material and rights context it actually needs?
4. Could it safely publish, deliver, assign, spend, or send without a human?
5. Is review still meaningful, or has most of the practical decision already been made?
6. What must always return for human approval in this lane?

## Media Production Authority Surfaces

### Usually Safe To Finish Inside The Workflow

- draft a brief
- assemble concept options
- prepare a review packet
- propose revisions
- surface likely rights or claim issues
- recommend next action

These are workflow-completion decisions, not permission to publish, deliver, spend, clear rights, or commit a contractor.

### Must Return For Human Approval

- publication
- client delivery
- rights or licensing judgment
- final factual or promotional claims
- contractor commitment beyond approved scope
- paid asset, tool, or distribution spend
- timing promises to clients or audiences

## Review Dimensions

| Dimension | What to ask | Typical warning sign |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Rights authority | Are licensing, attribution, likeness, music, image, or reuse questions actually resolved? | The packet looks finished, but the blocking rights judgment is still implicit |
| Publish authority | Can the workflow move content to publication or client delivery? | Draft readiness is treated as shipping readiness |
| Spend authority | Does the workflow imply paid tools, stock assets, contractors, or distribution without approval? | A smooth plan quietly assumes budget authority |
| Contractor authority | Is the Creative Production Operator being asked to decide strategy rather than execute a bounded brief? | The contractor packet starts implying final creative direction |
| Claim authority | Are product, historical, performance, or promotional claims still reviewable by a human? | The workflow locks in a message before source review |
| Fallback clarity | If the workflow stalls, who decides the next move? | A polished packet hides unresolved responsibility |

## Default Decision

Choose one:

- `Clear to continue`
- `Continue, but hold at approval boundary`
- `Hold for authority clarification`
- `Rework workflow`

When in doubt between `Clear to continue` and `Continue, but hold at approval boundary`, choose the latter.

When rights, claims, spend, or client commitment are unresolved, choose `Hold for authority clarification`.

## Boundary

This review does not exist to maximize automation.

It exists to separate:

- workflow friction that can be reduced safely
- from authority that should remain explicitly human in Media Production

Do not use this review to justify publication, client commitment, rights clearance, or spend without human approval.
