# Creative Production Operator Synthetic Readiness Exercise

Use this fixed, claim-neutral exercise only within an authorized [readiness sprint](creative-production-operator-readiness-sprint.md). Everything below is fictional and exists only for evaluation.

Exercise status: `Synthetic — not for publication or sale`

## Working Rules

- Time box: 120 minutes for the initial submission.
- Spend: none. Do not purchase assets, subscriptions, or services.
- Tools: use only tools already available to you and disclose any AI-assisted work.
- External action: do not publish, send, upload to public services, contact anyone, or create an account.
- Sources: use only the facts in this packet. Mark anything else `Missing`.
- Rights: use original geometric shapes, system fonts, and candidate-created work only.
- Storage: return files through the private channel named by the sprint owner; do not commit candidate records to Git.

## Synthetic Source Packet

Fictional project: `Daymark Weekly Desk Pad`

Provided facts:

- Daymark is a fictional printable weekly planning sheet for adults.
- The page contains seven day columns and one end-of-week reflection box.
- The permitted palette is navy, warm gray, and white.
- The intended benefit is simple visibility of weekly priorities.
- The supplied headline is: `See the week. Choose what matters.`
- The supplied call to action is: `Preview the layout.`
- The asset is a concept demonstration, not a product listing.

Missing and prohibited claims:

- price, availability, dimensions, paper quality, shipping, refunds, guarantees, testimonials, productivity results, and customer adoption;
- environmental, medical, therapeutic, or performance claims; and
- any fact not listed above.

## Asset Job

Help an adult understand the fictional layout and its intended use at a glance without implying that the product is available or proven.

## Deliverables

Return one review packet containing:

1. A one-page production brief with audience, asset job, supplied facts, do-not-invent list, and risks.
2. Three meaningfully different square social-graphic concepts described in words or low-fidelity sketches.
3. One 1080 × 1080 draft based on the recommended concept, using only permitted copy and original/right-safe elements.
4. A completed self-review against the Creative Abundance Quality Gate.
5. Source notes, working files or private links, actual working time, AI/tool disclosure, uncertainties, decision needed, recommended next action, and one reuse idea.

File names should begin with `daymark-readiness-` and remain easy for another operator to review and edit.

## Review Boundary

This exercise tests source discipline, creative judgment, review readiness, and capacity reporting. It does not test access to paid tools, live publishing, customer communication, client knowledge, or willingness to work without compensation.

The reviewer may request one bounded revision containing no more than three exact changes. No candidate output becomes a Media Production or client asset without separate human review, rights review, and explicit authorization.
