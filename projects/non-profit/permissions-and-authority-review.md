# Non-Profit Permissions And Authority Review

Use this review when a Non-Profit workflow looks capable, fast, or nearly complete, but the real question is whether it is actually allowed to finish the work safely.

This artifact applies the Singularity Science [Permissions and Authority Review Gate](../singularity-science/primitives/permissions-and-authority-review-gate.md) to Non-Profit.

Use it between:

- packet preparation
- stewardship review
- leadership review
- board review
- donor or grant submission decisions

Its purpose is to stop false completion when orchestration quality outruns real governance authority.

## Use When

Run this review when a workflow or turn:

- produced a strong board packet, donor packet, report draft, grant packet, or stewardship plan
- appears bottlenecked on restriction interpretation, approval, spend, send, or claim authority rather than intelligence
- could be mistaken for submission-ready because the packet looks polished
- hides governance choke points behind a smooth drafting or review workflow
- is beginning to imply donor commitment, grant commitment, board action, spending, or external claim authority

Common Non-Profit triggers:

- a grant report is strong, but one impact claim still has weak evidence
- a donor packet looks ready, but the commitment language has not been leadership-approved
- a stewardship workflow can prepare a packet, but should not send, submit, or commit without review
- a restriction-sensitive issue is packaged well, but restricted-fund or compliance judgment is still unresolved

## Core Questions

1. What real-world action is this workflow trying to finish?
2. Is the current bottleneck intelligence, or authority?
3. Does the workflow have the evidence, restriction notes, and stewardship context it actually needs?
4. Could it safely send, submit, commit, spend, or represent the organization externally without a human?
5. Is review still meaningful, or has most of the practical decision already been made?
6. What must always return for leadership, board, or professional approval in this lane?

## Non-Profit Authority Surfaces

### Usually Safe To Finish Inside The Workflow

- prepare a review packet
- summarize stewardship status
- surface weak evidence or unresolved restrictions
- draft board questions
- propose revisions
- recommend hold, revise, or escalate

### Must Return For Human Approval

- donor or grant submission
- board action
- restricted-fund interpretation
- legal, compliance, or accounting judgment
- external impact claims
- spending commitments
- partnership commitments
- asset disposition or real-estate-sensitive decisions

## Review Dimensions

| Dimension | What to ask | Typical warning sign |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Evidence authority | Are impact claims, numbers, and representations actually supportable? | The packet reads confidently, but the strongest claim is still thin |
| Restriction authority | Are fund restrictions, obligations, or stewardship terms actually resolved? | A draft implies permission that has not been interpreted or approved |
| Submission authority | Can the workflow send a donor, grant, or board-facing packet externally? | Draft readiness is treated as submission readiness |
| Spend authority | Does the workflow imply budget use, reimbursement, grant allocation, or vendor spend without approval? | A clean plan quietly assumes money authority |
| Board authority | Is the packet preparing a decision, or quietly deciding it already? | Board review becomes ceremonial after the real choice is embedded |
| Fallback clarity | If the workflow stalls, who owns the next judgment? | A polished packet hides unresolved governance responsibility |

## Default Decision

Choose one:

- `Clear to continue`
- `Continue, but hold at approval boundary`
- `Hold for authority clarification`
- `Rework workflow`

When in doubt between `Clear to continue` and `Continue, but hold at approval boundary`, choose the latter.

When donor claims, restricted-fund questions, spending, board implications, or external submissions are unresolved, choose `Hold for authority clarification`.

## Worked Example

```text
Non-Profit permissions and authority review:
Workflow or turn:
Grant-report stewardship packet review

Target action:
Prepare a grant packet for leadership review and possible submission

What looks complete:
The reporting packet, stewardship summary, and next-step recommendation are polished

Blocked authority point:
Restricted-fund interpretation and one thin impact claim

Type of permission gap:
Restriction authority and evidence authority

What the workflow can finish safely:
Revise the packet, hold the weak claim, and prepare the escalation question

What must return to human approval:
Restriction interpretation, final submission, and any external impact claim

Main risk if pushed forward:
The packet could create accidental claim overreach or governance drift under deadline pressure

Decision:
Continue, but hold at approval boundary

Required clarification or next step:
Resolve the restriction question, confirm claim evidence, then rerun leadership review before submission

Reviewer:
Non-Profit operator
```

## Boundary

This review does not exist to maximize automation.

It exists to separate:

- governance friction that can be reduced safely
- from authority that should remain explicitly human in Non-Profit

Do not use this review to justify donor commitments, board action, restricted-fund interpretation, spending, or external claims without human approval.
