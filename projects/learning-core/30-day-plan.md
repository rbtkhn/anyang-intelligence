# Learning Core 30-Day Plan

This is the first-real-cycle operating plan for Learning Core. It should help one family use a lightweight, parent-guided `Learning Core` without turning ordinary learning into a program-management burden.

The current lane remains `Hold` until real parent input satisfies the waiting conditions below. Fixtures may test the workflow, but they are not delivery evidence.

## Waiting Conditions

Do not draft a learner-specific plan until:

- Parent or guardian authority is identified.
- The parent has approved drafting from the information provided.
- Save and share boundaries are explicit.
- Basic learner context and at least one concrete learner signal are available.
- Household rhythm and realistic parent support are clear enough to avoid invention.
- Relevant tools are explicitly approved, declined, or left out.
- Any caution or outside-support context is clear enough to plan safely.

Classify the intake as `Ready`, `Provisional`, or `Hold` using the existing readiness checklist. If a waiting condition remains unresolved, preserve `Hold` and name the exact clarification required.

## Before The Month: Intake, Evidence, And Approval

- Collect the bounded parent intake and applicable privacy choices.
- Classify readiness without using fictional or synthetic details as learner evidence.
- Trace each learner-specific plan element in the evidence map to parent input, approved context, or a labeled provisional assumption.
- Draft one light rhythm that fits the family's actual life.
- Keep Khan Academy Kids, a reading basket, detailed daily planning, and other supporting tools optional unless parent approval and learner fit justify them.
- Send the draft and evidence map for parent review.
- Begin use only after the parent approval record confirms what is approved, changed, provisional, or paused.

## Week 1: Establish A Light Rhythm

- Try the smallest approved rhythm that can reveal useful learner signals without overloading the family.
- Let the learner choose one meaningful or proud item from the week.
- Invite the learner to notice one connection between experiences, questions, places, or subjects.
- Ask what the learner might want to try next.
- Preserve only the three weekly traces defined below.
- End with the five-minute parent review.

## Week 2: Repeat Or Adjust Carefully

- Repeat what fit and pause what created avoidable friction.
- Make an ordinary plan adjustment only when two modest signals align, such as a parent observation plus a learner choice or an artifact plus a learner reflection.
- Keep interpretations provisional when evidence is thin.
- Repeat the learner's three small ownership actions and the five-minute parent review.

## Week 3: Look For Coherence

- Notice whether learning in one context connected with a question, activity, or idea from another context.
- Check that no plan, app, portfolio, book, project, or school setting has started acting like the whole `Learning Core`.
- Preserve what is useful for continuity, not a complete record of the learner's life.
- Repeat the learner's three small ownership actions and the five-minute parent review.

## Week 4: Reflect And Choose

- Review the month's selected traces with the parent and learner at an appropriate level.
- Name what connected across contexts and what mattered enough to preserve.
- Name what the learner increasingly noticed, selected, connected, or proposed.
- Choose one clear next direction for the following cycle.
- Leave the family with intelligible artifacts it can continue using without subscription support.

## Three Weekly Traces

Preserve by default:

1. One learner-selected meaningful or proud item.
2. One concise parent observation.
3. One open question or possible next direction.

The parent decides what is saved, shared, omitted, or deleted. Do not expand capture merely because a tool makes capture easy.

## Five-Minute Parent Review

End each week with five decisions:

- **Repeat:** What is worth doing again?
- **Pause:** What should stop or wait?
- **Preserve:** Which three traces are worth carrying forward?
- **Watch:** What remains uncertain or needs more observation?
- **Try next:** What small next move should the parent approve?

Ordinary adjustments should normally rest on two aligned signals. Safety, privacy, authority, overload, or higher-stakes support concerns may trigger an immediate pause or `Hold` without waiting for a second signal.

## Month-End Success

The first cycle succeeds when the family has a clearer next direction and can explain:

- What connected across learning contexts.
- What mattered enough to preserve.
- What the learner increasingly noticed or chose.
- What should guide the next cycle.

Completion does not prove mastery, validate a diagnosis, require a subscription, or mean the system captured everything.
