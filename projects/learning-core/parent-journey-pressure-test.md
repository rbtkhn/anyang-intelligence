# Parent Journey Pressure Test

This document pressure-tests the Learning Core offer as one parent journey:

```text
intake
  -> free 30-day collaborative build
  -> first-month observation
  -> subscription invitation
  -> first continuity month
```

The goal is to see whether the transition into continuity feels natural, useful, and trust-preserving rather than salesy.

## Test Question

Can a parent experience the free 30-day collaborative build as a real act of help, then feel invited into continuity because the next layer is genuinely useful?

If the answer depends on withholding value, manufacturing urgency, or making the free plan feel incomplete on purpose, the model fails the test.

## Fictional Parent Scenario

Use one believable case:

- Parent has a 7-year-old student.
- Main schooling context is public school with significant parent support at home.
- Parent feels the child is bright and curious but school communication, reading confidence, and at-home follow-through feel scattered.
- Parent wants more visibility, calmer routines, and better next-step judgment without replacing the school or overcomplicating the household.

This scenario is useful because it fits the newer lane framing: the Student Operating System adds depth and continuity around an existing education experience.

## Stage 1: Intake

### Parent Need

The parent wants help seeing the child more clearly and turning scattered concerns into a calmer next month.

### What The Lane Does

- Sends the [parent-intake-message.md](parent-intake-message.md).
- Collects household-fit, school-fit, privacy, and learner-signal context.
- Preserves parent authority and marks unclear areas as missing rather than guessed.

### What Must Feel True

- The intake feels like relief, not paperwork.
- The parent feels more understood after answering.
- The lane sounds careful, not diagnostic.
- The lane sounds compatible with school, not hostile to it.

### Failure Mode

The intake starts sounding like a funnel questionnaire built to sell continuity rather than a real attempt to understand the child and household.

## Stage 2: Free 30-Day Build

### Parent Need

The parent wants a usable first month that brings more depth and continuity to the child's real learning life.

### What The Lane Does

- Produces a parent-reviewed 30-day plan.
- Suggests a daily and weekly rhythm.
- Connects school inputs, home observations, reading basket choices, and portfolio evidence.
- Gives the parent a practical first loop for observation and adjustment.
- Collaboratively builds the family's `Learning Core` so it can still be used after the first month.

### What Must Feel True

- The free build is complete enough to use with dignity.
- The parent can act on it without immediately needing paid help.
- The plan makes school and home feel more connected.
- The plan clearly improves visibility and next-step judgment.

### Failure Mode

The free build feels intentionally thin, vague, or obviously designed to force a subscription decision.

## Stage 3: First-Month Observation

### Parent Need

The parent wants help making sense of what the month revealed.

### What The Lane Does

- Uses weekly parent review prompts.
- Uses [monthly-portfolio-review.md](monthly-portfolio-review.md) to convert artifacts into parent-readable insight.
- Pays attention to reading-basket choices, household-fit patterns, school carryover, portfolio evidence, and recurring friction.

### What Must Feel True

- The month generated usable signal.
- The parent can now see patterns that were harder to notice before.
- The Student Operating System made the child and household more legible.
- The next question naturally becomes: what should we do with these patterns now?

### Failure Mode

The first month ends with no visible learning loop, no clearer parent decision, and no reason for continuity beyond generic encouragement.

## Stage 4: Subscription Invitation

### Parent Need

The parent does not need a pitch. The parent needs a clean answer to: what happens next if we want this layer to continue?

### What The Lane Does

The invitation should sound like:

```text
You now have a real first-month picture.

If you want, the next layer is monthly continuity support: a place to bring observations, get help interpreting patterns, refresh the reading basket and plan shape, and keep the system alive without starting from zero.
```

The invitation is strongest when it points to real patterns from month one:

- The child avoided dense books but kept returning to animal nonfiction.
- School reading assignments created friction that changed the home rhythm.
- The portfolio shows proud work in drawing and oral explanation, but thin writing evidence.
- The parent wants help deciding what to simplify next month.

### What Must Feel True

- The invitation names a real next need.
- The paid layer is about continuity and interpretation, not access to basic care.
- The parent can decline without feeling tricked.
- The subscription sounds like the next helpful layer, not the hidden real product.

### Failure Mode

The invitation appears before the month produced real evidence, or it leans on pressure, scarcity, or anxiety.

## Stage 5: First Continuity Month

### Parent Need

The parent wants the system to stay alive without rebuilding everything from scratch.

### What The Lane Does

- Gives chat-group access for bounded questions.
- Delivers one premium newsletter.
- Delivers one personalized consultation touchpoint.
- Produces one monthly recommendation refresh.
- Interprets how school inputs, home observations, reading-basket choices, and portfolio evidence should change next moves.

### What Must Feel True

- The continuity month creates a visible improvement over using the free plan alone.
- The parent receives interpretation, not just more content.
- The monthly touchpoints feel calm and sustainable.
- The child remains parent-mediated rather than directly managed by the system.

### Failure Mode

The continuity month turns into vague community chatter, hidden unlimited support, or generic advice that ignores the actual child and household.

## Natural Conversion Signals

The subscription invitation is most justified when one or more of these become visible:

- The parent wants help adapting the next month, not just repeating the first one.
- School and home signals need interpretation together.
- Reading-basket and portfolio patterns suggest useful next steps.
- The parent wants continuity without rebuilding context.
- The household needs lighter planning with better judgment.

These are signs of authentic demand for continuity, not just commercial opportunity.

## Salesy Conversion Signals

The model is drifting in the wrong direction when:

- The free plan is weakened to preserve paid upsell.
- The parent is reminded about the subscription before month-one insight exists.
- The invitation is mostly feature language with no evidence from the child or household.
- The parent feels that safety, responsiveness, or basic usefulness are locked behind payment.
- The lane talks more about access than about clearer parent decisions.

## Pressure-Test Verdict

The transition works when:

- Intake produces trust.
- The free month produces real clarity.
- Observation produces a visible pattern.
- The invitation names the exact continuity value revealed by that pattern.
- The first continuity month helps the parent make a better next decision than they could have made alone.

The transition fails when the subscription is asked to compensate for a weak first month.

## Recommended Follow-On Artifact

If this pressure test holds, the next operational surface should be a parent-facing or operator-facing continuity invitation template that:

- cites month-one evidence
- names the continuity layer in plain language
- explains what changes in month two
- keeps the tone helpful rather than sales-driven
