# Subscription Boundary

This document defines the commercial boundary between the **free 30-day collaborative build of the family's `Learning Core`** and the **monthly continuity subscription** in the Learning Core lane.

The goal is to keep the offer understandable, humane, and operationally sustainable.

## Boundary Decision

The first 30-day build is the trust-building entry experience.

It should let a parent experience the Student Operating System in a bounded way before making any ongoing commitment.

It should also leave the family with a `Learning Core` they can operate independently if they choose not to continue into the subscription layer.

The `Learning Core` belongs operationally to the family. Ending the subscription must not remove the family's plans, selected learning memory, portfolio structure, questions, or ability to continue the experience-notice-connect-preserve-reflect-choose loop.

That experience may sit alongside public school, homeschool, or a hybrid arrangement. The lane's job is to add depth and continuity, not to pretend every family starts from the same schooling structure.

The monthly continuity subscription begins only if the parent wants continuing guidance, interpretation, and higher-touch support after that first month.

## Free 30-Day Build Includes

The free entry build should include:

- One parent-reviewed 30-day learning plan for a new student.
- One learner context summary based on parent-provided inputs.
- One onboarding sequence for the first week.
- One suggested daily and weekly rhythm.
- One reading-basket recommendation shape.
- One Khan Academy Kids use rule when the app is part of the household setup.
- One portfolio evidence plan.
- One weekly parent review prompt set.
- One next-step recommendation at the end of the month.
- One companion second-brain structure the family can continue using independently.

The free build is allowed to be thoughtful and useful. It should not feel fake, crippled, or manipulative.

## Free 30-Day Build Does Not Include

The free entry build should not assume:

- Ongoing weekly custom planning beyond the first month.
- Unlimited revision cycles.
- Open-ended family consultation.
- Continuous operator monitoring.
- Guaranteed response access in a group or direct-support channel.
- Premium newsletter archive or premium update stream.
- Early feature access.

If the free build quietly includes the subscription layer, then the boundary is not real and the offer will be hard to sustain.

## Monthly Continuity Subscription Includes

The monthly continuity subscription is for families who want ongoing help interpreting and strengthening their own Learning Core after the first month. It supports continuity; it does not own the family's continuity or make the learner's coherent life of learning dependent on Anyang Intelligence.

The continuity layer may include:

- Chat-group participation.
- Premium newsletters.
- Personalized ongoing consultation.
- Early access to new upgrades and features.
- Recurring interpretation of learner patterns, parent observations, reading-basket choices, and portfolio signals.
- Monthly recommendation refreshes based on what the learner chose, resisted, repeated, requested, or outgrew.
- Ongoing help turning observation into the next parent-reviewed move.

## Core Value Of The Subscription

The subscription is not mainly payment for one more document.

Its value is ongoing operating support:

- The family does not have to restart from zero every month.
- Parent observations become interpreted, not just stored.
- The plan adapts from real learner signals over time.
- The family gains continuity, perspective, and earlier access to product improvements.
- The learner can increasingly participate in selecting what matters, making connections, reflecting, and choosing next directions.

## Subscription Trigger

The subscription invitation should happen when the parent has already experienced a useful first month and a natural need for continuity appears.

Good trigger signals include:

- The parent wants help adjusting the next month.
- The learner's reading-basket choices reveal patterns that deserve follow-up.
- Portfolio evidence is accumulating and the parent wants interpretation, not just storage.
- Household constraints changed and the plan needs adaptation.
- The parent wants an easier ongoing review rhythm.
- The family wants access to the group, premium updates, or early feature releases.

The offer should be presented as the next helpful layer, not as pressure attached to the free plan.

## Operator Guardrails

Operators should keep these boundaries explicit:

- Do not weaken the free plan so much that trust cannot form.
- Do not promise white-glove support if the subscription cannot sustain it.
- Do not hide the transition point between free and paid support.
- Do not use child-safety, urgency, or parent anxiety as conversion pressure.
- Do not place pricing or subscription language inside the child-facing or parent-facing plan body unless that surface is intentionally commercial.
- Do not imply that cancellation erases, disables, or invalidates the family's Learning Core.
- Do not make essential learning memory proprietary to the subscription; leave the family with portable, intelligible artifacts it can continue using.

## Evidence To Validate

This offer model is working only if the lane can observe:

- Parents actually use the free 30-day plan.
- Parents understand what changes when the subscription begins.
- Some families naturally want continuity after the first month.
- The subscription workload is operationally sustainable.
- The continuity layer creates better retention, better parent confidence, or better next-month decisions.

If those signals do not appear, the boundary or the offer may need redesign.

## Recursive Self-Enhancement Link

This boundary should improve over time through governed learning:

```text
free 30-day plan used
  -> parent friction or continuation request appears
  -> pattern is named
  -> offer boundary is clarified
  -> subscription surface improves
  -> next family experiences a cleaner transition
```

The offer should not rely on chat memory alone. It should be updated through repo-visible doctrine when real parent behavior reveals a better boundary.
