# 30-Day Personalized Learning Plan Template

This template is the parent-facing shell for the **30-Day Personalized Learning Plan** for new students, including onboarding.

Use it after the inputs in [30-day-plan-inputs.md](30-day-plan-inputs.md) are collected or clearly marked as missing. The plan should feel like a warm parent coach: practical, calm, and easy to adapt.

Do not include pricing, retainer, subscription, or internal skill language in the completed parent-facing plan.

The planning logic is:

```text
observe
  -> gather insight
  -> recommend next move
  -> parent reviews
  -> system updates
```

The draft should preserve that loop rather than pretending the first plan is the final answer.

## Parent Authority And Safety

The parent or guardian approves this plan before use.

Parent approval is needed for:

- Daily rhythm.
- Screen-time budget for learning activities, if the parent sets one.
- Learning resources and activities.
- Child-facing prompts.
- Khan Academy Kids use.
- Reading basket choices.
- Portfolio evidence to save.
- Watch items or support signals.
- Any outside-support question.
- What information may be saved or shared.

This plan does not replace the parent, teacher, tutor, clinician, legal authority, or specialist. It does not diagnose, grade, prove mastery, or satisfy legal/accountability requirements by itself.

## Khan Academy Kids Use Rule

Default:

- Use for `[15-20 minutes]` on learning days.
- Parent-supervised.
- Fit inside the parent screen-time budget if one exists.
- Treat it as an observation and practice tool.
- Do not treat app progress as a grade, diagnosis, full curriculum, or proof of mastery.

Parent should notice:

- What the child chooses.
- What feels easy.
- What causes frustration.
- What the child repeats.
- What questions or interests appear afterward.

## Portfolio Evidence Plan

Evidence collection is parent-chosen case by case.

Save evidence when it helps the parent or child see learning clearly:

- Proud work.
- A milestone.
- A meaningful question.
- A photo of physical work.
- A drawing, writing sample, math page, or project artifact.
- A parent observation note.
- A child reflection, if low-pressure.

Suggested rhythm:

- Save 1-3 useful items per week.
- Ask the child what they are proud of.
- Avoid turning the portfolio into paperwork.

## Weekly Parent Review

Once per week, answer:

- What did the child enjoy?
- What did the child resist?
- What felt too easy?
- What felt too hard?
- What should we repeat?
- What should we pause?
- What evidence should go in the portfolio?
- What did the child feel proud of?
- What should we watch next week?

## First Monthly Portfolio Review

At the end of the month, prepare a short parent-readable review:

```text
This month we noticed:

Proud work:

Milestones:

Growth evidence:

Recurring interests:

Friction or support needs:

Recommended next steps:

Portfolio pieces to keep:

Parent decision needed:
```

## Watch Items And Support Signals

Use a moderate risk posture: note patterns carefully without alarm.

Watch for:

- Persistent reading frustration.
- Persistent writing resistance.
- Persistent math confusion.
- Attention, fatigue, behavior, speech/language, health, or developmental concerns.
- Strong avoidance or distress.
- Anything the parent names as important.

If a concern persists or feels high-stakes, the next safe action is parent review and possible support from a teacher, tutor, clinician, specialist, attorney, district, or other qualified human support.

## Parent Decisions Needed

Before using the plan, decide:

- Does this rhythm feel realistic?
- Does the screen-time fit match the parent's budget?
- Does the reading basket fit the child?
- Is the Khan Academy Kids rule acceptable?
- What portfolio evidence should be saved?
- What information should remain private?
- What should trigger a pause or outside support question?
- What should be repeated or adjusted next month?

## Next-Month Options

At the end of the month, choose one:

- Continue the rhythm with small adjustments.
- Refresh the reading basket.
- Build a deeper learner profile.
- Focus next month on reading, math, writing, projects, portfolio, or routine.
- Request a revised 30-day plan.
- Pause and collect more evidence before deciding.

The best next step is the one that helps the parent see and support the actual child more clearly.
