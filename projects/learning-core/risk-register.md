# Learning Core Risk Register

This register tracks the main operating and governance risks for Learning Core.

Review cadence: weekly during active cycles, event-driven for intake or safety changes, and before any child-facing use.

| Risk | Consequence | Owner | Current posture | Next review |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Drafting begins before authority, privacy, or learner context is clear | The plan becomes a polished guess and weakens parent authority | Parent or guardian approval boundary; Anyang Intelligence operator | Preserve `Hold` until every waiting condition is resolved; then use `Ready` / `Provisional` / `Hold` gating | Each new intake |
| Learning activities remain fragmented across contexts | The family completes tasks without building understanding, continuity, or direction | Parent with operator support | Use the weekly coherence question to identify one real connection or unfinished thread | Weekly review |
| Capture expands because tools make it easy | Private learner context is over-preserved and useful signals disappear inside volume | Parent or guardian approval boundary | Default to three weekly traces and keep save, share, omission, and deletion under parent control | Weekly review and each tool change |
| One plan, app, portfolio, book, project, school setting, or service becomes the whole system | The tool begins driving the learner's life instead of supporting it | Parent with operator support | Review tool subordination and remove or simplify surfaces that do not serve an approved learner need | Weekly review |
| Learner ownership is introduced too quickly or treated as independent authority | The learner receives burdens or decisions beyond developmental fit and parent boundaries weaken | Parent or guardian | Limit the first cycle to choosing one item, noticing one connection, and suggesting one next step | Weekly review |
| Ordinary plan changes rest on a single weak or app-only signal | The plan overreacts and turns thin evidence into learner claims | Anyang Intelligence operator with parent approval boundary | Require two aligned signals for ordinary adjustments; keep interpretations provisional | Each weekly adjustment |
| Child-facing language is used without explicit parent approval | Parent authority and child safety boundaries are weakened | Parent or guardian approval boundary | Keep child-facing prompts behind explicit parent review | Before plan use |
| App activity is treated as proof of mastery or diagnosis | Learning evidence becomes misleading and unsafe | Anyang Intelligence operator with parent approval boundary | Keep evidence mapping and no-diagnosis framing explicit | Each draft review |
| Save/share boundaries drift across artifacts | Private child or family context could leak or be over-preserved | Parent or guardian approval boundary | Keep privacy and sharing decisions explicit in intake, evidence map, and approval record | Each plan review |
| Subscription support becomes the owner of family continuity | Ending service makes the family's learning memory or next direction unusable | Learning Core offer owner | Leave the family with intelligible plans, selected traces, reflections, and choices it can continue independently | Month end and offer review |
| Commercial claims exceed approved accounting or adoption evidence | The lane presents an offer hypothesis or unverified payment record as validated revenue | Learning Core offer owner | Label the retainer record evidence-pending and the subscription an unvalidated offer hypothesis | Each commercial update |
| Embodied or ambient child-facing AI is normalized through convenience | Hidden authority leak and surveillance pressure increase | Parent or guardian approval boundary | Keep embodied-AI policy and worksheet at `Hold` by default | Before any tool review |

Safety, privacy, authority, overload, and higher-stakes support concerns may trigger an immediate pause or `Hold`; they do not need two aligned signals.
