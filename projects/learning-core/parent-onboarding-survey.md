# Pseudonymous Parent Intake Preview

This optional preview helps a parent prepare for the two-part [evidence-first intake conversation](parent-intake-message.md). It is not a registration form and does not replace the authority, privacy, or readiness review.

Do not enter a learner name, parent name, email, phone number, precise location, school name, diagnosis, or private history. Use a parent-approved learner label and broad context only.

## Parent Instructions

Short answers are enough. A recent example is usually more useful than a general label. Write `not sure yet` when a decision is still open.

### Authority Preview

1. Parent-approved learner label:
2. Do you authorize intake and readiness classification from your answers? `[yes / no / not yet]`
3. Who approves a future plan before use?
4. What must not be saved or shared?
5. Are any tools, topics, activities, or approaches excluded?
6. Is there any caution or outside-support context whose minimum planning implication should be discussed privately?

If these answers reveal an unclear authority, privacy, or safety boundary, the operator pauses at `Hold` and asks only for the missing decision.

### Evidence Preview

1. What would you like to understand better about the learner this month?
2. What is already working that should not be disrupted?
3. Describe one recent learning moment that went well. What did the learner choose, notice, ask, build, repeat, or connect?
4. Describe one recent moment that stalled. What happened before it, and what helped?
5. On a difficult household day, what amount of learning is still sustainable?
6. What evidence would help you understand the month, and what would feel like paperwork?
7. What do you hope the learner can increasingly notice, choose, or carry forward independently?

## Operator Use

- Use these answers to shorten, not bypass, the guided conversation.
- Verify authority and privacy live before discussing learner-specific detail.
- Ask only follow-ups that could change readiness, first-week rhythm, approved resources, evidence rules, or pause conditions.
- Treat the preview as temporary working state until the parent verifies a pseudonymous summary.
- Keep real family information out of Git. Store an approved case record only in the operator-controlled tenant-private store.

Do not treat this preview as diagnosis, grade placement, proof of mastery, drafting permission, or plan-use approval.
