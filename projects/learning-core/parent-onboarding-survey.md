# Parent Onboarding Survey

This is a parent registration and onboarding survey for a new student entering the Student Operating System.

Its job is to gather basic student registration details first, then collect the minimum safe planning inputs in a lighter format than the full intake message.

Use it before drafting the **30-Day Personalized Learning Plan**.

## Purpose

This survey helps Learning Core quickly learn:

- what kind of situation the student is currently in
- what kind of learner context is already visible
- how much support the student usually needs
- what kind of daily rhythm is realistic
- which starter resources are in bounds
- what caution or outside-support signals matter

The survey is not a diagnosis tool, a grade-placement test, or a substitute for parent judgment.

## Parent Instructions

Please choose the answer that feels closest to your situation right now.

You do not need perfect answers. We use this to draft a thoughtful first-month plan, not to label your child.

If more than one answer fits, choose the closest one and add a short note afterward if needed.

## Part 2: 10-Question Onboarding Survey

### 1. Which situation feels most true for this student right now?

- A. Generally cooperative, but learning feels scattered and could use more structure
- B. Strengths and interests are visible, but follow-through changes a lot day to day
- C. Friction, avoidance, or fatigue shows up regularly in parts of learning
- D. The learner picture is still emerging and we mainly need a first month of observation

### 2. What kind of planning help is most needed right now?

- A. Turn a scattered situation into a simple first-month plan
- B. Build a steadier rhythm that is easier to maintain
- C. Match learning activities better to the child's interests, energy, and friction points
- D. Create a clearer way to track progress, proud work, and next-step decisions

### 3. Which schooling context best fits this student right now?

- A. Public school with home support around it
- B. Homeschool
- C. Hybrid or mixed arrangement
- D. Transitioning or not fully settled yet

### 4. Which learner picture feels most true right now?

- A. We already know clear interests, what the child enjoys, and at least one learning mode that usually works well
- B. We know some strengths and frustrations, but the best rhythm, triggers, or strongest interests still vary a lot
- C. We mostly know what the child resists, avoids, or gets tired by, and need the first month to discover better fits
- D. We do not yet have enough clear information about interests, strengths, frustration patterns, or learning preferences to plan confidently

### 5. How much support does this student usually need to keep going once a learning activity starts?

- A. Usually works independently once started
- B. Needs help getting started, then can often continue
- C. Needs frequent check-ins or redirection
- D. Needs close support most of the time

### 6. What kind of daily rhythm is most realistic right now?

- A. Light and simple
- B. Moderate and steady
- C. Fuller and more structured
- D. Variable from day to day

### 7. What should the first month avoid most?

- A. Too much screen time or passive app use
- B. Too much worksheet-style or sit-still work
- C. Too much parent prep, complexity, or paperwork
- D. Too much pressure, frustration, or overload

### 8. What is your current Khan Academy Kids decision?

- A. Approved as a small parent-supervised support block
- B. Maybe, but I want guidance before deciding
- C. Not using it for now
- D. Already using it and want it fit into the plan carefully

### 9. What reading situation best describes the student right now?

- A. Usually picks up books willingly and can stay with reading without much strain
- B. Will read in some situations, but interest, stamina, or confidence changes a lot by book or day
- C. Often avoids reading, gets frustrated quickly, or needs substantial support to keep going
- D. Cannot tell yet and need more observation across different books and moments

### 10. Which caution signal best fits right now?

- A. Normal first-month planning is fine; no special concern is top of mind
- B. Keep the plan flexible because friction, fatigue, or inconsistency is likely
- C. Handle specific concerns carefully, such as reading frustration, attention, emotional overload, or resistance
- D. Existing outside-support context may need to be considered, such as teacher, tutor, clinician, specialist, or school feedback

## Readiness Interpretation

Use the survey to support these default classifications:

- `Ready`:
  - schooling context is clear enough to plan around
  - learner picture is usable without invention
  - student support level is clear enough to choose realistic activities
  - rhythm reality is clear enough to draft a workable first month
  - Khan Academy Kids stance is explicit
  - caution signal does not hide unresolved outside-support or boundary questions

- `Provisional`:
  - the core picture is usable
  - some learner-fit details are still emerging
  - the first month can safely serve as a discovery month if assumptions stay visible

- `Hold`:
  - learner picture is too thin to draft without invention
  - rhythm reality is too unclear to design a usable month
  - outside-support context or other caution boundaries need clarification before drafting

## Boundary

Do not treat survey answers as:

- diagnosis
- grade placement
- proof of mastery
- permission to save or share information beyond parent approval

The survey is a registration and onboarding surface. Parent authority still governs what is drafted, what is saved, and what is used.
