# Evidence-First Parent Intake Conversation

Use this parent-facing conversation before drafting a **30-Day Personalized Learning Plan**. It is designed as two short conversations, not an enrollment form.

The intake collects the smallest amount of information that can change a planning decision. Ask for recent examples rather than labels, preserve what already works, and leave optional tools optional.

## Before The Conversation

- Use a parent-approved learner label, not a real name.
- Do not collect contact details, precise locations, school names, diagnoses, or private histories in this surface.
- Treat the conversation as temporary working state until the parent reviews the summary.
- Keep real family information out of Git. An approved case record belongs only in the operator-controlled tenant-private store.
- Intake permission does not authorize drafting, child-facing questions, plan use, or evidence saving.

## Opening

Hi,

I would like to understand the smallest useful starting picture for your family's first Learning Core month. We will begin with your authority and privacy boundaries, then talk about a few recent learning moments and the rhythm your household can actually sustain.

You remain the authority over the plan, child-facing language, tools, what is preserved, what is shared, and whether outside support is needed. Short answers are enough, and `not sure yet` is always acceptable.

## Conversation 1: Authority And Privacy

Target: 8–10 minutes. Stop at 12 minutes rather than pressuring a boundary decision.

1. What parent-approved label should we use for the learner?
2. Do you authorize this intake and a readiness classification from your answers?
3. Who must approve:
   - a plan draft;
   - child-facing questions;
   - plan use;
   - saved learner evidence?
4. What may be discussed during intake?
5. What, if anything, may be preserved in a pseudonymous summary?
6. What must not be saved?
7. May anything be shared beyond you and the operator?
8. Are any tools, topics, activities, or approaches excluded?
9. Is there safety, health, developmental, emotional, legal, accountability, or outside-support context that affects planning?
10. If so, what minimum planning implication may be considered?

If authority, privacy, save/share rules, or caution boundaries remain unclear, pause at `Hold`. Do not move into learner-specific questions merely to create momentum.

## Conversation 2: Evidence-First Learner Fit

Target: 15–20 minutes. Stop at 25 minutes and defer lower-value detail.

### Direction

1. What would you like to understand better about your child by the end of this month?
2. What would make the month feel useful even if no academic level changed?
3. What should the plan avoid?
4. What is already working that the plan should not disrupt?

### Concrete Learning Signals

5. Tell me about one recent learning moment that went surprisingly well.
6. What did the child choose, notice, ask, build, repeat, or carry into another setting?
7. Tell me about one recent moment that became difficult or stalled.
8. What happened immediately before the friction?
9. What helped it ease, if anything?
10. What is the child proud of, interested in, or returning to right now?

Describe what happened before interpreting why. One event is a signal, not a fixed learner label.

### Learning Across Contexts

11. Where does learning already happen naturally—school, home, books, play, conversation, outdoors, travel, relationships, or projects?
12. Has the child recently connected something learned in one setting to another?
13. What kinds of choices is the child ready to make now?
14. What still needs close parent structure or support?

### Household Fit

15. On a difficult household day, what amount of learning remains genuinely sustainable?
16. What does a normal day allow?
17. Is a fixed rhythm or flexible menu more realistic?
18. What signs tell you to shorten, pivot, or pause?
19. What parent preparation burden is sustainable?
20. Is there a screen-time boundary, and must all learning apps remain inside it?

### Tools And Resources

21. Which books, materials, spaces, curriculum, or current projects are already easy to use?
22. Which tools or approaches are approved, excluded, or undecided?
23. Is Khan Academy Kids approved, declined, or undecided?
24. Is a reading basket useful now, or should it remain optional?
25. Are detailed daily plans helpful, burdensome, or unnecessary?

Ask only for resources that could change the first-week plan. Do not build an exhaustive inventory.

### Evidence And Independence

26. What evidence would genuinely help you understand the month?
27. What would merely feel like paperwork or excessive observation?
28. Who decides what is preserved or deleted?
29. Should the learner choose one meaningful artifact each week?
30. What should trigger an immediate pause or outside-support review?
31. At the end of the month, what do you hope the child can increasingly notice, choose, or carry forward without depending on this service?

The light default is three weekly traces:

1. One learner-selected item.
2. One concise parent observation.
3. One open question or next direction.

The parent may narrow this further.

## Optional Learner Voice

Target: 5–7 minutes, only after the parent approves the questions.

Ask no more than:

1. What are you interested in learning about right now?
2. What is something you feel proud of?
3. What might you like to make, explore, or try next?

The learner may skip anything. Stop if attention, comfort, or interest falls. The parent decides whether any response is preserved. Learner participation is optional and does not block an otherwise sufficient parent intake.

## What Happens Next

The operator prepares a short pseudonymous summary and classifies the intake as `Ready`, `Provisional`, or `Hold`. For `Ready` or `Provisional`, the operator also prepares a separate proposed learner profile. The parent corrects the summary, decides what may be preserved, and reviews any complete profile separately.

- `Hold` leads only to the missing clarification request.
- An initial profile becomes approved only when the named guardian explicitly approves its exact contents.
- An approved profile remains `Awaiting Persistence` until an authorized operator confirms it was preserved in the private store.
- `Ready` or `Provisional` with a confirmed-effective profile leads to a separate question asking whether the parent authorizes an evidence-mapped 30-day draft.

Classification, profile approval, private persistence, drafting permission, and plan-use approval are separate decisions.
