# Abigail Adaptive Intake Clarification

This synthetic fixture demonstrates how [adaptive intake clarification](phase-2-survey-generation.md) closes only the gaps that could change readiness or the first-week plan.

The fictional parent has already completed the authority and privacy gate. Authority, save/share rules, and caution boundaries are clear. The case is `Provisional` because the initial answers describe general preferences but contain no concrete learning episode and household capacity remains thin.

## Parent Framing

Hi,

Thank you. I do not need another full survey. Two short areas would help me classify this intake without guessing.

### Concrete Learning Signal

1. Tell me about one recent learning moment that went surprisingly well. What did Abigail choose, notice, ask, build, repeat, or connect?
2. Tell me about one recent moment that stalled. What happened immediately before it, and what helped it ease?

### Household Reality

3. On a difficult household day, what amount of learning remains genuinely sustainable?
4. What does an ordinary day allow, and how much preparation feels realistic?
5. What signs tell you to shorten, pivot, or pause?

## Reclassification Logic

- Move to `Ready` if the answers provide concrete usable signals and a realistic household boundary without introducing a new authority, privacy, tool, or caution gap.
- Keep `Provisional` if all waiting conditions are clear but the learner evidence remains thin enough that assumptions must stay visible.
- Move to `Hold` if the answers reveal an unresolved overload, safety, privacy, authority, or outside-support boundary.
