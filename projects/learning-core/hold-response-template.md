# Hold Response Template

Use this template when parent intake is classified as `Hold` by [onboarding-readiness-checklist.md](onboarding-readiness-checklist.md).

The goal is to pause safely without alarm, ask only for the missing decisions needed to proceed, and preserve parent authority.

Do not use this as a diagnosis, grade, legal/accountability determination, proof-of-mastery statement, or customer-pricing message.

## When To Use

Use a `Hold` response when drafting the 30-day plan would require Anyang Intelligence to guess about:

- who approves the plan
- what may be saved or shared
- the child's basic starting context
- the family's schedule or parent-time reality
- child-facing communication
- high-stakes support concerns
- legal, district, evaluator, homeschool, or accountability requirements
- whether app use or portfolio evidence may be treated only as observation, not proof

## Parent-Facing Template

Hi,

Thank you for sending this. I have enough to see that a 30-day plan could be useful, but I do not want to draft it yet because a few authority, privacy, or context details need to be clear first.

I am pausing here so the plan stays accurate, safe, and parent-led. I do not want to guess about your child, your schedule, what should be saved, or what kind of support or accountability context matters.

Could you answer the relevant questions below? Short answers are fine, and `not sure yet` is okay.

## Missing Decisions Needed

### Approval

- Who should approve the plan before it is used?
- Are there any parent or guardian decision boundaries I should know before drafting?

### Privacy And Records

- What information may be saved?
- What information should not be saved?
- What information, if any, may be shared outside the household?
- Who decides what portfolio evidence is kept?

### Goal And Accountability

- Is the first-month goal a gentle learning rhythm, a specific skill focus, support for an outside requirement, or something else?
- Are there district, evaluator, homeschool, reporting, legal, or accountability requirements you want considered?
- If an outside requirement matters, what source or instruction should I use?

### Schedule And Parent Time

- Which days are realistic learning days?
- What time of day usually works best?
- How much parent time is realistic on a light day?
- How much parent time is realistic on a fuller day?

### Learner Starting Context

- What does your child enjoy or feel proud of right now?
- What feels hard, frustrating, or avoided?
- What books, topics, games, animals, places, activities, or projects does your child like?
- What resources do you already have available?

### Apps, Portfolio, And Evidence

- Do you approve using Khan Academy Kids only as a parent-supervised practice and observation tool?
- Do you agree that app activity should not be treated as a grade, diagnosis, full curriculum, or proof of mastery?
- What kinds of portfolio evidence are okay to save: photos, worksheets, drawings, recordings, screenshots, notes, projects, questions, or milestones?

### Support Concerns

- Are there reading, writing, math, attention, fatigue, behavior, speech/language, health, developmental, emotional, or other concerns you want handled carefully?
- Are there teacher, tutor, clinician, specialist, evaluator, attorney, district, or other qualified support notes you want considered?
- If yes, what may be used for planning?

Once these pieces are clear, I can classify the plan as either `Ready` or `Provisional` and draft only from parent-approved inputs.

The goal is not to slow you down. The goal is to make sure the plan supports the actual child in front of you while keeping you in the authority role.

## Short Version

Use this when the parent needs a lighter reply:

```text
Thank you. I am going to pause before drafting the 30-day plan because a few authority, privacy, or context details are not clear enough yet.

To keep the plan accurate and parent-led, can you clarify:

- who approves the plan before use
- what may be saved or shared
- what the first-month goal is
- what schedule and parent time are realistic
- what your child enjoys, avoids, or feels proud of
- whether Khan Academy Kids may be used only as parent-supervised practice/observation
- who decides what portfolio evidence is kept
- whether any outside support or accountability context should be considered

Short answers are fine. Once these are clear, I can draft a Ready or Provisional plan without guessing.
```

## Operator Checklist Before Sending

Before sending a `Hold` response, remove questions that are already answered.

Keep the response:

- calm
- short enough to answer
- specific to the missing inputs
- free of pricing, retainer, or subscription language
- free of diagnosis, grading, proof-of-mastery, legal/accountability conclusions, or app-mastery claims

If the parent named a high-stakes concern, do not minimize it. Ask what qualified support context may be considered and preserve parent authority over next steps.
