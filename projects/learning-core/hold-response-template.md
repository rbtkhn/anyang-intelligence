# Evidence-First Hold Response

Use this template when [onboarding-readiness-checklist.md](onboarding-readiness-checklist.md) classifies an intake as `Hold`.

The goal is to pause safely and request only the unresolved decision or fact. Do not send every question below.

## Parent-Facing Opening

Hi,

Thank you. I am pausing before any plan draft so I do not guess about your child, your household, or a boundary that belongs to you.

The intake currently remains `Hold` because:

- `[state the exact unresolved condition in neutral language]`

To continue safely, could you clarify only the relevant item below? A short answer or `not sure yet` is fine.

## Select Only The Missing Block

### Authority

- Who holds the unresolved approval decision?
- Do you authorize the next named action?

### Privacy Or Preservation

- May this information be discussed or used for planning?
- What must not be preserved or shared?
- Who decides whether the pseudonymous summary may be retained?

### Concrete Learner Signal

- What happened during one recent learning moment that went well or stalled?
- What did the learner do, and what happened immediately before or after?

### Household Feasibility

- What can a difficult household day genuinely sustain?
- What preparation burden and pause signals should the plan respect?

### Tool Boundary

- Is the relevant tool approved, excluded, or undecided?
- If undecided, should it simply remain out of the first-week plan?

### Evidence Boundary

- What evidence would help, and what would feel excessive?
- Who decides what is preserved or deleted?

### Caution Or Outside Support

- What minimum planning implication may be considered?
- What should remain outside the plan?
- What should trigger an immediate pause or qualified-support review?

## Closing

Once this boundary is clear, I will reclassify the intake as `Ready`, `Provisional`, or `Hold`. Classification will not authorize drafting; I will ask separately before preparing a 30-day draft.

## Operator Check

Before sending:

- remove every block that is already answered;
- keep the request to 1–5 questions;
- do not request identifiers or unnecessary private history;
- do not diagnose, grade, infer mastery, or make legal/accountability conclusions;
- preserve the parent's authority to leave an optional tool undecided and omitted.
