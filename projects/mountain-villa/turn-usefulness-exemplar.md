# Mountain Villa Turn Exemplar

This worked example shows a property-risk turn that stops cleanly at owner and professional judgment.

## Input Context

Risk signal:

- a seasonal walk identifies runoff cutting closer to the driveway edge
- last year there was minor washout after heavy rain
- no immediate emergency exists, but proof of current condition is thin

## Orchestration Steps Performed Inside The Turn

1. Classify the issue as a rising access and erosion risk rather than a completed diagnosis.
2. Prepare a risk-ranked recommendation with immediate documentation needs.
3. Separate what can be done now from what requires contractor or professional review.
4. Package a monitor-plus-escalate recommendation instead of improvising a property fix.
5. Stop before any spending, access, or contractor commitment is made.

## Produced Artifacts Or Decisions

- risk note
- monitor-plus-escalate recommendation
- proof checklist for photos and runoff observations
- owner-ready next-step packet

## Approval Stop Point

Owner or professional review remains required for:

- contractor engagement
- spending
- structural or drainage decisions
- any insurance-sensitive claim or remediation choice

## Why This Counts As A Small Workflow Turn

The turn did not only note a risk. It ranked the issue, planned evidence capture, packaged the next decision, and preserved the authority boundary around physical action.
