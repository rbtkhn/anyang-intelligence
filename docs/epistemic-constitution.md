# The Epistemic Constitution: Warrant Travels Downstream, Evidence Does Not Travel Back

Title rationale: The constitution names the directional law that lets dependent work feel a claim change without turning its own repetition into evidence.

## Lead Judgment

Anyang Intelligence must preserve how consequential claims acquire, lose, and recover warrant. Every semantic transition is human-authorized, append-only, attributable to a cause, and propagated as a downstream review obligation rather than an automatic conclusion.

## Controlling Object

Whether the system can reconstruct a claim's present warrant, transition history, upstream support, downstream dependencies, and next responsible review without confusing evidence, interpretation, confidence, authorization, or presentation.

## Constitutional Law

Every consequential claim carries its epistemic state, provenance, scope, dependencies, and transition history into each downstream use; no downstream repetition upgrades that state.

## State Vocabulary

| State | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| `attributed` | A bounded claim has been recorded as an identified source's assertion. |
| `interpreted` | An operator has stated a bounded analytical reading without claiming verification. |
| `contested` | Material counterevidence, source conflict, or a credible alternative requires review. |
| `supported` | Accountable upstream evidence supports the bounded claim for its declared scope. |
| `disconfirmed` | Accountable evidence defeats the bounded claim for its declared scope. |
| `unresolved` | Available authority or evidence does not permit a responsible verdict. |
| `adopted` | A named human authority has approved the claim for a bounded judgment or use. |
| `retired` | The claim is no longer eligible for current use; its history remains available. |

Permitted transitions are declared in the operating service and tested as an explicit state machine. `retired` is terminal. A transition requires a cause type, cause reference, actor, rationale, timestamp, previous-transition hash, and transition hash.

## Six Changes Remain Distinct

Never collapse these into one update:

1. the world changed;
2. available evidence changed;
3. interpretation changed;
4. the confidence boundary changed;
5. authorization changed;
6. presentation changed.

Operational `claim.status`, evidence classification, evidence strength, epistemic state, approval receipts, and published presentation therefore remain separate fields or records.

## Directional Dependency Law

- Dependencies point from an upstream claim to a downstream claim, work item, artifact, forecast, or publication.
- An upstream transition creates an impact record for each active dependency.
- Impact records may require review, conditional language, unresolved posture, stale marking, or no action.
- Propagation never changes a downstream conclusion silently.
- A downstream artifact, citation, summary, or repetition is never new support for its upstream claim.
- Unknown or dependent source origins cannot be counted as independent corroboration.

## Human Authority and Resolution

Only a named human operator may authorize a semantic claim transition, acknowledge an impact, or resolve an impact with a rationale. Automation may validate structure, calculate hashes, enumerate dependencies, create deterministic impact queues, and block objectively unsafe use. It may not decide that evidence proves a claim.

## Legacy Migration

Pre-v4 claims migrate to `unresolved` with a system-authored `legacy-migration` transition. Existing operational status remains unchanged. Migration does not reconstruct missing epistemic history or imply that earlier use was unsupported.

## Entropy and Control Budget

Operational epistemic entropy is a governance metric, not a Shannon-information claim. The repository reports missing claim identity, provenance, scope, dependencies, review triggers, transition lineage, source independence, approval bindings, synthetic corroboration, and open critical impacts. Human retrieval and revision-impact measurements remain separate and must be observed rather than inferred from validator success.
