# Membranes

Membranes are the filter-judgment layer between work lanes.

Anyang Intelligence should learn across projects, but not everything should be shared. A useful Executive OS transfers operating wisdom while protecting project-specific facts, authority boundaries, sensitive information, and domain obligations.

## Core Principle

Transfer primitives, not private context.

A good membrane allows lessons to pass while blocking inappropriate leakage.

```text
customer fact
  -> membrane judgment
  -> reusable primitive, checklist, question, pattern, or guardrail
```

## What Should Cross Lanes

These can usually transfer after translation:

- Operating primitives.
- Loop structures.
- Review questions.
- Quality gates.
- Evidence standards.
- Risk patterns.
- Governance checklists.
- Capacity rules.
- Cadence lessons.
- Template improvements.
- Skill improvements.

Example:

- Grace Gems teaches "high-value external claims need owner approval."
- The transferable primitive is not Grace Gems' exact product facts.
- The transferable primitive is claim discipline: external claims need source evidence and approval.

## What Should Stay Local

These should not cross lanes unless explicitly approved and appropriately transformed:

- Customer identities when not already public or intentionally named.
- Private financial details.
- Customer support transcripts.
- Child or family information.
- Donor identities or donor restrictions.
- Board deliberations.
- Contractor personal information.
- Legal, tax, payroll, or compliance details.
- Proprietary strategy, pricing, margins, or performance data.
- Draft claims not approved for external use.
- Sensitive property, access, security, or insurance details.

Local facts may inspire better shared patterns, but they should not be copied into other lanes as facts.

## Membrane Tests

Before moving knowledge between lanes, ask:

- **Source test:** Where did this knowledge come from?
- **Consent test:** Was it approved for this wider use?
- **Specificity test:** Is this a customer fact or a reusable primitive?
- **Sensitivity test:** Does it involve money, children, legal status, health, property, donors, customer messages, or private operations?
- **Authority test:** Who has authority over this lane?
- **Evidence test:** Is the lesson supported by evidence or just an impression?
- **Translation test:** Can the lesson be rewritten as a generic checklist, question, or guardrail?
- **Harm test:** Could sharing this distort another lane or create a false assumption?

If any answer is uncertain, hold the transfer and ask for approval or professional review.

## Crossing Rules

Use these rules when a lesson wants to move:

- Customer facts do not cross by default.
- Public facts can cross only with source context and without exaggeration.
- Sensitive facts require explicit approval before reuse.
- Money facts route through tax-financial governance before becoming shared financial guidance.
- Child/family facts stay inside the Learning Core lane unless anonymized into safety principles.
- Donor and nonprofit facts preserve restrictions, governance, and board authority.
- Property facts preserve owner authority, safety implications, and insurance/legal sensitivity.
- Creative lessons can cross freely only after removing client-specific claims, assets, and private context.
- Templates and skills may absorb patterns, but should not embed private customer facts.
- The strictest applicable membrane wins.

## Membrane Types

### Privacy Membrane

Protects personal, family, customer, donor, contractor, and sensitive operational information.

Default action: anonymize, aggregate, or keep local.

### Authority Membrane

Protects human decision rights.

Default action: convert the lesson into a question, recommendation, or approval checklist rather than an instruction to act.

### Evidence Membrane

Protects the system from turning impressions into doctrine.

Default action: require source notes, receipts, transcripts, approvals, metrics, or artifacts before generalizing.

### Domain Membrane

Protects one lane from inappropriate assumptions imported from another lane.

Default action: translate the lesson into the new domain and check whether the governance strictness changes.

### Financial/Legal Membrane

Protects tax, payroll, accounting, nonprofit, legal, investment, real estate, and compliance-sensitive areas.

Default action: preserve evidence and prepare professional-review questions.

### Tone Membrane

Protects customer-facing, employee-facing, community-facing, and internal-only language boundaries.

Default action: rewrite for the receiving audience and remove unnecessary Anyang Intelligence framing when the document belongs to a customer.

## Membrane Output

When a cross-lane transfer is proposed, the Executive OS should produce:

```text
Source lane:
<where the lesson came from>

Receiving lane:
<where the lesson may be used>

Transfer candidate:
<fact, pattern, template, checklist, or question>

Membrane classification:
<safe to transfer | translate first | approval required | professional review required | keep local>

Reason:
<why this classification applies>

Safe transformed version:
<generic primitive, anonymized lesson, checklist, or review question>

Required approval:
<none | owner | parent/guardian | board | CPA/bookkeeper | attorney | customer | contractor | other>
```

## Failure Modes

Watch for:

- **Context leak:** a private fact appears in the wrong lane.
- **Overgeneralization:** a pattern from one lane is treated as universally true.
- **Authority leak:** a recommendation becomes an action without approval.
- **Tone leak:** internal strategy language appears in customer-facing or employee-facing docs.
- **Governance leak:** tax, legal, child-safety, nonprofit, or property decisions bypass review.
- **Identity leak:** a person, donor, contractor, child, customer, or family becomes identifiable when not needed.
- **Commercial leak:** private pricing, margin, strategy, or performance assumptions become shared claims.

## Product Implication

Membranes should become a first-class product primitive alongside loops.

Loops move work forward. Membranes decide what may move across boundaries.

Every mature installation should define:

- Its local memory boundary.
- Its shareable primitives.
- Its sensitive facts.
- Its approval authorities.
- Its evidence requirements.
- Its cross-lane transfer rules.

The Executive OS becomes more intelligent by learning across lanes, but it becomes trustworthy by filtering what it learns.
