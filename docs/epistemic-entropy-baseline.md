# Epistemic Entropy Baseline: Structural Bindings Begin at Maximum Burden

Title rationale: The baseline records the honest starting condition: governed surfaces existed, but no repo-wide claim-state and dependency manifest bound them together.

## Lead Judgment

Before the v1 epistemic manifest, the twelve fixed outcome surfaces had no common claim identity, state, dependency, transition-history, impact, or approval-binding contract. For the new structural metric, that absence is the maximum applicable baseline burden: `100.0`.

## Controlling Object

Whether the same fixed cohort becomes easier to trace and revise without claiming human outcome improvement from structural validation alone.

## Baseline

- Recorded: `2026-07-15`
- Cohort: the twelve IDs in `epistemic-state.yaml`
- Structural entropy rate: `100.0`
- Structural points: `186 / 186` applicable points
- Human retrieval success: `pending`
- Human revision-impact accuracy: `pending`
- Composite entropy: `pending`
- Critical-gap baseline: not asserted retrospectively; the new validator begins counting from its first run.

## Measurement Rule

The structural rate is calculated from applicable missing bindings. Human burden is `100 - (60 × retrieval success + 40 × revision-impact accuracy)`. Composite entropy is `80% structural + 20% human burden`. Relative reduction is reported only against a recorded comparable baseline.
